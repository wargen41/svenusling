  • Cast member [Simon Callow][/p?id=1883] originally portrayed the part of Mozart in the 1979 stage production.
  • [Mel Gibson][/p?id=1635], [Mick Jagger][/p?id=Mick%20Jagger], [Sam Waterston][/p?id=Sam%20Waterston], and [Tim Curry][/p?id=1938] all auditioned for the role of Mozart.
  • The music was pre-recorded and played in the background as scenes were filmed. [Tom Hulce][/p?id=1951] practiced four hours a day at the piano to appear convincing.
  • [Tim Curry][/p?id=1938] auditioned for the role of Mozart (and played Mozart on Broadway).
  • [Mark Hamill][/p?id=1609] also played the role of Mozart on Broadway and lobbied heavily for the role in the film.
  • Originally, a very young [Kenneth Branagh][/p?id=1540] was cast as Mozart, but [Milos Forman][/p?id=Milos%20Forman] changed his mind and decided to go with American actors for the principal roles.
  • Several professors of music stated, after studying all of the musical keys struck on pianos throughout the film, that not one key is struck incorrectly when compared to what is heard at the exact same moment. In other words, what you see is exactly what you hear.
  • [Elizabeth Berridge][/p?id=Elizabeth%20Berridge], during the Nipples of Venus scene, did not know she could spit out the candy (which was really lumps of marzipan) between takes and ate about 15 whole pieces. She later describes how she thought that they were disgusting and that she eventually made herself sick.
  • According to [Milos Forman's][/p?id=Milos%20Forman] autobiography, one studio offered to provide funding for [Amadeus][/f?id=10277] (1984) on the one condition that [Forman][/p?id=Milos%20Forman] cast [Walter Matthau][/p?id=1987] (a reported Mozart enthusiast) for the role of Mozart. [Forman][/p?id=Milos%20Forman] refused the offer, considering [Matthau][/p?id=1987] to be too old for the role.
  • When shooting the scene in which Salieri is writing down death mass under Mozart dictation, [Tom Hulce][/p?id=1951] was deliberately skipping lines to confuse [F. Murray Abraham][/p?id=1293], in order to achieve the impression that Salieri wasn't able to fully understand the music he was dictated.
  • [Meg Tilly][/p?id=Meg%20Tilly] originally was cast as Stanze but tore a leg ligament in a street soccer game the day before she was to film her first scene. [Elizabeth Berridge][/p?id=Elizabeth%20Berridge] replaced her.
  • One of only 4 productions to win both the Best Play Tony (1981) and the Best Picture Oscar (1984). The other 3 are My Fair lady (1957/1964), The Sound of Music (1960/1965) and A Man For All Seasons (1962/1966).
