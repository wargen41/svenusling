Visste du?

  • [Anthony Perkins][/p?id=1055] was ordained as a priest as part of his research for his role.
  • According to [Kathleen Turner][/p?id=Kathleen%20Turner], [Anthony Perkins][/p?id=1055] would sniff a form of nitrate before each take.
  • [Alec Baldwin][/p?id=1017] and [Patrick Swayze][/p?id=1718] auditioned for the role of Bobby Grady which in the end was cast with [John Laughlin][/p?id=John%20Laughlin].
  • Composer [Rick Wakeman's][/p?id=Rick%20Wakeman] soundtrack is based entirely on Antonín Dvorák's "From the New World" Symphony. This was partly an in-joke on the releasing company who were similarly named New World Pictures, and also partially as the film's director [Ken Russell's][/p?id=Ken%20Russell] "take" on America.
  • [Kathleen Turner][/p?id=Kathleen%20Turner] had recently been rejected for the lead in [Nine 1/2 Weeks][/f?id=9%201%2F2%20Vecka] (1986) when she signed on to do this picture in March 1984. ([9½ Weeks][/f?id=9%201%2F2%20Vecka] was made before Crimes of Passion, despite being released almost 2 years later.)
  • In the original screenplay, Peter Shayne is a shoe salesman. At the suggestion of [Anthony Perkins][/p?id=1055], writer Barry Sandler changed the role to a minister to better satirize American sexual mores.
  • [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441] was offered the role of Bobby Grady, but was not cast over salary disputes. The part was cast with [John Laughlin][/p?id=John%20Laughlin].
  • [Kathleen Turner][/p?id=Kathleen%20Turner] was initially hesitant about [Ken Russell's][/p?id=Ken%20Russell] suggestion that she smear yogurt over her lips to simulate semen. She complied with his request but drew the line at gulping down an oyster to emulate swallowing ejaculate.
  • According to film critic Roger Ebert, the picture "required massive cuts in the movie before it could qualify for an R rating" in the USA with studio distributor New World Pictures reluctant to release the film stateside with an X rating.
