  • [Tom Savini][/p?id=Tom%20Savini] turned down [Friday the 13th Part 2][/f?id=14051] (1981) to work on this.
  • [Tom Savini][/p?id=Tom%20Savini] was not particularly happy with Cropsy's burn make-up as he was only given three days to work on it.
  • Feature film debut of Jason [Alexander][/f?id=10123], [Fisher Stevens][/p?id=1296], and [Holly Hunter][/p?id=1375].
  • The film's composer, Rick Wakeman, was originally offered a percentage of whatever profits the film made but he had decided to opt for a fee instead as he felt the film had no chance of being successful. The film ended up being the biggest grossing horror movie in Japan.
  • To create Cropsy's distorted POV shots, the cinematographer rubbed Vaseline on the outside edges of the camera lens.
  • This is the film that launched the careers of acclaimed producers [Harvey Weinstein][/p?id=Harvey%20Weinstein] and Bob [Weinstein][/p?id=Harvey%20Weinstein]. Their mother Miriam [Weinstein][/p?id=Harvey%20Weinstein] is also a pre-production assistant on the film.
  • This film was one of the first movies to land on the UK's Video Nasties list, specifically because of the infamous raft massacre.
  • The wardrobe of the film is mostly the actual wardrobe of the cast. There was rarely a costume-person on set.
  • Director [Tony Maylam][/p?id=Tony%20Maylam] said there was much more footage shot of the Cropsy killer for the film but he decided to cut back coverage on the killer to keep Cropsy more frightening on screen.
  • Director [Tony Maylam][/p?id=Tony%20Maylam] said there was so much dust in the copper mine where the film's finale was shot that he was still coughing it up several weeks after completing the shoot.
  • Actors had trouble holding up the garden sheers to the liking of director [Tony Maylam][/p?id=Tony%20Maylam], so in a lot of the scenes in which costumed Cropsy kills (most notably the infamous raft scene), he played the killer himself.
  • [Tom Savini][/p?id=Tom%20Savini] would play classical music during the long process of creating plaster castings of the actors faces.
  • According to director [Tony Maylam][/p?id=Tony%20Maylam] the reason the film's finale didn't take place in a cave, as it was originally supposed to, was due to the fact that the cave where it was going to be shot was full of bats. [Tom Savini][/p?id=Tom%20Savini] also said another cave that was scouted for the film collapsed just after it had been surveyed.
  • It was director [Tony Maylam][/p?id=Tony%20Maylam] who chose garden shears as Cropsy's weapon of choice.
  • In an interview for Total Film magazine, [Holly Hunter][/p?id=1375] had the following to say about her screen debut: "- Well, look, I got paid more than I ever could've imagine on The Burning; I was making over a thousand dollars a week, which was incredible. I could make my rent, I didn't have to wait tables for a while, I got all these new friends and I was kind of a glorified extra. I got my Screen Actors Guild card. So it was fantastic."
  • Debut feature film produced by [Harvey Weinstein][/p?id=Harvey%20Weinstein].
  • Many people also claim that there was an early appearance by [Laurence Fishburne][/p?id=1567], as the hospital orderly whose arm is grabbed by Cropsy's horrifically burnt hand just before the opening title sequence. This actor was actually [Mansoor Najee-ullah][/p?id=Mansoor%20Najee-ullah].
