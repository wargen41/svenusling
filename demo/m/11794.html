  • [Burgess Meredith][/p?id=1119] plays a man who competes for the affections of Ellen Miller, played by [Paulette Goddard][/p?id=Paulette%20Goddard]. This foreshadowed [Meredith][/p?id=1119] and [Goddard's][/p?id=Paulette%20Goddard] real-life marriage in 1944.
  • [Fred Astaire][/p?id=Fred%20Astaire] would later refer to this as the worst film he ever made. Artie Shaw confided that it put him off film acting.
  • While [Fred Astaire][/p?id=Fred%20Astaire] was making this film, [Ginger Rogers][/p?id=Ginger%20Rogers] was making Kitty Foyle (1940). This went on to win her the Academy Award for Best Actress. When she won, [Astaire][/p?id=Fred%20Astaire] sent her a telegram simply reading "Ouch".
  • The Oscar-nominated song "Would You Like to Be the Love of My Life" was written one afternoon over lunch. Artie Shaw knew they had a big hit with the song and was eager to share it with the studio, but co-writer Johnny Mercer persuaded him to sit on it for a couple of weeks, lest the studio started thinking they weren't working too hard.
  • [Paulette Goddard][/p?id=Paulette%20Goddard] was in the midst of divorcing Charles [Chaplin][/f?id=11492] during filming. When her divorce came through, she later married her co-star [Burgess Meredith][/p?id=1119].
  • [Fred Astaire][/p?id=Fred%20Astaire] and [Burgess Meredith][/p?id=1119] play college students. This rather stretches credibility as [Astaire][/p?id=Fred%20Astaire] was 41 and [Meredith][/p?id=1119] was 33 at the time of filming.
