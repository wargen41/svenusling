Visste du?

  • [Samuel Fuller][/p?id=Samuel%20Fuller] submitted a screenplay that was almost completely different from what was filmed. Among other things, the [Lee Marvin][/p?id=1572] character was not a sheriff, but a KKK leader whose racist viewpoint is completely changed around. However, Paramount got nervous about the provocative nature of the screenplay and ordered it rewritten, infuriating [Fuller][/p?id=Samuel%20Fuller] (who left the project because of this) and [Marvin][/p?id=1572] (who wanted out but had already signed a contract.)
  • Co-writer [Samuel Fuller][/p?id=Samuel%20Fuller] was originally set to direct, but walked off the project.
  • With a budget of $5,000,000 it was the most expensive film ever produced by an African-American (William Alexander) at that time.
  • [Richard Burton][/p?id=1782] and [Lee Marvin][/p?id=1572] were both drinking heavily during filming. At one point [Burton][/p?id=1782] confided to [Marvin][/p?id=1572] that he would like to play a cop in a film set in New York, but couldn't trust himself to do a convincing American accent. (His accent in this film was widely ridiculed, particularly since it wavered throughout the movie). [Marvin][/p?id=1572] replied, "But Richard, you're a great actor. You can learn." [Burton][/p?id=1782] apparently treasured the compliment for the rest of his life.
  • Shortly after filming had ended, [Richard Burton][/p?id=1782] was hospitalized at an American clinic to be treated for his alcoholism.
