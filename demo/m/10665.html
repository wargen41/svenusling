  • Pre-production began in 1936 and was intended to be Walt Disney's second full-length animated film after [Snow White and the Seven Dwarfs][/f?id=18613] (1937). Disney's perfection and quest for realism delayed the project significantly, so that [Pinocchio][/f?id=Pinocchio] (1940), [Fantasia][/f?id=Fantasia] (1940), [The Reluctant Dragon][/f?id=Den%20Fredliga%20Draken] (1941) and [Dumbo][/f?id=Dumbo] (1941) were released earlier than Bambi.
  • A test animation of baby Bambi stuck on a fallen tree-trunk was sufficiently charming to convince Walt Disney to make the film.
  • After [Dumbo][/f?id=Dumbo] (1941), this is the second Walt Disney animated feature to be set in the present day.
  • Two asteroids have been named after Bambi and Thumper.
  • Unusually for the time, Walt Disney insisted on children providing the voices for the animals when they were young, instead of using adults mimicking youngsters.
  • The movie lost money at the box office for the first run, but began to recoup its considerable cost (over $2,000,000) during the 1947 re-release.
