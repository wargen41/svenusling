  • To maintain a sense of realism, [John Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] never warned the actors about when shots would be fired. Their reactions are real.
  • [John Singleton's][/p?id=John%20Singleton] Oscar nomination for Best Director at the age of 24 made him the youngest director to ever receive such an accolade, beating [Orson Welles][/p?id=1711] by a good two years.
  • In terms of investment, this was 1991's most financially successful film, making $56.1 million from a budget of only $6.5 million.
  • [Ice Cube][/p?id=1390] was [John Singleton's][/p?id=John%20Singleton] first choice for Doughboy. He approached him at a rap music concert a couple of times, and [Cube][/p?id=1390] was reluctant to audition via a screen test, conducted by [Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] himself. [Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] later encouraged [Cube][/p?id=1390] to write screenplays (based on [Cube's][/p?id=1390] contribution to rap lyrics) - which resulted in the successful 'Friday' film series.
  • Columbia Pictures wanted to make the picture but wanted someone other than [John Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] to direct it. [Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] believed only he could do it, replying, "Hell, no, I'm not gonna let somebody from Idaho or Encino direct a movie about living in south-central Los Angeles. They can't come in here and cast it and go through the rewrites and know exactly what aesthetics are unique to this film."
  • The opening and closing scenes are direct references to [Rob Reiner's][/p?id=1801] movie [Stand By Me][/f?id=18853] (1986).
  • [John Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] based Tre's childhood on his own. [Singleton's][/p?id=John%20Singleton] father was a mortgage broker like [Laurence Fishburne's][/p?id=1567] character. When he was 12, [Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] moved in with his father in South Los Angeles. Like Tre, [Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] stayed out of trouble with his father's guidance and went to college.
  • Film debuts of [Regina King][/p?id=Regina%20King] and [Ice Cube][/p?id=1390].
  • [Laurence Fishburne][/p?id=1567], who plays [Cuba Gooding Jr.'s][/p?id=1193] father, is only six and a half years older than him in real life.
  • [Eddie Murphy][/p?id=1261] was considered for the role of Jason 'Furious' Styles.
  • Part of [John Singleton's][/p?id=John%20Singleton] inspiration to making this film came when an executive from Orion Pictures came to talk to his class at USC's film school. [Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] told the executives that he didn't like that the movie "Colors" wasn't about [South Central][/f?id=18639] Los Angeles at all but was about "two white cops". When the exec defensively said that Ice-T wrote the music for the film, [Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] said to a lot of applause "Well, Ice-T didn't write the fucking script!" [Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] also cited "[New Jack City][/f?id=16987]" as a film that he didn't like and wanted to make this film as different from as possible.
  • The film was shot in sequence. [John Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] noted that the camera work gets better as the film goes on and he finds his foothold as a director, saying that he was getting better at camera work which he wrote into the script for when Furious is cutting Tre's hair, he tells him "I'm not getting old, I'm getting better."
  • When [John Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] was applying for film school one of the questions on the application was to describe "three ideas for films". One of [Singleton's][/p?id=John%20Singleton] ideas, titled "Summer of 84", would later evolve into this movie. [Singleton][/p?id=John%20Singleton] was protective of his script, insisting that he be the one to direct the project and explaining at a retrospective screening of the film, "I wasn't going to have somebody from Idaho or Encino direct my movie."
