  • According to TV Guide, "In October 1972 an account written by Leonard Greenwood appeared in the Los Angeles Times. It told of a Peruvian engineer whose son had been kidnapped by a band of Indians and of the man's successful search to locate the child. Screenwriter Rospo Pallenberg saw the news item and took it to producer-director [John Boorman][/p?id=John%20Boorman]". The result was this movie.
  • According to director [John Boorman's][/p?id=John%20Boorman] book "Money Into Light - The Emerald Forest: A Diary" (1985), [Boorman's][/p?id=John%20Boorman] initial choice for the part of the son Tomme was [C. Thomas Howell][/p?id=1123][Howell][/p?id=.%20When%20C.%20Thomas%20Howell] was unavailable, John decided to use his own son [Charley Boorman][/p?id=Charley%20Boorman] for the part.
  • Due to the fact that many of the native South American actors wear very little and many of their women appear topless in the picture, reportedly, filming each day started with the non-South American cast and crew personnel performing naked aerobic exercising.
  • [Powers Boothe][/p?id=Powers%20Boothe] almost drowned during the shooting of one sequence where [Charley Boorman][/p?id=Charley%20Boorman] was assisting [Boothe][/p?id=Powers%20Boothe] cross a river. [Boorman's][/p?id=John%20Boorman] pleas for assistance were initially interpreted by distant crew personnel as being part of his performance.
  • The film was "released with native language dialogue" and "became the first feature ever made for U.S. markets in a principal language other than English" according to the book "Picture This!: A Guide to Over 300 Environmentally, Socially, and Politically Relevant Films and Videos" (1992) by Sky Hiatt.
  • The picture was screened out of competition at the Cannes Film Festival in 1985.
