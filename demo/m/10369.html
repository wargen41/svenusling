Visste du?

  • Jaguars, unlike leopards, are not generally known to kill humans, although there are a few cases of this happening.
  • For the waterfall jump scene, [Rudy Youngblood][/p?id=Rudy%20Youngblood] performed his own stunt, jumping in a harness from a 15-story building in Veracruz over about 10 takes. (The shot was later digitally composited with the real waterfall.) After director [Mel Gibson][/p?id=1635] harassed [Youngblood][/p?id=Rudy%20Youngblood] about the actor's initial hesitancy and fear of jumping, [Youngblood][/p?id=Rudy%20Youngblood] got together with the stunt crew and goaded [Gibson][/p?id=1635] into taking a jump for himself.
  • In casting, it was important to [Mel Gibson][/p?id=1635] that he and Farhad Safinia find actors that matched the archetype each character represented. For instance, [Rudy Youngblood][/p?id=Rudy%20Youngblood] struck [Gibson][/p?id=1635] as fitting the mythic archetype of a hero. [Gibson][/p?id=1635] saw that as necessary to allow people to identify with the film, since the movie's context is unfamiliar to most viewers, being in a foreign language and concerning an indigenous culture in the 16th century.
  • [Mel Gibson][/p?id=1635] turned down the lead role in [World Trade Center][/f?id=World%20Trade%20Center] (2006) in order to direct this film.
  • The Jaguar's attack in the film is portrayed accurately, with the cat attacking the skull, rather than the throat. Unique among the other big cats, as where Lions, Cheetahs, Tigers, and Leopards asphyxiate their prey with a bite to the throat, Jaguars kill with a penetrating bite to the brain.
