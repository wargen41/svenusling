  • After this movie was released restaurants were suddenly bombarded with requests for pink champagne.
  • This was [Irene Dunne's][/p?id=1395] favorite among her films. This also happened to be [Charles Boyer's][/p?id=1143] favorite among his films as well.
  • In 1957, director [Leo McCarey][/p?id=1579] remade the movie as [An Affair to Remember][/f?id=10261] (1957), starring [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] and [Deborah Kerr][/p?id=1234]. ([Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] and [Irene Dunne][/p?id=1395] had previously co-starred in [The Awful Truth][/f?id=Min%20Fru%20Har%20En%20F%C3%A4stman] (1937), which was also directed by [McCarey][/p?id=1579].) The movie was remade a second time as [Love Affair (1994)][/f?id=Love%20Affair%20(1994)], starring [Warren Beatty][/p?id=1991], [Annette Bening][/p?id=1050], and [Katharine Hepburn][/p?id=1531], directed by [Glenn Gordon Caron][/p?id=Glenn%20Gordon%20Caron].
  • [Charles Boyer][/p?id=1143] and [Irene Dunne][/p?id=1395] were paired again as a romantic couple in [When Tomorrow Comes][/f?id=Morgondagen%20%C3%84r%20V%C3%A5r] (1939), made the same year as this film. Both films feature a scene where [Dunne][/p?id=1395] sings while [Boyer][/p?id=1143] plays the piano. Their third and final film together was [Together Again][/f?id=Blixt%20K%C3%A4r] (1944).
  • [Astrid Allwyn][/p?id=Astrid%20Allwyn], who plays [Boyer's][/p?id=1143] heiress fiancé, also appeared in the unrelated film [Love Affair (1932)][/f?id=Love%20Affair%20(1932)], starring a young and relatively-unknown [Humphrey Bogart][/p?id=1383].
