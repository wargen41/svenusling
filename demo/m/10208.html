Visste du?

  • Animator [Chuck Jones][/p?id=Chuck%20Jones] based the Warner Brothers cartoon character "Pepe le Pew" on the "Pepe le Moko" character played by [Charles Boyer][/p?id=1143] in this film.
  • Remake of the 1937 French film [Pépé le Moko][/f?id=P%C3%A9p%C3%A9%20Fr%C3%A5n%20Marseille] (1937). When Walter Wanger produced this film, he tried to have all copies of the original "Pépé le Moko" destroyed. Fortunately, he was unsuccessful.
  • The allure of [Hedy Lamarr][/p?id=Hedy%20Lamarr], as well as the exotic location of the Casbah, was said to be two of the chief inspirations for [Casablanca][/f?id=11455] (1942). However, MGM refused to release [Lamarr][/p?id=Hedy%20Lamarr] from her contract for the Warner Brothers film.
  • When Julius Epstein, one of several screenwriters for [Casablanca][/f?id=11455] (1942), was trying to "pitch" it to David O. Selznick, from whom they wanted to borrow their preferred female star, [Ingrid Bergman][/p?id=1392]. Epstein started in on a long, drawn-out summary but finally wrapped up with, "Oh, what the hell! It's going to be a lot of shit like '[Algiers'][/f?id=10208]!"
  • The title for this film was the inspiration for [Casablanca][/f?id=11455] (1942).
  • The cast and credits are based on the 98-minute print shown on Turner Classic Movies, but the AFI Catalogue lists slightly different changes which suggest that their print may have been a re-release. In the AFI Catalogue listing, [Sigrid Gurie's][/p?id=Sigrid%20Gurie] name is above the title with the rest of the cast list the same. The crew credits are identical, except that [James Wong][/p?id=James%20Wong] Howe is credited for "photography" instead of "director of photography." The latter terminology was rare in 1938, but not unheard of.
  • The main theme and the closing credits music are identical to those heard in [Pépé le Moko][/f?id=P%C3%A9p%C3%A9%20Fr%C3%A5n%20Marseille] (1937), of which this film is an extremely faithful remake, except for the way the story ends.
  • Producer Walter Wagner wanted either Dolores del Rio or [Sylvia Sidney][/p?id=Sylvia%20Sidney] for the role of Gaby. [Sidney][/p?id=Sylvia%20Sidney] was also considered for the role of Ines, but she felt that she had played that type of character many times before and turned it down.
  • Features [Gene Lockhart's][/p?id=1319] only Oscar nominated performance.
