Visste du?

  • The original script specified [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] and [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117].
  • [Arthur Hiller][/p?id=Arthur%20Hiller] removed his name from the credits after the film's production company, Cinergi Pictures, preferred the cut made by producer/writer [Joe Eszterhas][/p?id=Joe%20Eszterhas] over [Arthur Hiller's][/p?id=Arthur%20Hiller] cut.
  • [Joe Eszterhas][/p?id=Joe%20Eszterhas] announced through the media during post-production that Cinergi Pictures didn't have the money to pay for soundtrack. He said that he would finance the soundtrack from his own money and asked to submit tracks for it to him. He received 9,200 CDs and cassettes from mostly unknown and unsigned artists, listened to all that was sent (at least a couple of tracks from each record) and compiled the soundtrack.
  • '[Alan Smithee'][/p?id=Alan%20Smithee] exclaims that the film is "worse than [Showgirls][/f?id=18325] (1995)", also written by [Joe Eszterhas][/p?id=Joe%20Eszterhas].
  • After [Arthur Hiller][/p?id=Arthur%20Hiller] had his credit changed to [Alan Smithee][/p?id=Alan%20Smithee], the Director's Guild of America unregistered the name [Alan Smithee][/p?id=Alan%20Smithee], therefore this film is the last film to ever bear that pseudonym.
