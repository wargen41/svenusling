Visste du?

  • Writer/director [Joe Cornish][/p?id=Joe%20Cornish] interviewed various kids in youth groups in order to find out what kind of weapons they would use if an alien invasion occurred.
  • Writer/director [Joe Cornish][/p?id=Joe%20Cornish] was inspired to make this film after actually being mugged in real-life one night (much in the same way Sam was as portrayed in the film). He noticed his five young assailants were as scared as he was, and started researching their lives.
  • [Joe Cornish][/p?id=Joe%20Cornish] based the character of Brewis on himself when he was in his 20s.
  • [John Boyega][/p?id=John%20Boyega] found out about this film from an ad placed on-line.
  • In this movie, [Nick Frost][/p?id=Nick%20Frost] still wears the long hair he grew for Paul (2011).
  • Writer/director [Joe Cornish][/p?id=Joe%20Cornish] has stated that watching Signs (2002) and imagining what would happen if it took place in south London was an inspiration for [Attack][/f?id=10495] the Block.
  • When doing research for the film, [Joe Cornish][/p?id=Joe%20Cornish] asked one of the girls, "What would you think of this creature if you found it?" The girl said, "I wouldn't touch it, don't want to get chlamydia." That quote went straight into script; many lines were taken directly from research.
  • Writer/director [Joe Cornish][/p?id=Joe%20Cornish] did in depth research on language to accurately convey the way South London street kids speak.
  • [Joe Cornish][/p?id=Joe%20Cornish] was "very keen" that only Hi-Hatz used guns. "He's the genuine villain, the only villain. He's the only guy who shoots anyone. If I put guns in hands of other characters, it would become a whole different film."
  • [John Boyega's][/p?id=John%20Boyega] film debut.
  • On filming a scene with five boys and five girls-all with dialogue- [Joe Cornish][/p?id=Joe%20Cornish] watched a lot of Larry Clark films to figure out how Clark filmed scenes of teenagers chatting in such a naturalistic style.
