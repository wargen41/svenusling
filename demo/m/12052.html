Visste du?

  • The famous scene in the Trevi Fountain was shot in March, when nights were still cold. According to [Federico Fellini][/p?id=Federico%20Fellini] (in an interview with Costanzo Costantini), [Anita Ekberg][/p?id=Anita%20Ekberg] stood in the cold water in her dress for hours without any trouble. [Marcello Mastroianni][/p?id=Marcello%20Mastroianni], on the other hand, had to wear a wetsuit beneath his clothes, and even that wasn't enough. Still freezing, he downed an entire bottle of vodka, so that he was completely drunk while shooting the scene.
  • The film contributed the term "paparazzo" to the language. The term derives from Marcello's photographer friend Paparazzo. [Federico Fellini][/p?id=Federico%20Fellini] took the name "Paparazzo", as he explained in a later interview, from the name of someone he met in Calabria (Southern Italy) where Greek names are still common. "Paparazzi" is the plural meaning.
  • When shooting the famous Fontana di Trevi scene, director [Federico Fellini][/p?id=Federico%20Fellini] complained that the water in the fountain looked dirty. A representative of Scandinavian Airlines System (SAS) present at the shooting was able to supply the film team with some of the airline's green sea dye marker (for use in case of an emergency landing at sea). This was used to color the water, and the director was satisfied.
  • This was late film critic Roger Ebert's favorite movie. According to him, he saw it over 25 times.
  • After [Federico Fellini][/p?id=Federico%20Fellini] rejected the idea of [Paul Newman][/p?id=1727] for the lead role, Dino De Laurentiis suggested Gérard Philipe. He thought [Marcello Mastroianni][/p?id=Marcello%20Mastroianni] was "too soft and goody-goody; a family man rather than the type who flings women onto the bed."
