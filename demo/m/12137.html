Visste du?

  • The General is from "Valverde", the fictitious Latin-American country used in [Commando][/f?id=11647] (1985). (The first [Die Hard][/f?id=12136] (1988) was originally supposed to be the sequel to that movie).
  • In the original [Die Hard][/f?id=12136] (1988), John McClane only had a few scripted one-liners. However, '[Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] (I)' ad-libbed so many one liners and audiences liked them so much that in this sequel (and the next one), more gags were added and [Willis][/p?id=1117] was told he could ad-lib as many more as he saw fit.
  • It was [Renny Harlin's][/p?id=1777] idea that [William Sadler][/p?id=2007], the film's main villain be introduced naked doing martial arts exercises during the film's opening sequence. He would later say that it was "an effective, but unusual way to introduce a character".
  • [John McTiernan][/p?id=John%20McTiernan] had planned to direct this film, but could not because of his commitment to directing [The Hunt for Red October][/f?id=15206] (1990).
  • According to [John Leguizamo][/p?id=1491] in his autobiography, his role was intended to be much larger until the filmmakers realized how short he was. His part was cut down to one line which was dubbed by someone else.
  • This film was originally titled [Die Hard 2][/f?id=12137]: [Die Harder][/f?id=12136].
  • This is [Bruce Willis's][/p?id=1117] least favorite film of the "[Die Hard][/f?id=12136]" trilogy.
