  • Director [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] was insistent that he didn't want to use any kind of elaborate special effects or computer trickery when making the movie. He initially hired a standard visual effects team, but they told him that the things he wanted to achieve were impossible without using modern digital technology. Coppola disagreed and fired them, replacing them with his 29 year old son Roman Coppola, who set about achieving some the effects by using old-school cinematic trickery.
  • During preproduction of the movie, director [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] came up with the idea that when in the presence of a being such as a vampire, the laws of physics don't work correctly. This is why shadows often seem to act independently of the figure casting them, why rats can run along a ceiling upside-down and why liquid drips up instead of down.
  • [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] considered at one point of giving the film the title, "D" in order to distinguish it from previous Dracula adaptations.
  • In an attempt to elicit more emotion, director [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] shouted "whore" and "slut" at [Winona Ryder][/p?id=Winona%20Ryder] while filming the scene when [Van Helsing][/f?id=21273] catches Mina with Dracula.
  • [Steve Buscemi][/p?id=1900] was the first choice to play Renfield but turned down.
  • [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] has openly criticized his own reasoning for casting [Keanu Reeves][/p?id=1536] as Jonathan Harker. According to him, he needed a young, hot star that would connect with the girls.
  • At the first "cast meeting" called by [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299], he got all the principal actors to read the entire Bram Stoker novel out loud to get a feel for the story. According to [Anthony Hopkins][/p?id=1053], it took two whole days to complete.
  • Among those who auditioned for the part of Dracula were [Andy Garcia][/p?id=Andy%20Garcia] (who had concerns over the number of sex scenes), [Gabriel Byrne][/p?id=Gabriel%20Byrne], [Armand Assante][/p?id=Armand%20Assante], [Antonio Banderas][/p?id=1059] and [Viggo Mortensen][/p?id=1977].
  • [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] had [Richard E. Grant][/p?id=Richard%20E.%20Grant], [Cary Elwes][/p?id=1133] and [Bill Campbell][/p?id=Bill%20Campbell] embark on a series of "adventures" including horse back riding and hot air ballooning to build the camaraderie between the three.
  • Originally, director [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] had wanted to use highly impressionistic sets using only lights and shadows with minimum props. Instead he wanted to spend the entirety of the production design budget on the costumes. The studio however wouldn't allow this, and ordered him to build 'proper' sets.
