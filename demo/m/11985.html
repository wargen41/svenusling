  • [Darryl F. Zanuck][/p?id=Darryl%20F.%20Zanuck] originally offered the role of Marcellus to [Tyrone Power][/p?id=Tyrone%20Power] in a bid to get him to renew his contract with Fox. [Power][/p?id=Tyrone%20Power] instead opted to star in the play "[John Brown's][/p?id=John%20Brown] Body" on Broadway, which closed after 65 performances.
  • Director [Henry Koster][/p?id=Henry%20Koster] chose his Second Assistant Director [Donald C. Klune][/p?id=Donald%20C.%20Klune] to play the role of Jesus in the film. Klute would thus sign all the extras' vouchers and finish the paperwork while still in costume. He also had to eat lunch in his dressing room, as the studio thought it would be inappropriate for "Jesus" to eat in the commissary at Fox.
  • The second movie made in CinemaScope, but the first to be released (Sept. 16, 1953). The first film to be made in CinemaScope was [How to Marry a Millionaire][/f?id=Hur%20Man%20F%C3%A5r%20En%20Miljon%C3%A4r] (1953) and was released Nov. 4, 1953.
  • The movie was advertised as "the modern entertainment miracle you can see without the use of glasses" - a dig at the 3D movies that were en vogue at the time.
  • [Burt Lancaster][/p?id=1120] was originally cast in the role played by [Victor Mature][/p?id=Victor%20Mature].
  • [Richard Burton][/p?id=1782] once said this was the least favorite of all his films.
  • [Richard Burton][/p?id=1782] was once threatened with a gun by [Stewart Granger][/p?id=1908] because of the affair he was having with [Granger's][/p?id=1908] wife [Jean Simmons][/p?id=Jean%20Simmons] during filming.
  • [Richard Burton][/p?id=1782] hated making the film so much that he turned down a studio contract from 20th Century Fox. He was amazed to receive an Oscar nomination after critics had almost universally described his performance as "wooden".
