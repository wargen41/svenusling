  • Despite the fact that [Elvis Presley][/p?id=Elvis%20Presley] is the main character, not one piece of Elvis's music is heard. [Coscarelli][/p?id=Don%20Coscarelli] explained that it would have cost about half the budget to license one of Elvis's songs for the movie.
  • Most of the movie was shot at an abandoned veterans' hospital outside of Los Angeles. Even the trailer park explosion was done there. They used three hallways of one of the buildings for the interior of the rest home.
  • When Elvis turns on the TV and sees the [Elvis Presley][/p?id=Elvis%20Presley] movie marathon, none of the clips are from any of Elvis's movies. Because the licensing costs would have been too much for the budget, they used stock footage and never showed the faces of the Elvis-like actors.
  • [Coscarelli][/p?id=Don%20Coscarelli] originally wanted a dual narration, not just an Elvis narration. He had recorded another voice narrating the action as it came from the short story it was based upon, but this was abandoned after his friends told him it was terrible. Some of this narration can be heard on the DVD's deleted scenes.
  • [Bruce Campbell][/p?id=1112] helped promote the movie by bringing it along with him on his book tour for his autobiography, "If Chins Could Kill: Confessions of a B-Movie Actor."
  • Before [Bruce Campbell][/p?id=1112] accepted the role of "Elvis," he had only one question for director [Don Coscarelli][/p?id=Don%20Coscarelli]: "Are you going to show the penis?"
