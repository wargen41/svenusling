Visste du?

  • This started out as a project re-teaming [Paul Newman][/p?id=1727] and director [George Roy Hill][/p?id=George%20Roy%20Hill] after [Butch Cassidy and the Sundance Kid][/f?id=11386] (1969). Although Hill dropped out of the mix in the intervening years, [Newman][/p?id=1727] remained on board.
  • Although set in Wyoming, the movie was made mostly in Alberta, Canada. The production crew bulldozed a remote field and constructed a full-sized copy of William F. Cody's outdoor theater complex.
  • Features a cast of over 500.
  • As the film came out in 1976, film critics and others felt that this scathing indictment of Americana was made as a countermeasure to the celebratory atmosphere of the American bicentennial, but [Robert Altman][/p?id=1804] claimed in interviews that he did not intend for this to be a commentary on the bicentennial at all.
  • This movie won [Robert Altman][/p?id=1804] the Golden Bear for the best film at the 1976 Berlin Film Festival. [Altman][/p?id=1804] declined the award, however, in protest over the re-cutting of the film at the hands of Executive Producer Dino De Laurentiis.
