Visste du?

  • Director [David Cronenberg][/p?id=David%20Cronenberg] had to re-shoot the scene in which [John Smith][/p?id=John%20Smith] has his first premonition. It showed a little girl's room burning and a small E.T. doll could be seen on one of the shelves. The scene had to be re-shot when Universal Pictures threatened to sue.
  • [Cronenberg][/p?id=David%20Cronenberg] fired a .357 Magnum loaded with blanks just off camera to make [Smith's][/p?id=John%20Smith] flinches seem more involuntary; this was [Christopher Walken's][/p?id=1173] own idea.
  • The "sweat" on [Christopher Walken's][/p?id=1173] face during the "burning bedroom" sequence was in fact a flame-retardant chemical that had been sprayed onto him. The resulting effect, which hadn't been anticipated, looked surprisingly dramatic on film.
  • One of only three [David Cronenberg][/p?id=David%20Cronenberg] films that do not have a score by his friend, composer Howard Shore. This was due to studio politics in which Paramount wanted a more familiar composer to write the music for the film. Michael Kamen, who had written the music for the film [Venom][/f?id=Svarta%20mamban] (1981) for the studio, was chosen instead.
  • During the time Michael Kamen was composing the music for the film in London, he would play the score on the piano in his home. He received several complaints by his neighbors who asked, "Can you please stop playing that music? I can't sleep and it's giving my family nightmares."
  • [Hal Holbrook][/p?id=1352] was [Cronenberg's][/p?id=David%20Cronenberg] original choice to play Sherrif Bannerman, but Dino De Laurentiis rejected this idea as he had never heard of [Holbrook][/p?id=1352] at the time.
