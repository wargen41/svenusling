Visste du?

  • Elwood removes his hat three times in the film: when going to sleep in his room, to break the window to get into the Palace Hotel, and towards the end of the movie when the Bluesmobile falls apart. His sunglasses are never removed. Jake removes his sunglasses once, when he is talking to [Carrie Fisher][/p?id=1131], but never removes his hat. In the DVD and cable versions, Elwood doesn't wear sunglasses when he quits his job.
  • At the end, after the Universal Studios logo is shown, there is an ad for Universal Studios in Hollywood. Below "When in Hollywood, visit Universal Studios", it says "Ask For Babs". The same appeared in [Animal House][/f?id=11920] (1978) (Babs is the Animal House character Babs Jensen), and it reappeared in [Blues Brothers 2000][/f?id=11077] (1998) underneath a new Universal Studios Hollywood logo at the end of that movie.
  • Every time we see the window in Elwood's apartment a train goes past.
  • At time of release, this film held the world record for the number of cars crashed.
  • [Paul Shaffer][/p?id=Paul%20Shaffer] was an original member of the [Blues Brothers][/f?id=11076] Band and was supposed to be in the film. But because he was also working on Gilda Live (1980), according to [Shaffer's][/p?id=Paul%20Shaffer] memoir, Belushi fired him for being disloyal to the band.
  • [Carrie Fisher][/p?id=1131] guest-hosted the SNL episode the [Blues Brothers][/f?id=11076] debuted in.
  • In the new DVD version of the film, there are several added scenes. Elwood appears in one (sans sunglasses!) to tell his boss that he needs to quit because he wishes to become a priest.
  • During filming, [John Belushi][/p?id=1472] apparently got drunk, went to a stranger's house, asked if he could have a glass of milk and a sandwich and then crashed on their couch for a couple of hours before the cast and crew found him.
  • During the filming of the opening scene, security guards of the prison fired shots at the helicopter filming the overhead shots, thinking that the helicopter was attempting to spy on the structure.
  • Some of the performers were not used to lip-syncing to their pre-recorded songs - the standard procedure for movie musicals. [James Brown][/p?id=James%20Brown] ended up singing his number live with a recorded backing (the rest of his choir was lip-syncing). [John Lee Hooker's][/p?id=John%20Lee%20Hooker] performance of "Boom Boom" was recorded live at [Chicago's][/f?id=11526] Maxwell Street Market. [Aretha Franklin's][/p?id=Aretha%20Franklin] performance is cut together from many, many takes, using the parts where her lip-syncing was actually in sync.
  • In the scene where [Carrie Fisher][/p?id=1131] is in the hair salon doing her nails and reading the instruction manual for the flamethrower, you can see a trio of pictures on the table. They are all of Fisher's character and Jake Blues. In every picture, Jake is wearing his sunglasses and hat.
  • The Head Nazi, played by [Henry Gibson][/p?id=1372], gives a taunting speech to the assembled counter protesters and leads his men in a pledge of allegiance to [Adolf Hitler][/p?id=Adolf%20Hitler], taken almost word-for-word from the documentary The California Reich (1975). He introduces his Nazi group as the "American Socialist White People's Party". The acronym of which, ASWPP, is a diminutive of "Ass Wipe".
  • The line, "They broke my watch!" occurs three times in the film, each time spoken or voiced over by a policeman on the losing end of a car chase with the [Blues Brothers][/f?id=11076]. The first line is in the shopping mall, the second is in the rollover ditch, and the third is in the pile-up under the elevated train line.
  • When Jake Blues is being processed for release from prison, the guards tell the clerk that Blues is from the "Maximum Wing, Block Nine", a reference to a song recorded by the [Blues Brothers][/f?id=11076] called "Riot in Cell Block #9".
