Visste du?

  • Of her films, this was [Vivien Leigh's][/p?id=Vivien%20Leigh] personal favorite.
  • This was [Robert Taylor's][/p?id=1825] favorite of his films.
  • The scene in which Myra and Roy dance to "Auld Lang Syne" was supposed to have dialogue, but nobody could come up with the right words. At about 3:00 in the morning before shooting the scene was to take place, [Mervyn LeRoy][/p?id=Mervyn%20LeRoy], a veteran of silent films, realized that there shouldn't be any lines and that the images should speak for themselves. The result is the most celebrated scene of the film.
  • Released a few months after the invasion of Poland, and in the middle of the Blitzkrieg, this is likely the earliest Hollywood film to include the Second World War in its plot.
