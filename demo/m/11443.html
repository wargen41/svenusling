Visste du?

  • [Jon Favreau][/p?id=1507] was originally chosen by Marvel Studios to direct this film (which he intended to make as a buddy comedy), but he chose to direct [Iron Man][/f?id=15119]. [Nick Cassavetes][/p?id=Nick%20Cassavetes], was also considered to direct this film, and had been set as a director for [Iron Man][/f?id=15119] in December 2004.
  • Despite being "The First Avenger", it is the last solo Avenger film to be released before the team-up film, [The Avengers][/f?id=10515].
  • [Joe Johnston][/p?id=Joe%20Johnston] was chosen as the film's director because of his previous work on the period films The [Rocketeer][/f?id=17741] (like [Captain America][/f?id=11441], also an adventure movie) and October Sky.
  • [Louis Leterrier][/p?id=Louis%20Leterrier] viewed some of the concept art for the film, and was impressed enough to offer his services, but Marvel Studios turned him down. However, his film [The Incredible Hulk][/f?id=15058] features a small appearance by [Captain America][/f?id=11441]: a deleted scene set in the Arctic features his body hidden in a slab of ice.
  • [Sam Worthington][/p?id=1854] and [Will Smith][/p?id=2002] were in early talks for the role of [Captain America][/f?id=11441]. Later on [Garrett Hedlund][/p?id=Garrett%20Hedlund], [Channing Tatum][/p?id=1142], [Scott Porter][/p?id=Scott%20Porter], [Mike Vogel][/p?id=Mike%20Vogel], [Sebastian Stan][/p?id=Sebastian%20Stan], [Chris Evans][/p?id=Chris%20Evans], [Wilson Bethel][/p?id=Wilson%20Bethel], [John Krasinski][/p?id=John%20Krasinski], [Michael Cassidy][/p?id=Michael%20Cassidy], [Chace Crawford][/p?id=Chace%20Crawford] and Jensen Ackles were on the final shortlist for the role. [Kellan Lutz][/p?id=1538], [Ryan Phillippe][/p?id=Ryan%20Phillippe] and [Alexander][/f?id=10123] Skarsgård carried out auditions, but ultimately the role went to [Chris Evans][/p?id=Chris%20Evans].
  • [Alice Eve][/p?id=Alice%20Eve], [Gemma Arterton][/p?id=Gemma%20Arterton] and [Keira Knightley][/p?id=1537] were considered for the role of Peggy Carter. [Emily Blunt][/p?id=1279] turned down the role.
  • This is the fifth live-action adaptation of the superhero. The first was the serial Captain America; the second was the TV movie Captain America and its sequel Captain America II: Death Too Soon; and the fourth was the theatrical film [Captain America][/f?id=11441].
  • [Chris Evans][/p?id=Chris%20Evans] was attracted to the role of [Captain America][/f?id=11441] by its character: "Even if it wasn't a comic book. I think the story of Steve Rogers is great. He's a great guy. Even if it was just a script about anybody, I would probably want to do it. It wasn't necessarily about the comic itself. He's a great character to play; he just happens to be a comic book character."
  • [Chris Evans][/p?id=Chris%20Evans] sixth comic book movie after the two [Fantastic Four][/f?id=13724] movies, Push, The Losers, and Scott Pilgrim vs. the World. He also voiced Casey Jones in TMNT, also based on a comic book.
  • The final Paramount Pictures film produced with Marvel Studios. Disney bought the rights to [The Avengers][/f?id=10515] and [Iron Man][/f?id=15119] 3.
  • [Chris Evans][/p?id=Chris%20Evans] declined the role three times before accepting the part. After that, he had a meeting with the director and the producers who convinced him to take the role.
  • [Stanley Tucci][/p?id=1892] took the role of Dr. Erskine because the role enabled him to use a German accent, which he always wanted to do.
  • The film was originally meant to be a standalone film, but after [Joss Whedon][/p?id=Joss%20Whedon] was hired to direct [The Avengers][/f?id=10515] he was given a copy of the film's script and made a few rewrites to tie it in to the Marvel Cinematic Universe: "I just got to make some character connections. The structure of the thing was really tight and I loved it, but there were a couple of opportunities to find his voice a little bit - and some of the other characters - and make the connections so that you understood exactly why he wanted to be who he wanted to be. And progressing through the script to flesh it out a little bit."
