Visste du?

  • [Michael Biehn][/p?id=1642] was considered for the role of Col. Quaritch. [Cameron][/p?id=1414] rejected him because he'd already cast [Sigourney Weaver][/p?id=1882], and he didn't want people to think it was [Aliens][/f?id=10227] (1986) all over again.
  • [Sigourney Weaver][/p?id=1882] plays a [James Cameron][/p?id=1414] persona for her character in this film. Sigourney stated in an interview, "I teased him because to me I'm playing [Jim Cameron][/p?id=1414] in the movie as this kind of brilliant, approach-driven, idealistic perfectionist. But that same somebody has a great heart underneath. So I have to say I was always kind of channeling him."
  • [James Cameron][/p?id=1414] originally planned to have the film completed for release in 1999. At the time, the special effects he wanted increased the budget to $400 million. No studio would fund the film, and it was shelved for almost 10 years.
  • The movie is 40% live action and 60% photo-realistic CGI. A lot of motion capture technology was used for the CGI scenes.
  • One of the most expensive movies ever made (as of December 2009), with an estimated budget of US $280,000,000.
  • The first time [Sigourney Weaver][/p?id=1882] has appeared in a [James Cameron][/p?id=1414] film since [Aliens][/f?id=10227] (1986).
  • [Avatars][/f?id=10514] have eyebrows and five fingers and toes on their hands and feet, whereas the Na'vi only have four fingers and no eyebrows. The [Avatars][/f?id=10514] are also broader in the chest and arms, appearing more 'muscular'.
  • The Na'vi language was created entirely from scratch by linguist Paul R. Frommer. [James Cameron][/p?id=1414] hired him to construct a language that the actors could pronounce easily, but did not resemble any single human language. Frommer created about 1000 words.
  • Though he is not credited in the film, several locations look very similar to paintings by English surrealist [Roger Dean][/p?id=Roger%20Dean], most notably his works "Floating Islands" and "Arches".
  • The year is never stated, but the video log shows that the year is 2154.
  • The second [James Cameron][/p?id=1414] film not to feature [Michael Biehn][/p?id=1642], [Bill Paxton][/p?id=1083], [Jenette Goldstein][/p?id=Jenette%20Goldstein] or [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062]. The other is [Piranha Part Two: The Spawning][/f?id=17449] (1981).
  • [Matt Damon][/p?id=1624] and [Jake Gyllenhaal][/p?id=1407] were the studio's first choice to play Jake Sully, but [James Cameron][/p?id=1414] decided to cast an unknown [Sam Worthington][/p?id=1854] in the lead role.
  • [James Cameron's][/p?id=1414] first directorial feature film since [Titanic][/f?id=20448] (1997).
  • Became the highest-grossing film of all time on January 26th, 2010, with a worldwide gross of US$2,550,560,068. The previous record-holder was [Titanic][/f?id=20448] (1997), also written and directed by [James Cameron][/p?id=1414], with a worldwide gross of US$1,843,201,268.
  • [Jodie Foster][/p?id=1467] was considered for the role of Dr. Grace Augustine.
  • [Sam Worthington][/p?id=1854] said in an interview that it was easier for him to master the Na'vi language than the American accent.
