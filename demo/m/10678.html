  • The cast spent a month training at a barber college to prepare for their roles. Only [Troy Garity][/p?id=Troy%20Garity] had had previous hair-cutting experience.
  • Shortly after the film's theatrical release in late September 2002, Rev. [Jesse Jackson][/p?id=Jesse%20Jackson] and Rev. [Al Sharpton][/p?id=Al%20Sharpton] protested over some of the statements made by [Cedric the Entertainer's][/p?id=Cedric%20the%20Entertainer] character Eddie about African-American historical figures Rosa Parks ("Rosa Parks ain't do nuthin' but sit her Black ass down; there was a whole lotta other people that sat down on the bus, and they did it way before Rosa did!"), Rev. Dr. Martin [Luther][/f?id=15841] King, Jr. ("Martin [Luther][/f?id=15841] King was a ho [whore]!"), and [Jackson][/p?id=Jesse%20Jackson] himself ("Fuck [Jesse Jackson][/p?id=Jesse%20Jackson]"). [Jackson][/p?id=Jesse%20Jackson] and [Sharpton][/p?id=Al%20Sharpton] pressured MGM to edit these scenes out of the film before its DVD release in January 2003; the film was released with the "controversial" scenes intact.
