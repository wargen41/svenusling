Visste du?

  • According to [Dave Chappelle][/p?id=Dave%20Chappelle] on The [Oprah Winfrey][/p?id=Oprah%20Winfrey] Show (1986), a scene was written during filming in which his character was to wear a dress while disguised as a prostitute. [Chappelle][/p?id=Dave%20Chappelle] adamantly refused. The writers complained to the producers who then tried to convince [Chappelle][/p?id=Dave%20Chappelle] to do the scene, but he still refused. According to [Chappelle][/p?id=Dave%20Chappelle], he felt that it was part of a disturbing trend in which African-American men wear dresses in films.
  • There were plans to do a sequel which never materialized.
  • During pre-production, the film was originally supposed to carry a hard R rating, but after deliberations, the studio had to tone down the film's script to PG-13. This meant changing the plot, dialogue, and characters. John August was hired as a "script doctor" to make these changes.
