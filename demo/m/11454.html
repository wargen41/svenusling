  • [Sissy Spacek][/p?id=1885] wasn't considered for the role of [Carrie][/f?id=11454] until her husband, art director [Jack Fisk][/p?id=Jack%20Fisk], convinced director [Brian De Palma][/p?id=1105] to allow her to audition.
  • The name of the high school is Bates High, a reference to Norman Bates from [Psycho (1960)][/f?id=17541]. In addition, the four note violin theme from Psycho is used over and over in the film.
  • The dizzying camera shot during the prom scene was achieved by placing [William Katt][/p?id=William%20Katt] and [Sissy Spacek][/p?id=1885] on a platform that was spinning in one direction, while the camera was being dollied in the opposite direction.
  • The pig's blood dumped on [Sissy Spacek][/p?id=1885] was karo syrup and food coloring, although she was willing to have real blood dumped on her.
  • [Melanie Griffith][/p?id=1636] auditioned for the title role.
  • This was the first [Stephen King][/p?id=Stephen%20King] novel adapted into a movie.
  • [George Lucas][/p?id=George%20Lucas] and [Brian De Palma][/p?id=1105] held a joint audition for [Carrie][/f?id=11454] and Star Wars (1977). There is a long-standing rumor that originally, [Sissy Spacek][/p?id=1885] was cast as Princess Leia, and [Carrie][/f?id=11454] Fisher as [Carrie][/f?id=11454], but when Fisher refused to appear in nude scenes and [Spacek][/p?id=1885] was willing to do them, they switched parts. However, Fisher refuted this story in a Premiere magazine article called "The Force Wasn't With Them," about actors who auditioned unsuccessfully for Star Wars (1977). That article quoted Fisher as saying, "Not only do I love being nude, I would've been nude then... But anyway, it's total bullshit [that Fisher refused to play [Carrie][/f?id=11454]]."
  • [Amy Irving][/p?id=1030], who played Sue Snell, originally read for the part of Princess Leia, and [William Katt][/p?id=William%20Katt], who played Tommy Ross, originally read for the part of Luke Skywalker in Star Wars (1977).
  • [Nancy Allen][/p?id=1683] claims that she never realized that her character was going to be so evil until she saw the finished film, she thought that she and [John Travolta][/p?id=1501] were playing such self-centered, bickering morons that they were there for comic relief. [Piper Laurie][/p?id=1761] also thought that the character of Margaret White was so over the top that the film had to be a comedy.
  • Originally, [Brian De Palma][/p?id=1105] had used the split screen effect extensively during the prom scene. Disappointed with the results, he re-edited most of the scenes into full frame shots leaving only the few split screen moments that he felt worked.
  • [Stephen King][/p?id=Stephen%20King] based [Carrie][/f?id=11454] White on two girls he knew while at school, both were social outcasts from deeply religious families and both died while still in their twenties.
