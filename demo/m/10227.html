Visste du?

  • Hicks was originally played by [James Remar][/p?id=James%20Remar], but [Michael Biehn][/p?id=1642] replaced him a few days after principal photography began, due to "artistic differences" between [Remar][/p?id=James%20Remar] and director [James Cameron][/p?id=1414]. However, [Remar][/p?id=James%20Remar] still appears in the finished film - but wearing the same armor, and shot from behind, it's impossible to tell the difference between the two actors.
  • All of the cast who were to play the Marines (with the exception of [Michael Biehn][/p?id=1642], who replaced [James Remar][/p?id=James%20Remar] one week into filming) were trained by the S.A.S. (Special Air Service, Britain's elite special operations unit) for two weeks before filming. [Sigourney Weaver][/p?id=1882], [Paul Reiser][/p?id=1728], and [William Hope][/p?id=William%20Hope] didn't participate/attend the training because director [James Cameron][/p?id=1414] felt it would help the actors create a sense of detachment between the three and the Marines - the characters these three actors played were all outsiders to the squad; Ripley being an advisor to the Marines while on the trip to LV-426, Burke being there just for financial reasons and Gorman being a newly-promoted Lieutenant with less experience than most of the Marines.
  • [Sigourney Weaver][/p?id=1882] had initially been very hesitant to reprise her role as Ripley, and had rejected numerous offers from Fox Studios to do any sequels, fearing that her character would be poorly written, and a sub-par sequel could hurt the legacy of the original film. However, she was so impressed by the high quality of [James Cameron's][/p?id=1414] script - specifically, the strong focus on Ripley, the mother-daughter bond between her character and Newt, and the incredible precision with which [Cameron][/p?id=1414] wrote her character, that she finally agreed to do the film.
  • While salary negotiations were going on with [Sigourney Weaver][/p?id=1882] to reprise her character in the second movie, the studio asked [James Cameron][/p?id=1414] to work on an alternative storyline excluding Ripley, but [James Cameron][/p?id=1414] indicated the series is all about Ripley and refused to do so.
  • In a deleted scene, Ripley's ([Sigourney Weaver][/p?id=1882]) daughter was played by [Elizabeth Inglis][/p?id=Elizabeth%20Inglis], [Sigourney Weaver's][/p?id=1882] real-life mother.
  • Bishop states that "it is impossible for me to harm, or by omission of action allow to be harmed, a human being." This is based upon the First Law of Robotics written by science fiction author Isaac Asimov.
