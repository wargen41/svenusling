  • This film marks the 7th time [Johnny Depp][/p?id=1505] has worked under the direction of [Tim Burton][/p?id=1937] and the 6th time for [Helena Bonham Carter][/p?id=1370].
  • Despite the fact that there have been many other Alice in Wonderland films, [Tim Burton][/p?id=1937] has said he never felt a emotional connection to it and always thought it was a series of some girl wondering around from one crazy character to another. (In fact, the original books are part of a once-popular fantasy genre in which the character does nothing except wander around from one crazy encounter to another. Those films which replicated this were being true to the spirit of the original books.) So with this, he attempted to create a framework, an emotional grounding, which he felt he never really had seen in any version before. Tim said that was the challenge for him - to make Alice feel like a story as opposed to a series of events.
  • [Tim Burton][/p?id=1937] and [Johnny Depp][/p?id=1505] worked hard to give the Mad Hatter more depth and presence than in past portrayals. In fact, the pair swapped sketches and themes for the character prior to creating this new version.
  • This marks the 3rd time [Michael Gough][/p?id=Michael%20Gough] has come out of retirement to appear in a film by [Tim Burton][/p?id=1937]. This also marks his 5th film under [Burton's][/p?id=1937] direction.
  • [Johnny Depp][/p?id=1505], who says that he likes "an obstacle" whilst filming, admitted that he found the process of filming on a green screen "exhausting", and that he felt "befuddled by the end of the day".
  • [Danny Elfman][/p?id=Danny%20Elfman] scored the film to green screen footage.
  • Before [Tim Burton][/p?id=1937] was involved with the project, [Anne Hathaway][/p?id=1049] was offered the titular role of Alice, but she turned it down because it was too similar to other roles she had previously played. However, she was keen to work with [Burton][/p?id=1937], so was pleased to be cast as the White Queen.
  • The first [Tim Burton][/p?id=1937] film not to have opening credits.
  • [Michael Sheen][/p?id=1664] was originally cast as the Cheshire Cat, but backed out due to scheduling conflicts.
  • [Dwayne Johnson][/p?id=1255] and [David Walliams][/p?id=David%20Walliams] were considered for the role of The Mad Hatter.
  • The Red Queen asks Stayne at one point whether it is better to be feared than loved, if not of both, he affirms, as she does later, that it is better to be feared than loved. This is a reference to a famous quote from Niccolo Machiavelli's The Prince, where he asks the same question, and answers: "It may be answered that one should wish to be both, but, because it is difficult to unite them in one person, it is much safer to be feared than loved."
