  • [Bud Ekins][/p?id=Bud%20Ekins] who drives the Mustang also did the motorcycle jump for [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904] in [The Great Escape][/f?id=11999] (1963).
  • The director called for speeds of about 75-80 mph, but the cars (including the ones containing the cameras) reached speeds of over 110 mph. Filming of the chase scene took three weeks, resulting in 9 minutes and 42 seconds of footage. They were denied permission to film on the Golden Gate Bridge.
  • The Mustang's interior mirror goes up and down depending who is driving it - [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904] (up, visible) or [Bud Ekins][/p?id=Bud%20Ekins] (down, not visible).
  • [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904] based his character on San Francisco Homicide Inspector Dave Toschi, made famous for his work on the Zodiac killings. [McQueen][/p?id=1904] had a copy made of Toschi's custom fast-draw shoulder holster.
  • [Robert Vaughn][/p?id=1826], who plays politician Walter Chalmers received the script and didn't like it. [Vaughn][/p?id=1826] felt that there was no plot nor a sensible storyline. [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904] insisted [Vaughn][/p?id=1826] do the film which the actor had refused, until the studio kept offering him more money when he finally said yes.
  • Director [Peter Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] was personally selected for this movie by [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904] because [Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] had filmed a realistic car chase a year earlier through the streets of London in [Robbery][/f?id=R%C3%A5net] (1967).
  • One of the first things [Peter Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] did when he got the job was persuade Warner Brothers to buy him a lightweight Arriflex camera that he could use for all his hand-held footage.
  • [Robert Vaughn][/p?id=1826] has repeatedly said that his performance in this film is his best and contains the work he is most proud of.
  • [Bullitt's][/f?id=11380] reverse burnout during the chase scene actually wasn't in the script - [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904] had mistakenly missed the turn. The footage was still kept, though.
  • According to [Peter Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates], [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904] made a point to keep his head near the open car window during the famous chase scene so that audiences would be reassured that it was he, not a stunt man, who was driving.
  • [Peter Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] hired a local trucking company for some background shots (most notably the scene where the Dodge Charger crashes into the gas station), but sent back the initial truck because it was red. He didn't want any red vehicles in the movie because it would detract from the blood. A blue truck was dispatched in its place.
  • [Joanna Cassidy's][/p?id=Joanna%20Cassidy] film debut.
  • The film's famous chase scene wasn't originally in the script. In the first draft of "[Bullitt][/f?id=11380]", adapted from Robert L. Pike's novel "Mute Witness", Det. Frank [Bullitt][/f?id=11380] was a Boston policeman who ate a lot of ice cream and never solved a case. The book had originally been bought with [Spencer Tracy][/p?id=1888] in mind; but with [Tracy's][/p?id=1888] death, the property fell into the hands of [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904] and Producer Philip D'Antoni. D'Antoni added the chase and changed the location to San Francisco.
  • First major film to use the word "bullshit".
  • Although [Bonnie and Clyde][/f?id=11260] (1967) had popularized the use of squibs to simulate gunshot wounds a year earlier, this was one of the first films to incorporate them with blood packets.
  • Much was made at the time, and over the years since, of Lt. [Bullitt's][/f?id=11380] stylish "casual" attire of a turtleneck worn with a sport coat, slacks, and suede-like shoes. Since the major portion of the story in the film takes place over a Saturday and Sunday, this was actually in keeping with some police department's traditions of a more relaxed dress code on weekends for plainclothes officers. [Bullitt][/f?id=11380] is first seen at work when meeting Chalmers on a Friday morning - wearing a traditionally conservative navy suit under his trench coat, with a white shirt, dark tie and dress shoes.
