Visste du?

  • [Sylvester Stallone][/p?id=1915] appears uncredited as a subway thug. This was one of his earliest film roles, not a cameo. According to website Every [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] Movie, "[Allen][/p?id=2010] initially sent [Stallone][/p?id=1915] back to the casting agency after deciding he wasn't 'tough-looking' enough. [Stallone][/p?id=1915] pleaded with him, and eventually convinced him to change his mind".
  • According to the Eric Lax biography, the musicians in the dinner scene at General Vargas' house were actually to be playing instruments, but the rented instruments hadn't arrived, and rather than wait, [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] decided the miming would fit with the tone of the film.
  • In an interview, [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] was asked why he named the movie "[Bananas][/f?id=10666]". His response: "Because there are no bananas in it." A reference to the 1920s novelty song "Yes we have no bananas."
  • The majority of the scenes in the film were improvised. When [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] felt he had captured the right shot, he would move on to the next one.
  • The third feature film directed by [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010], and the first in which he had nearly full creative control.
  • [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] said he made a conscious decision not to show any blood to maintain the light, farcical tone of the film.
  • During the trial [J. Edgar Hoover][/p?id=J.%20Edgar%20Hoover] testifies, disguised as a black woman. While it was meant here as a joke, it would be revealed to the world after he died that Hoover liked to wear women's clothes, something that no one at the time of the movie would have ever believed.
  • The picture was originally conceived as a star vehicle for actor [Robert Morse][/p?id=Robert%20Morse].
  • During the training montage when Fielding is learning how to throw a hand grenade, the pin in his hand explodes after he throws the grenade. The reaction by [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] is real as he was slightly singed by the incendiary device hidden in his hand, but Woody decided against doing the scene again and left it in the film as is.
  • Although they play love interests, the film was made after [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] and [Louise Lasser's][/p?id=Louise%20Lasser] divorce.
  • In an interview with Robert B. Greenfield of Rolling Stone magazine in 1971, [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] said: "They say it's a political film but I don't really believe much in politics. [Groucho Marx][/p?id=Groucho%20Marx] has told me that [The ][/p?id=The%20]Groucho Marx||Marx[ Brothers'][/p?id=%20Brothers] films were never consciously anti-establishment or political. It's always got to be a funny movie first".
  • This was the first of a three-picture deal [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] had with United Artists. [Allen's][/p?id=2010] affiliation with United Artists continued into the late 1980s.
