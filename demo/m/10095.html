  • Author Ian Fleming wanted his cousin [Christopher Lee][/p?id=1169] to play Dr. No. ([Lee][/p?id=1169] would later appear as Scaramanga in the 007 flick [The Man with the Golden Gun][/f?id=16015] (1974), and would play the character that inspired Fleming to create Dr. No, Dr. Fu Manchu, in several films.) Fleming also asked [Noel Coward][/p?id=Noel%20Coward] to play the part of Dr. No. [Coward][/p?id=Noel%20Coward] turned down the part by replying with a telegram that read, "Dr. No? No! No! No!" One of [Coward's][/p?id=Noel%20Coward] objections was having to wear metal hands. [Max von Sydow][/p?id=1632] turned down the part in order to play Jesus Christ in [The Greatest Story Ever Told][/f?id=16008] (1965), and would finally play a Bond villain in [Never Say Never Again][/f?id=16985] (1983). The role went to [Joseph Wiseman][/p?id=Joseph%20Wiseman], the only early Bond villain not to have his voice dubbed by another actor.

  • [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866] is morbidly afraid of spiders. The shot of the spider in his bed was originally done with a sheet of glass between him and the spider, but when this didn't look realistic enough, the scene was re-shot with stuntman [Bob Simmons][/p?id=Bob%20Simmons]. [Simmons][/p?id=Bob%20Simmons] reported that the tarantula crawling over Bond was the scariest stunt he had ever performed. According to Steven Jay Rubin's 1981 book "The James Bond Films", this tarantula was named Rosie.
  • The only James Bond movie that does not feature a pre-titles sequence.
  • Ken Adam's sets so impressed [Stanley Kubrick][/p?id=1891] that he hired him the following year to be production designer on [Dr. Strangelove or: How I Learned to Stop Worrying and Love the Bomb][/f?id=12943] (1964).
  • Ian Fleming didn't originally like the casting of [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866] as James Bond. Bond was English and [Connery][/p?id=1866] was Scottish, Bond was upper-class and [Connery][/p?id=1866] was working-class, Bond was refined and educated and [Connery][/p?id=1866] was too rugged. After seeing the film, Fleming softened and decided that [Connery][/p?id=1866] was perfectly cast. In the novel "[On Her Majesty's Secret Service][/f?id=14989]," Bond was revealed to have Scottish ancestry and Bond's girlfriend Tracy Vicenzo was described with [Ursula Andress'][/p?id=Ursula%20Andress] details. Ironically, in the movie version of [On Her Majesty's Secret Service][/f?id=14989] (1969), Bond and Tracy are played by [George Lazenby][/p?id=George%20Lazenby] and [Diana Rigg][/p?id=Diana%20Rigg] who do not fit these descriptions.
  • Producers Albert R. Broccoli and Harry Saltzman used [Alfred Hitchcock's][/p?id=1021] [North by Northwest][/f?id=15005] (1959) as the template for this film and the subsequent early James Bond films. In fact, the role of James Bond was first offered to [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134], the star of [North by Northwest][/f?id=15005] (1959), who would commit to one film only and was otherwise too old, and then to its suave and urbane villain, [James Mason][/p?id=1422], who would commit to only two, while Broccoli and Saltzman wanted an actor willing to make a multi-film commitment to the role and the projected series. American actor [Steve Reeves][/p?id=Steve%20Reeves] who turned the role down. At the time, [Reeves][/p?id=Steve%20Reeves] had become an international box office sensation in a group of European-made mythological/historical spectacles. Acoording to legend, Irish actor [Patrick McGoohan][/p?id=Patrick%20McGoohan] of "Danger Man" (1960) turned the role down on moral grounds. Other actors considered for the lead role included [Trevor Howard][/p?id=1964], [Rex Harrison][/p?id=1778], [Richard Johnson][/p?id=Richard%20Johnson], William Franklyn, [Stanley Baker][/p?id=Stanley%20Baker], [Ian Hendry][/p?id=Ian%20Hendry] (co-star of "The Avengers" (1961)) and [Richard Burton][/p?id=1782]. Director [John Frankenheimer][/p?id=John%20Frankenheimer] claims Broccoli offered him the role of James Bond. According to Albert R. Broccoli's autobiography "When the Snow Melts", [Roger Moore][/p?id=1835] was Ian Fleming's choice to play Bond, largely based on his performance as "The Saint" (1962). This, however, turns out not to be true, as "The Saint" (1962) didn't begin airing in the UK until October 1962, one day after the premiere of Dr. No (1962). [David Niven][/p?id=1221], too old for a serious Bond, played the parody Bond in [Casino Royale (1967)][/f?id=11457], and [Roger Moore][/p?id=1835] played the official James Bond in [Live and Let Die][/f?id=15719] (1973) and 6 other movies.
