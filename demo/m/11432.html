  • The final note in the credits, "To Johnny LaRue - thanks to you, we got our crane shot", is a reference to one of [John Candy's][/p?id=1475] recurring characters on "SCTV Channel" (1983) .
  • [Michael Moore][/p?id=Michael%20Moore] wrote the screenplay during the first Gulf War, in reaction to what he felt was the US government's manipulation of the media in supporting Operation Desert Storm.
  • A deleted scene featured [James Belushi][/p?id=James%20Belushi] and Mary Charlotte Wilcox as Canadian border guards wearing beaver hats. They improvised the entire scene. Afterward, [Belushi][/p?id=James%20Belushi], a relative newcomer to the comedy scene, told comedy veteran Wilcox (whom he didn't recognize) "You're kind of funny" and that she should try to get into comedy.
  • [Michael Moore][/p?id=Michael%20Moore] was turned down by 47 different film companies before [Madonna's][/p?id=Madonna] Maverick Productions picked up the option.
  • In one scene, the characters played by [John Candy][/p?id=1475], [Bill Nunn][/p?id=Bill%20Nunn] and [Kevin J. O'Connor][/p?id=Kevin%20J.%20O'Connor] sit around a campfire and discuss films in which black characters are killed before their white co-stars. O'Connor mentions [Forrest Gump][/f?id=14003] (1994), which was released six months after principal photography on "Bacon" was completed, and four months after [Candy's][/p?id=1475] death. The line was looped in after the scene was shot.
