  • [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] once famously said the following quotes about sex: (1) "It's the most fun I've ever had without laughing" (2) "Having sex is like playing bridge. If you don't have a good partner, you'd better have a good hand" and (3) "I'm a practicing heterosexual, although bisexuality immediately doubles your chances for a date on Saturday night". Moreover, [Allen][/p?id=2010] once said that he included in this movie "every funny idea I've ever had about sex, including several that led to my own divorce".
  • According to the book "[Woody Allen][/p?id=2010]" by John Baxter, [Gene Wilder][/p?id=1321] once said of the making of this movie: "It was like walking on a Bergman set: people talking in whispers, serious looks on Woody's face. He communicates through silence".
  • [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] saw Dr. David Reuben promoting his book on which this film is based on The Tonight Show Starring [Johnny Carson][/p?id=Johnny%20Carson] (1962). When asked by [Johnny Carson][/p?id=Johnny%20Carson] "Is sex dirty?", Reuben replied, "It is if you're doing it right" which is a line from [Allen's][/p?id=2010] [Take the Money and Run][/f?id=Ta%20Pengarna%20Och%20Stick] (1969). [Allen][/p?id=2010] was offended by Dr. Reuben using his joke and by his book so he made this film as a form of revenge against him. Dr. Reuben did not like the film.
  • A segment that was filmed but eventually cut out of the film was "What Makes a Man a Homosexual?" The sequence had [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] as a common spider and [Louise Lasser][/p?id=Louise%20Lasser] playing a black widow. After a mating dance on the black widow's web, the spiders make love and the black widow eats the common spider. [Allen][/p?id=2010] cut the sequence as he was unable to find a suitable ending for it. Apparently, some production stills from it have been used for promotional materials for the film even DVD covers.
  • [Woody Allen's][/p?id=2010] favorite segment in this movie is "Why Do Some Women Have Trouble Reaching an Orgasm?".
  • The first [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] film which had a considerable budget.
  • The only ever official filmed adaptation of another person's previously written work by [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] though a number of [Allen's][/p?id=2010] films have been inspired and influence by various films and novels. However, though little is taken from Dr. David Reuben's source book 'Everything You [Always][/f?id=10276] Wanted to Know About Sex (But Were Afraid to Ask)', the picture hardly counts as an adaptation.
  • [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] interviewed [Lon Chaney Jr.][/p?id=1590] for a role in this film. This was possibly for the mad scientist role that went to [John Carradine][/p?id=1476].
  • [Laurence Olivier][/p?id=1568] was the second choice to play Dr. Doug Ross. The part was played by [Gene Wilder][/p?id=1321].
  • The fourth feature film directed by [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010].
  • [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] originally began work on the screenplay with Marshall Brickman.
