Visste du?

  • The story that [Billy Crystal][/p?id=1087] tells about his "best day" of going to a Yankee game with his father is a true story from his childhood. He notes at one point that, "I still have the program." Not only does he really still have it, but he got Mickey Mantle to autograph it twice: once at the game that day and once again some 20 years later on a talk show they were both guests on.
  • [Billy Crystal][/p?id=1087] co-wrote the story, but is not given on-screen credit.
  • [Billy Crystal][/p?id=1087] liked the horse he rode so much, he bought him.
  • [Bruno Kirby][/p?id=1118] was extremely allergic to horses. Prior to each day of filming involving a horse, he required an injection of allergy medication to get through the day's shooting.
  • [Daniel Stern][/p?id=1205] declined the riding lessons offered for the movie in order to better convey his character's inexperience at horse riding.
  • There is a 38-year gap between [Jack Palance's][/p?id=1403] Oscar nomination for Shane (1953) in 1954 and his win for [City Slickers][/f?id=11557] (1991) in 1992.
  • [Rick Moranis][/p?id=1795] was cast in a major role but left the project because of creative differences.
  • [Jake Gyllenhaal's][/p?id=1407] film debut.
  • [Billy Crystal][/p?id=1087], a notoriously die hard New York Yankees fan, wears a New York Mets cap in the film. As explained by [Crystal][/p?id=1087] in the DVD Commentary, he did this because the New York Mets had made a major contribution and helped out on his annual Comic Relief charity.
  • According to [Billy Crystal][/p?id=1087] in the "making of" documentary, when he first came up with the story and came up with the character of Curly, he immediately wrote down [Jack Palance's][/p?id=1403] name as the ideal choice for the role. No other actor was considered for the role.
