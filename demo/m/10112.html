Visste du?

  • "The Arabian Nights Reprise", written by Alan Menken and Howard Ashman, was originally written and recorded for the first [Aladdin][/f?id=Aladdin].
  • After a contract dispute with the Disney company over likeness rights, [Robin Williams][/p?id=1828] agreed to return for this film as the voice of the Genie. As a result, many of the ideas and gags that were not used in [Aladdin][/f?id=Aladdin] (1992) could be retried in the making of this film.
  • Among the Genie's many transformations is an old, grandmotherly woman, clearly meant to be "Mrs. Doubtfire" a character played by [Robin Williams][/p?id=1828] in the film of the same title, Mrs. Doubtfire (1993). The character originated as "'[Andy Kaufman''s][/p?id=Andy%20Kaufman] Grandmother" at his famous Carnegie Hall Show and was adapted for the movie.
  • As [Aladdin][/f?id=Aladdin] and Jasmine walk down the aisle together, Prince Uncouthma, his wife, and his son Bud are at the front of the crowd. Uncouthma was a reoccurring character from the [Aladdin][/f?id=Aladdin] TV series.
  • [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866] was originally cast in the role of Cassim, but scheduling conflicts with another film forced him to pull out.
  • The scene with the Genie consoling Princess Jasmine while [Aladdin][/f?id=Aladdin] is away at the lair of the 40 thieves was completely improvised by [Robin Williams][/p?id=1828].
