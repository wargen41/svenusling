  • The studio hoped to turn the film into a franchise, but it never happened. Dangerous Passion (1990), a totally unrelated film which also starred [Carl Weathers][/p?id=Carl%20Weathers], was re-titled 'Action Jackson 2' in some countries.
  • [Carl Weathers][/p?id=Carl%20Weathers] came up with the idea for the film on the set of [Predator][/f?id=17792] (1987), during conversations with producer [Joel Silver][/p?id=Joel%20Silver] about their shared love of 1970s blaxploitation films. The title was born after [Weathers][/p?id=Carl%20Weathers] spoke to an Australian crew member about the project. To indicate his interest in working on the film, the crew member said, "I'm in like Action Jackson."
  • [Carl Weathers][/p?id=Carl%20Weathers], who was 39 when shooting started, joked that the rigorous shooting schedule for the action sequences aged his body into his 60's, and blamed director [Craig R. Baxley][/p?id=Craig%20R.%20Baxley].
  • [Carl Weathers][/p?id=Carl%20Weathers], [Bill Duke][/p?id=Bill%20Duke], and [Sonny Landham][/p?id=Sonny%20Landham] all had roles in [Predator][/f?id=Robdjuret] (1987). In one scene, when [Weathers][/p?id=Carl%20Weathers] and Vanity are walking down the sidewalk of a busy street, multiple film posters are visible on the side of a building.
  • The film has become a cult hit. It was a box office success, despite mixed reviews from critics. A planned sequel was ultimately scrapped after the film company sold the rights to Sony Pictures, which later sold its library to Warner Bros., where the plans languished.
  • According to the Captain, Jericho "Action" Jackson was a high school star athlete in track and field who later earned a Harvard Law degree, but chose to pursue a career in police work. In real life, [Carl Weathers][/p?id=Carl%20Weathers] played professional football for five years in the NFL and in the Canadian Football League before pursuing an acting career.
