  • [Alfred Hitchcock's][/p?id=1021] final film.
  • A street sign in the film reads "Bates Ave". The Bates Motel was the setting for [Hitchcock's][/p?id=1021] earlier film [Psycho (1960)][/f?id=17541].
  • [Roy Thinnes][/p?id=Roy%20Thinnes] was originally hired to play [Arthur Adamson][/p?id=Arthur%20Adams], but [Hitchcock's][/p?id=1021] first choice [William Devane][/p?id=William%20Devane] became available so [Hitchcock][/p?id=1021] fired [Thinnes][/p?id=Roy%20Thinnes] without a reason and hired [Devane][/p?id=William%20Devane]. Some key scenes had been shot prior to this. Everything that had been shot was re-shot except for long shots which to this day remain as [Roy Thinnes][/p?id=Roy%20Thinnes] and not [William Devane][/p?id=William%20Devane].
  • [Alfred Hitchcock][/p?id=1021] was famous for making his actors follow the script to the word, but in this movie he let the characters improvise and use their own dialogue.
  • [Alfred Hitchcock][/p?id=1021] initially wanted [Al Pacino][/p?id=1008] for the role of Lumley. According to an interview on the DVD with [Bruce Dern][/p?id=1114], who ultimately got the part, [Pacino's][/p?id=1008] asking price was too high because of the recent successes he had enjoyed ([Serpico][/f?id=18244] (1973), [The Godfather][/f?id=14369] (1972), etc.)
  • The final shot in the movie, a wink by the [Barbara Harris][/p?id=Barbara%20Harris] character was a jokey reference that was not planned but [Alfred Hitchcock][/p?id=1021] decided to leave in.
  • [Lillian Gish][/p?id=Lillian%20Gish] wanted to test for the role of Julia Rainbird but the role had been promised to [Cathleen Nesbitt][/p?id=Cathleen%20Nesbitt].
  • [Jack Nicholson][/p?id=1402] couldn't accept the role of George Lumley, as he was doing [One Flew Over the Cuckoo's Nest][/f?id=G%C3%B6kboet] (1975).
  • [Liza Minnelli][/p?id=Liza%20Minnelli] was originally cast to play the role that later went to [Barbara Harris][/p?id=Barbara%20Harris].
  • [Karen Black][/p?id=Karen%20Black] initially wanted the role of Blanche.
  • [Faye Dunaway][/p?id=1295] was offered the role of Fran.
