Visste du?

  • After Michael Myers died at the end of Alla helgons blodiga natt 2 (1981), the plan by [John Carpenter][/p?id=John%20Carpenter] was to make a new "Halloween" movie each year, each telling a different Halloween-related story. After this movie underperformed at the box office, the film-makers decided to bring Michael back to life for future sequels.
  • A novelization of the film was published in 1982 by science-fiction writer [Dennis][/f?id=12020] Etchison under the pseudonym Jack Martin. Despite the film's commercial failure, the book became a best-seller and was even reissued two years after the film's release, in 1984.
  • The original writer of the story was Nigel Kneale but he sued the producers to take his name off the movie after seeing how violent it was.
  • Supposedly, part of the genesis of this film came from a comment made by film critic [Rex Reed][/p?id=Rex%20Reed]. [Reed][/p?id=Rex%20Reed] panned [Halloween 2][/f?id=Alla%20helgons%20blodiga%20natt%202] (1981), saying it was so bad that, "If they make a Halloween III, I'll turn in my press card."
  • The film's original director, [Joe Dante][/p?id=Joe%20Dante], approached Nigel Kneale to write the film while Kneale was temporarily living in Hollywood writing the remake of [Creature from the Black Lagoon][/f?id=18516] (1954) for director [John Landis][/p?id=1490] that was never made due to budget cost. [Dante][/p?id=Joe%20Dante] wanted a new and different story than the two previous films in the series, so he suggested Kneale write a treatment around the word Halloween. The producers liked the idea, and after [Joe Dante][/p?id=Joe%20Dante] moved on to another project, producer [John Carpenter's][/p?id=John%20Carpenter] regular collaborator, [Tommy ][/p?id=Tommy%20]Lee Wallace[[, came in as the new director. Kneale initially blamed the drastic changes to his script on executive producer Dino De Laurentiis not understanding his dialogue when it was translated to Italian. Kneale requested his writing screen credit be removed once his comical mystery screenplay was rewritten by an uncredited ][/p?id=][/p?id=%2C%20came%20in%20as%20the%20new%20director.%20Kneale%20initially%20blamed%20the%20drastic%20changes%20to%20his%20script%20on%20executive%20producer%20Dino%20De%20Laurentiis%20not%20understanding%20his%20dialogue%20when%20it%20was%20translated%20to%20Italian.%20Kneale%20requested%20his%20writing%20screen%20credit%20be%20removed%20once%20his%20comical%20mystery%20screenplay%20was%20rewritten%20by%20an%20uncredited%20%5D%5B%2Fp%3Fid%3D]%2C%20came%20in%20as%20the%20new%20director.%20Kneale%20initially%20blamed%20the%20drastic%20changes%20to%20his%20script%20on%20executive%20producer%20Dino%20De%20Laurentiis%20not%20understanding%20his%20dialogue%20when%20it%20was%20translated%20to%20Italian.%20Kneale%20requested%20his%20writing%20screen%20credit%20be%20removed%20once%20his%20comical%20mystery%20screenplay%20was%20rewritten%20by%20an%20uncredited%20]John Carpenter||Carpenter[, and then later ][/p?id=%2C%20and%20then%20later%20]Lee Wallace||Wallace** (who received sole screen credit as writer), to include more gore and simplify the story.
  • [Stacey Nelkin][/p?id=Stacey%20Nelkin] was hired on the spot after she read for the role of Ellie Grimbridge.
  • In an interview with the Pittsburgh Press newspaper shortly before the film's release, [Tom Atkins][/p?id=Tom%20Atkins] told the hometown publication that he didn't know how the movie was going to end because they "shot a couple of different endings".
  • "Season of the Witch" was the original working title of [Martin Scorsese's][/p?id=1619] [Mean Streets][/f?id=13294] (1973). "[Season Of The Witch][/f?id=18069]" is also the name of a song by Donovan and an alternative name for the [George A. Romero][/p?id=George%20A.%20Romero] film Hungry Wives (1972). Also the name of a [Nicolas Cage][/p?id=1696] movie: [Season Of The Witch][/f?id=18069] (2011).
