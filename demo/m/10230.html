Visste du?

  • The real Nando Parrado ([Ethan Hawke's][/p?id=Ethan%20Hawke] character) was technical advisor to the film.
  • Shot in sequence, an unusual practice for a film of this size.
  • The crash sequence took nine days to film. As the set was mounted on a huge gimbel, most of the cast were taking motion sickness pills as they spent a large amount of time being tossed about.
  • The film's main location was the ski town of Panorama in the Canadian Rockies. To get all 150 cast and crew members to the location every morning took a fleet of five helicopters.
  • The real plane crashed on Friday the 13 October 1972.
  • The opening and closing statements narrated by [John Malkovich][/p?id=1493] (playing an older Carlitos Paez) were written by the real Carlitos Paez for the film.
  • Director [Frank Marshall][/p?id=Frank%20Marshall] auditioned novice actor [Brad Pitt][/p?id=1096] for the Roberto Canessa part, but [Pitt's][/p?id=1096] reading was poor, and the part went to Josh [Hamilton][/f?id=14417] instead.
