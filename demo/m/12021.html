  • [Martin Scorsese][/p?id=1619] wanted to shoot the film in Boston, where the story is set. But due to concerns on setting up production and politics, the producers chose New York City to double for Boston because of the state's 15% tax credit. The bulk of the movie was shot in New York City while a six-week shooting schedule was split in two for Boston, shooting the first half in June and the second half in August. After the success of this film, Massachusetts created a 25% tax credit for filmmaking.
  • A possible reason why [Leonardo DiCaprio][/p?id=1582] did not receive an Oscar nomination for his performance in this movie was because the Warner Bros. Studios initially did not want to favor [DiCaprio][/p?id=1582] over his co-stars and place him in the leading actor category. The studio favored [DiCaprio's][/p?id=1582] leading performance in Blood Diamond (2006) (which eventually got him a nomination). [DiCaprio][/p?id=1582] himself refused to campaign against his male co-stars in the supporting actor category, so Warner bought no supporting actor ads for [DiCaprio][/p?id=1582], and he did not receive a nomination.
  • [Leonardo DiCaprio][/p?id=1582] was cast in the title role in [The Good Shepherd][/f?id=Den%20Innersta%20Kretsen] (2006), but he dropped out to play Billy Costigan in this movie. [Matt Damon][/p?id=1624] then took the role. [Robert De Niro][/p?id=1808] turned down the role of Queenan to appear in [The Good Shepherd][/f?id=Den%20Innersta%20Kretsen].
  • [Mark Wahlberg][/p?id=1614] based his performance on the police officers who'd arrested him about two dozen times in his youth, and the reactions of his parents who had to come bail him out with their grocery money.
  • This is the movie with the most uses of the word "fuck" and its derivatives (237) to win the Best Picture Oscar.
  • When receiving the top award from the Director's Guild of America for this film, [Martin Scorsese][/p?id=1619] said that this "is the first movie I have ever done with a plot."
  • [Martin Scorsese][/p?id=1619] said that he made this film to honor crime genre directors such as [Robert Aldrich][/p?id=Robert%20Aldrich], [Samuel Fuller][/p?id=Samuel%20Fuller] and [Don Siegel][/p?id=Don%20Siegel].
  • Originally, [Jack Nicholson][/p?id=1402] turned down his role in the movie, but after a meeting with [Martin Scorsese][/p?id=1619], [William Monahan][/p?id=William%20Monahan] and [Leonardo DiCaprio][/p?id=1582], he was finally convinced to play the role of [Frank Costello][/p?id=Frank%20Costello]. The main reason he joined the production was because he had previously done a few comedies, and wanted to play a villain again, and he considered the character of [Costello][/p?id=Frank%20Costello] to be the ultimate incarnation of evil.
  • As research for his character's occupation, [Matt Damon][/p?id=1624] worked with a Massachusetts State Police unit out of Boston. He accompanied them on routine patrols, participated in a drug raid and was taught proper police procedures like how to pat down a suspect.
  • [Martin Scorsese][/p?id=1619] had originally wanted to cast a known actress, either [Kate Winslet][/p?id=1530], [Emily Blunt][/p?id=1279] or [Hilary Swank][/p?id=1374], for the part of Madolyn. But he later decided to go with someone new instead ([Vera Farmiga][/p?id=1975]).
  • [Jack Nicholson][/p?id=1402] refused to wear a Boston Red Sox hat during filming and instead wore his New York Yankees hat.
  • [Martin Scorsese][/p?id=1619] put the finishing touches on this film a week before its theatrical release.
  • Originally [Brad Pitt][/p?id=1096] was cast as Colin Sullivan, but later dropped out to work with Alejandro González Iñárritu in Babel (2006). He continued to produce the film under his (and his then wife [Jennifer Aniston's][/p?id=1446]) production company, Plan B.
  • After completing The Aviator (2004), [Martin Scorsese][/p?id=1619] kept [Alec Baldwin][/p?id=1017] in mind for future collaboration and ultimately decided to cast him in the role of Ellerby which was offered to [Mel Gibson][/p?id=1635] first, but [Gibson][/p?id=1635] was unable to accept the part because he was starting production on [Apocalypto][/f?id=10369] (2006) at the time.
  • The scene where [Frank Costello][/p?id=Frank%20Costello] throws cocaine on hookers was one of many bizarre ideas contributed by [Jack Nicholson][/p?id=1402], who also suggested wearing a strap-on for the scene with [Matt Damon][/p?id=1624] in the porn theater.
  • The only remake of a foreign film to win an Academy Award for Best Picture.
