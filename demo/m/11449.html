  • The name of Carlito's ([Al Pacino's][/p?id=1008]) nightclub is "El ParaÃ­so", the same name as the food stand that [Pacino's][/p?id=1008] character operated in [Scarface][/f?id=18000] (1983).
  • The exterior of the hospital where Carlito visits his bedridden attorney is the same one where Vito Corleone is taken in The [Godfather][/f?id=14369] (1972), also starring [Al Pacino][/p?id=1008].
  • [Sean Penn][/p?id=1868] took the role of [David Kleinfeld][/p?id=David%20Klein] just for the money so he could use it to fund his second film [The Crossing Guard][/f?id=14964] (1995).
  • [Carlito's Way][/f?id=11449] is adapted from the novel "After Hours" by 'Edwin Torres'. However, it took the title from Torres' first novel about the rise of crime boss Carlos Brigante so that it would not be confused with [Martin Scorsese's][/p?id=1619] New York club-based comedy-drama, [After Hours][/f?id=13403] (1985).
  • Directors John McKenzie and [Abel Ferrara][/p?id=Abel%20Ferrara] were considered before [De Palma][/p?id=1105] was hired. [De Palma][/p?id=1105] was originally reluctant to accept the job because he did not want to make another film about a Latino gangster as he did in [Scarface][/f?id=18000] (1983). But the script convinced him otherwise.
  • According to [John Leguizamo][/p?id=1491] in his autobiography, he and [Penelope Ann Miller][/p?id=Penelope%20Ann%20Miller] did not get along during filming. He claims he got revenge on her by having the wardrobe department take in her clothes an inch at a time causing [Miller][/p?id=Penelope%20Ann%20Miller] to go on a crash diet.
  • In 1989, film producer Elliott Kastner filed a lawsuit against [Pacino][/p?id=1008] for allegedly backing out of a deal to star in a film as Carlito with [Marlon Brando][/p?id=1616] as [Kleinfeld][/p?id=David%20Klein]. The suit was eventually dropped.
  • [Brian De Palma's][/p?id=1105] first choice for the role of Gail was Irish actress [Alison Doody][/p?id=Alison%20Doody] from [Indiana Jones and the Last Crusade][/f?id=15062] (1989). She rejected the offer because of a topless dance sequence she would have to perform in the movie.
  • [Pacino][/p?id=1008] first met Judge 'Edwin Torres' in 1973 while he was preparing for [Serpico][/f?id=18244] (1973). At the time, Torres was in the midst of writing the novels on which the film is based and discussed them with [Pacino][/p?id=1008]. Twenty years later, Torres took [Pacino][/p?id=1008] to East Harlem to help him prepare of the role of Carlito.
