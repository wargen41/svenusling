  • [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904] was the first to turn down the role of Captain Willard.
  • [Harvey Keitel][/p?id=1364] was then cast as Willard. Two weeks into shooting, director [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] replaced him with [Martin Sheen][/p?id=1620].
  • [George Lucas][/p?id=George%20Lucas] was originally set to direct "Apocalypse Now" from a screenplay by [John Milius][/p?id=John%20Milius]. [Lucas'][/p?id=George%20Lucas] initial plan was to shoot the movie as a faux documentary on location in South Vietnam while the war was still in progress. [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299], who was to be the executive producer, tried to get the film made as part of a production deal with Warner Bros. The deal fell through, and [Coppola][/p?id=1299] went on to direct [The Godfather][/f?id=14369] (1972). By the time both men were powerful enough to get the film made, Saigon had fallen and [Lucas][/p?id=George%20Lucas] was busy making [Star Wars][/f?id=19851] (1977). [Milius][/p?id=John%20Milius] had no interest in directing the film. [Lucas][/p?id=George%20Lucas] gave Coppola his blessing to direct the film himself.
  • While in pre-production, director [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] consulted his friend and mentor [Roger Corman][/p?id=Roger%20Corman] for advice about shooting in the Phillipines. [Corman's][/p?id=Roger%20Corman] advice: "Don't go."
  • The boat's name is "Erebus," seen on the transom. The back of the seat in the forward turret of the PBR (Lance's position) has the words "God's country" written on it. The steel shield protecting the turret on the back of the boat is imprinted with the words "Canned Heat".
  • [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] believed that [Marlon Brando][/p?id=1616] was familiar with Joseph Conrad's "Heart of Darkness" and had prepared for the role before the legendary actor arrived on the set. When [Brando][/p?id=1616] did come out, Coppola was horrified to find that [Brando][/p?id=1616] had never read "Heart of Darkness", did not know his lines, and had become extremely fat (Kurtz had always been written as a tall but starvingly-thin man). After some panicking, [Coppola][/p?id=1299] decided to film the 5'10" [Brando][/p?id=1616] as if he was a massively built, 6'5" brute (to explain [Brando's][/p?id=1616] size) and steered the camera clear of [Brando's][/p?id=1616] huge belly.
  • [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] spent days reading Joseph Conrad's source novel "Heart of Darkness" out loud to [Marlon Brando][/p?id=1616] on the set.
  • Willard tells Kurtz that he is from Ohio, [Martin Sheen][/p?id=1620] is actually from Ohio.
  • The scene at the beginning with Captain Willard alone in his hotel room was completely unscripted. [Martin Sheen][/p?id=1620] told the shooting crew to just let the cameras roll. [Sheen][/p?id=1620] was actually drunk in the scene and punched the mirror which was real glass. [Sheen][/p?id=1620] also began sobbing and tried to attack [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299]. The crew was so disturbed by his actions that they wanted to stop shooting, but [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299]||Coppola** wanted to keep the cameras going.
  • When [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] asked [Al Pacino][/p?id=1008] to play Willard, [Pacino][/p?id=1008] turned him down saying, "I know what this is going to be like. You're going to be up there in a helicopter telling me what to do, and I'm gonna be down there in a swamp for five months." The shoot actually lasted 16 months.
  • [Marlon Brando][/p?id=1616] so angered [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] that the director turned over the filming of [Brando's][/p?id=1616] scenes to [Jerry Ziesmer][/p?id=Jerry%20Ziesmer], the assistant director.
  • [Francis Ford Coppola][/p?id=1299] lost 100 pounds while filming.
  • [Martin Sheen][/p?id=1620] had a heart attack during the filming and some shots of Willard's back are of doubles, including [Sheen's][/p?id=1620] brother who was flown out specially.   • Philippines President Ferdinand Marcos agreed to let his army supply the helicopters and pilots used in the film. The Marcos government was also fighting rebels in the area where filming was taking place, and sometimes withdrew the helicopters and pilots when they were needed in battle, replacing them with pilots who weren't familiar with the filming, which caused some problems.
