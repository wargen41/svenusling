  • To get the role of Michael [Myers][/p?id=1674], Dick [Warlock][/f?id=21885] revealed that as he passed by a room, the Michael [Myers][/p?id=1674] mask was on a table. He put the mask on and walked into [Rick Rosenthal's][/p?id=Rick%20Rosenthal] office and stood there. After [Rosenthal][/p?id=Rick%20Rosenthal] continuously asked him who he was and he didn't reply, he took off the mask and asked if he could play Michael [Myers][/p?id=1674].
  • Believing [Rick Rosenthal's][/p?id=Rick%20Rosenthal] version of the film to be too tame, [John Carpenter][/p?id=John%20Carpenter] shot a few gory scenes that were added into the film despite [Rosenthal's][/p?id=Rick%20Rosenthal] objections. This annoyed [Rosenthal][/p?id=Rick%20Rosenthal] because he had wanted the sequel to emulate the way the original avoided explicit violence and gore in favor of well-crafted suspense and terror. In fact, [Carpenter][/p?id=John%20Carpenter] had intended for "Halloween II" to do just that, but the success of the new wave of slasher films in 1979 and 1980 made him afraid that a film which was scary and R-rated but lacked bloodshed and nudity would do poorly at the box office, leading to the extra graphic material inclusions.
  • This is the only Halloween film to show the morning after the 31st, every other movie ends on Halloween night.
  • The film is set immediately after the first [Halloween][/f?id=10235] (1978). Since [Jamie Lee Curtis][/p?id=1428] had begun to wear a much shorter hairstyle in the 1980s, she had to wear a wig that matched her original hairstyle for the film.
  • [John Carpenter][/p?id=John%20Carpenter] and [Debra Hill][/p?id=Debra%20Hill] had no interest in making a sequel as they believed the original [Halloween][/f?id=10235] (1978) was a standalone movie. When the studio offered him to write the script and pay them more money ([Carpenter][/p?id=John%20Carpenter] states that to this day he saw very little earnings from the success of the original movie) he took the job so he could earn back what he believes was his owed pay. However, the script was not forming out as well as he thought, and he has personally stated that the only thing helping him through the screenplay process was a six pack of Budweiser every day which led to what he believes an inferior script and bad choices in the movie's story.
  • [John Carpenter][/p?id=John%20Carpenter] turned down an offer to direct, but remained involved with the production by writing the screenplay.
  • [Dana Carvey][/p?id=Dana%20Carvey], who has a brief cameo in this film, went on to star with [Mike Myers][/p?id=1674] in the two "[Wayne's World][/f?id=21901]" films.
  • [Tommy Lee Wallace][/p?id=Tommy%20Lee%20Wallace] was originally offered the opportunity to direct this film, but turned down said offer because he didn't like the script.
  • Debut feature film as a director for [Rick Rosenthal][/p?id=Rick%20Rosenthal].
