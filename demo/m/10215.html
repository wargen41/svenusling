Visste du?

  • Originally to be directed by [Walter Hill][/p?id=1986], but he pulled out and gave the job to [Ridley Scott][/p?id=1797].
  • [Veronica Cartwright][/p?id=Veronica%20Cartwright] was originally to play Ripley, but producers opted for [Sigourney Weaver][/p?id=1882].
  • All of the names of the main characters were changed by [Walter Hill][/p?id=1986] and David Giler during the revision of the original script by [Dan O'Bannon][/p?id=Dan%20O'Bannon] and Ronald Shusett. The script by [O'Bannon][/p?id=Dan%20O'Bannon] and Shusett also had a clause indicating that all of the characters are "unisex", meaning they could be cast with male or female actors. However, Shusett and [O'Bannon][/p?id=Dan%20O'Bannon] never thought of casting Ripley as a female character.
  • [Ridley Scott][/p?id=1797] is reportedly quoted as saying that originally he wanted a much darker ending. He planned on having the alien bite off Ripley's head in the escape shuttle, sit in her chair, and then start speaking with her voice in a message to Earth. Apparently, 20th Century Fox wasn't too pleased with such a dark ending.
  • The rumor that the cast, except for [John Hurt][/p?id=1488], did not know what would happen during the chestburster scene is partly true. The scene had been explained for them, but they did not know specifics. For instance, [Veronica Cartwright][/p?id=Veronica%20Cartwright] did not expect to be sprayed with blood.
  • 130 alien eggs were made for the egg chamber inside the downed spacecraft.
  • The chestbursting scene was filmed in one take with four cameras.
  • It was conceptual artist 'Ron Cobb (I)' who came up with the idea that the Alien should bleed acid. This came about when [Dan O'Bannon][/p?id=Dan%20O'Bannon] couldn't find a reason why the Nostromo crew just wouldn't shoot the Alien with a gun.
  • There is no dialog for the first 6 minutes.
