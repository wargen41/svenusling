Visste du?

  • Footage of the Saturn V was computer generated specifically for this film; no Saturn V stock footage was used.
  • The cast and crew flew between 500 and 600 parabolic arcs in NASA's KC-135 airplane (nicknamed the "Vomit Comet") to achieve real weightlessness. Each of the arcs got them 23 seconds of zero gravity. All of these flights were completed in 13 days. The actual KC135 used (NASA serial number N930NA) was decommissioned in 2000 after 27 years of service and is on display at Ellington Field.
  • Several items in the movie, including [Jim Lovell's][/p?id=Jim%20Lovell] jumpsuit and a coffee mug in mission control bear the mission patch for Apollo 8, the mission that took [Lovell][/p?id=Jim%20Lovell] to the moon for ten orbits a year and a half earlier.
  • At one point during the return flight there is a bang and nobody is very alarmed; it's just a "burst helium disk." This was actually a significant event, though an expected consequence of the situation. The helium disk served a protective function in the LM descent engine and, after it burst, they might no longer be able to restart that engine. A final course correction, not shown in the movie, had to be done using thrusters instead.
  • The role of Fred Haise ([Bill Paxton][/p?id=1083]) was first offered to [John Cusack][/p?id=1480] but was turned down.
  • [John Travolta][/p?id=1501] turned down the part of [Jim Lovell][/p?id=Jim%20Lovell].
  • [Brad Pitt][/p?id=1096] turned down an offer to star in this film and chose, instead, to star in Se7en (1995)
  • Several actors from the movie including: [Tom Hanks][/p?id=1950], [Bill Paxton][/p?id=1083], [Kevin Bacon][/p?id=1543], and [Gary Sinise][/p?id=1316] visited US Space and Rocket Center Space Camp program and worked on their simulators before production of the movie began to help them get a feel for what it would be like to work in zero gravity.
  • [Jim Lovell][/p?id=Jim%20Lovell] is actually left-handed, but [Tom Hanks][/p?id=1950] refused to write with his left hand for the movie.
  • [Tom Hanks][/p?id=1950] is too tall at 6'1" to be an astronaut. The maximum height for an astronaut is 6'0".
  • In interviews, the real [Jim Lovell][/p?id=Jim%20Lovell] had said that he thought [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544] looked a little bit like him, but [Costner][/p?id=1544] was never cast. When Brian Grazer and Imagine Entertainment got the rights to the script, [Ron Howard][/p?id=Ron%20Howard] signed on to direct and knowing that [Tom Hanks][/p?id=1950] was an Apollo/space buff, sent the script to him. They set a meeting and [Hanks][/p?id=1950] agreed to play [Jim Lovell][/p?id=Jim%20Lovell] during [Hanks'][/p?id=1950] and [Howard's][/p?id=Ron%20Howard] first meeting about the film.
  • [Ron Howard's][/p?id=Ron%20Howard] favorite film of his own.
  • The first film made with the co-operation of NASA.
