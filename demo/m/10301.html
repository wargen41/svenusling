  • [Edward Norton][/p?id=1266] was said to have re-edited the film to lengthen his screen time. Director [Tony Kaye][/p?id=Tony%20Kaye] then attempted to get his name removed but violated a Directors' Guild of America rule that states that directors that use pseudonyms (such as "[Alan Smithee][/p?id=Alan%20Smithee]") must not talk about why they had their name removed - which [Tony Kaye][/p?id=Tony%20Kaye] had done in ads in Variety. [Kaye][/p?id=Tony%20Kaye] sued the DGA and New Line Cinema for $200 million stating that the DGA rule violated his first amendment rights.
  • [Edward Norton][/p?id=1266] gained 30 pounds of muscle for his role.
  • Director [Tony Kaye][/p?id=Tony%20Kaye] wanted his credit to read "Humpty Dumpty" according to Entertainment Weekly.
  • In the midst of the dispute about the time he was taking to edit the film, Director [Tony Kaye][/p?id=Tony%20Kaye] attended a meeting with Michael De Luca (then New Line's senior product president). [Kaye][/p?id=Tony%20Kaye] arranged for a Rabbi, a Catholic priest and a Buddhist monk to be present at the meeting to support his argument and "make the meeting a more spiritual one".
  • [Marlon Brando][/p?id=1616] turned down [Stacy Keach's][/p?id=Stacy%20Keach] role.
