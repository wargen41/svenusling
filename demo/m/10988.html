Visste du?

  • Out of respect for [Paul Newman][/p?id=1727], who died in 2008, Doc Hudson Hornet from Bilar is not included in [Bilar 2][/f?id=10988]. An early scene implies that Hudson has died. However, Fillmore the VW van, once voiced by [George Carlin][/p?id=1324] who also died in 2008, appears here voiced by [Lloyd Sherr][/p?id=Lloyd%20Sherr].
  • The first [John Lasseter][/p?id=John%20Lasseter]-directed Pixar film not to have a score by [Randy Newman][/p?id=Randy%20Newman].
  • The name Miles Axlerod, as voiced by [Eddie Izzard][/p?id=1259], was taken from one of [Izzard's][/p?id=1259] stand-up routines about the invention of the wheel and axle.
  • [John Lasseter][/p?id=John%20Lasseter] conceived the story for [Cars 2][/f?id=10988] while he was in Europe promoting the first [Cars][/f?id=10987].
  • Finn McMissile was originally conceived for an unused scene in the first Bilar where Lightning McQueen and Sally were seeing a spy movie featuring McMissile while on a date.
  • During the race in England there is a sign on track wall that says "Lassetyre." This is a reference to the film's director/writer [John Lasseter][/p?id=John%20Lasseter].
  • When Mater is trying to drive away from McQueen while driving backwards, Darrell Cartrip mentions "He's got to be the best backwards driver in the world!" Mater had made this claim himself in the first [Cars][/f?id=10987] movie.
  • Finn McMissile's license plate - 314-FNMC - includes [Michael Caine's][/p?id=1643] birthday, March 14th.
  • The last Pixar film released before Steve Jobs' death.
  • The film was going to be directed by [Brad Lewis][/p?id=Brad%20Lewis], but due to problems during production, [John Lasseter][/p?id=John%20Lasseter] was brought in to direct while [Lewis][/p?id=Brad%20Lewis] was made the co-director.
  • As of 2012, this the only Pixar film to not receive an Academy Award nomination.
