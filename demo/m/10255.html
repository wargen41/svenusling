Visste du?

  • Graffiti on a wall in a sewer at the end of the film reads "Harry Lime Lives," a reference to the character from [The Third Man][/f?id=20743] (1949) who is chased through the sewer system of Vienna in that film.
  • The proceeds that [John Sayles][/p?id=John%20Sayles] earned from his screenwriting duties were used to finance Return of the Secaucus Seven (1980).
  • The shot of the swat team emerging from the sewers looked so real that people actually thought they were terrorists and some even called the police.
  • [Robert Forster][/p?id=1812] was recovering from a case of spinal meningitis when principal filming began.
  • As in the shooting of this film's main inspiration, [Jaws][/f?id=14407] (1975), the mechanical, giant alligator was often malfunctioning. In a few scenes, [Lewis Teague][/p?id=Lewis%20Teague] either shot around the alligator to build suspense or shot a regular alligator in miniature sets (which is plainly obvious in some shots).
  • [Robert Forster][/p?id=1812] improvised the jokes in regard to his receding hairline, which a delighted [John Sayles][/p?id=John%20Sayles] wrote into the script during shooting for the other characters.
  • [John Sayles][/p?id=John%20Sayles] read [Frank Ray Perilli's][/p?id=Frank%20Ray%20Perilli] script for the film once and then totally scrapped it. [Sayles][/p?id=John%20Sayles] said it was set in Milkwaukee and explained that the alligator grew gigantic because of beer from a beer factory going into the sewers.
  • The final cinema film of [Dean Jagger][/p?id=Dean%20Jagger].
  • The oft-malfunctioning animatronic alligator used in the film was later donated to the Miami Gators as a team mascot.
