Visste du?

  • Because of the patriotic nature of the script and the success of using [Top Gun][/f?id=20703] (1986) as recruitment material, the producers persuaded NASA to allow director [Michael Bay][/p?id=Michael%20Bay] and company to shoot in the normally restricted space agency. This included the neutral buoyancy lab, a 65-million-gallon, 40-foot-deep pool used to train astronauts for weightlessness and the use of two $10-million space suits. The crew was also allowed to shoot in the historic launch pad that went out of service after the Apollo 1 disaster, and parts of the movie were filmed at Edwards Air Force Base in California.
  • This was the first movie that the cast was allowed to use genuine NASA spacesuits. The cast are the only civilians to ever wear NASA spacesuits, which cost over $3 million each.
  • Director [Michael Bay][/p?id=Michael%20Bay] had the actors write their list of demands on the papers that [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] read from.
  • The film opened on [Liv Tyler's][/p?id=Liv%20Tyler] 21st birthday.
  • [Liv Tyler][/p?id=Liv%20Tyler] turned down the role of Grace Stamper twice before finally accepting.
  • Regarding the film's premise, [Ben Affleck][/p?id=1070] asked director [Michael Bay][/p?id=Michael%20Bay], "Wouldn't it be easier for NASA to train astronauts how to drill rather than training drillers to be astronauts?" [Bay][/p?id=Michael%20Bay] told [Affleck][/p?id=1070] to shut up.
  • [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] had a picture of his daughters in front of him to help him cry during his goodbye to Grace.
  • The film crew was also allowed to shoot sequences at the top of a real launch pad with an actual space shuttle docked to it. The only condition was that they not step into the shuttle itself. [Ben Affleck][/p?id=1070] admitted to stepping inside the orbiter for a brief moment before NASA technicians ordered him out of the spacecraft.
  • [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] came to the film after he decided a comedy he was filming called "Broadway Brawler" could not be salvaged and sought a way to exit the project. Disney's then-head Joe Roth worked out a deal where [Willis][/p?id=1117] would star in [Armageddon][/f?id=10463] and two future films for the studio, and in exchange Disney would absorb the failed project's costs as an advance against his initial salary. The two films [Willis][/p?id=1117] later made under this deal were [The Sixth Sense][/f?id=18486] (1999) and [Unbreakable][/f?id=21135] (2000).
