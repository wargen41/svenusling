Visste du?

  • [Diane Keaton's][/p?id=1242] real name is [Diane Hall][/p?id=Diane%20Hall] and her nickname is Annie.
  • The scene where Alvy and Annie are at their psychiatrists, which looks like a split screen scene, was actually shot simultaneously on one set with an adjoining wall.
  • Alvy's ([Woody Allen's][/p?id=2010]) sneezing into the cocaine was an unscripted accident. When previewed, the audience laughed so loud that director [Allen][/p?id=2010] decided to leave it in, and had to add footage to compensate for people missing the next few jokes from laughing too much.
  • The film's working title was "Anhedonia" - the inability to feel pleasure. United Artists fought against it (among other things, they were unable to come up with an ad campaign that explained the meaning of the word) and [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] compromised on naming the film after the central character three weeks before the film's premiere. Other titles suggested were "It Had to Be Jew", "A Rollercoaster Named Desire", and "Me and My Goy".
  • [Sigourney Weaver's][/p?id=1882] screen debut, in a speaking part as Alvy's date near the end of the movie.
  • According to [Tony Roberts][/p?id=Tony%20Roberts], in the scene where Rob picks Alvy up from jail, [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] was unaware that [Roberts][/p?id=Tony%20Roberts] was going to pull the green visor down on his coat. [Allen][/p?id=2010] ad-libbed the line "Are we driving through plutonium?" They shot a second take during which [Allen][/p?id=2010] changed the line to "Are we driving through a field of bees?" The first take is the one in the film.
  • Annie's outfits, which caused a brief fashion rage, were [Diane Keaton's][/p?id=1242] own clothes.
  • At 93 minutes, it is the second shortest film to win the Best Picture Oscar. The shortest film to win the Best Picture Oscar is Marty (1955) at 91 minutes.
  • The jokes that [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] tells in front of the audience at the University of Wisconsin and on The [Dick Cavett][/p?id=Dick%20Cavett] Show (1968) are from his stand-up comic days.
  • [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] originally envisioned this movie as a murder mystery, with a subplot about a romance. During script revisions, [Allen][/p?id=2010] decided to drop the murder plot, which he and Marshall Brickman later revitalized in [Manhattan Murder Mystery][/f?id=Manhattan%20Murder%20Mystery] (1993).
  • The first rough cut ran 2 hours and 20 minutes. Among the scenes later eliminated were: segments showing Alvy's former classmates in the present day; Alvy as a teenager; a scene in a junk-food restaurant (featuring [Danny Aiello][/p?id=1206]); extensive additional scenes featuring [Carol Kane][/p?id=Carol%20Kane], [Janet Margolin][/p?id=Janet%20Margolin], [Colleen Dewhurst][/p?id=Colleen%20Dewhurst] and [Shelley Duvall][/p?id=Shelley%20Duvall]; and a fantasy segment at Madison Square Garden featuring the New York Knicks competing against a team of five great philosophers. [Christopher Walken's][/p?id=1173] driving scene was also cut, but was restored a week before the film was completed. New material for the ending was filmed on three occasions, but most was discarded. The final montage was a late addition.
  • [Marshall McLuhan][/p?id=Marshall%20McLuhan] was not [Allen's][/p?id=2010] first choice. [Federico Fellini][/p?id=Federico%20Fellini] and [Luis Buñuel][/p?id=Luis%20Bu%C3%B1uel] were asked first.
  • Though frequently regarded as one of [Woody Allen's][/p?id=2010] best films, [Allen][/p?id=2010] himself doesn't think very highly of it, only being able to see how much it doesn't live up to what he really wanted it to be.
  • One scene cut from the film is a fantasy sequence of Annie and Alvy visiting hell. This scene was rewritten 20 years later for [Allen's][/p?id=2010] [Deconstructing Harry][/f?id=14466] (1997).
  • An early appearance by [Jeff Goldblum][/p?id=1443] playing the party guest who "forgot his mantra".
  • [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] and [Diane Keaton][/p?id=1242] had trouble keeping a straight face when working together. An example of the uncontrollable laughter between the two was the lobster dinner scene. It was the first scene shot for the movie and neither Woody nor Diane had to do much acting for the scene, for their laughter was completely spontaneous.
  • [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] originally filmed a scene in which a traffic advisory sign "urges" Alvy to go to Annie in California. Editor Ralph Rosenblum wrote that [Allen][/p?id=2010] was so disgusted by the scene's cuteness that he took the footage and threw it into the East River. The traffic-sign motif was later used in [Steve Martin's][/p?id=1903] [L.A. Story][/f?id=15604] (1991).
  • The movie theater line-up scene is done on a long take. The shot starts when the man behind Alvy starts speaking.
  • [Brooke Shields][/p?id=Brooke%20Shields] appeared in a deleted scene in which she played a schoolgirl crush of young Alvy's.
  • [Shelley Hack's][/p?id=Shelley%20Hack] film debut.
  • [Shelley Duvall][/p?id=Shelley%20Duvall] filmed her scenes in two days.
  • For a sequence set at Coney Island, [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] once kept a crew of 200 extras hanging around for an entire morning and never found the inspiration to shoot that day.
  • On Late Night with [Conan O'Brien][/p?id=Conan%20O'Brien] (1993) (28 February 1995), [Harvey Fierstein][/p?id=Harvey%20Fierstein] revealed that both he and [Danny Aiello][/p?id=1206] had bit parts in this classic, but their scenes ended up on the cutting room floor.
  • [Kay Lenz][/p?id=Kay%20Lenz] was offered the title role, but her boyfriend David Cassidy convinced her to turn it down.
  • The movie that Alvy and Annie originally intend to see, when Annie gets delayed, is Face to Face by [Ingmar Bergman][/p?id=1391]. [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] cites [Ingmar Bergman][/p?id=1391] as one of his main inspirations.
  • The budget was $3 million when production began and slowly swelled to $4 million.
  • [Diane Keaton][/p?id=1242] turned 31 and [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] turned 41 during the making of the film.
