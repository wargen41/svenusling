  • A running gag is the absence of an elevator to get to the newlyweds' 6th-floor apartment; new arrivals are out of breath throughout the film. For its release in France, however, the dubbed dialogue placed the apartment on the 9th floor (equivalent to the 10th floor in the US), since in France, older buildings with six stories and no elevator are not uncommon and audiences wouldn't have understood why climbing the stairs was so arduous.
  • [Robert Redford][/p?id=1821] loathed wearing a suit and tie all day which was required for his character. During breaks between filming, [Redford][/p?id=1821] wore western boots and a black cowboy hat.
  • When actor [Robert Redford][/p?id=1821] was offered the lead male role in the film, he was surprised. [Redford][/p?id=1821] had played the part on Broadway and did not like the idea of repeating himself. [Redford][/p?id=1821] believed that it was standard practice for players in Broadway productions to not be considered for parts they had created on the stage for the Hollywood movie version. However, [Redford][/p?id=1821] decided to do the film.
  • Neil Simon based this story on his first marriage.
  • First box-office hit movie of actor [Robert Redford][/p?id=1821].
  • [Robert Redford][/p?id=1821] had effectively given up acting prior to filming this.
