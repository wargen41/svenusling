  • [Vincent Cassel][/p?id=Vincent%20Cassel] has compared his character to George Balanchine, who co-founded New York City Ballet. The actor said Balanchine was "a control freak, a true artist using sexuality to direct his dancers".
  • [Meryl Streep][/p?id=1641] was considered to play Erica, Nina's mother.
  • [Blake Lively][/p?id=Blake%20Lively] auditioned for the role of Lily.
  • Both [Rachel Weisz][/p?id=1766] and [Jennifer Connelly][/p?id=1447] were rumored to star in the lead roles when the film was first discussed.
  • The production considered setting the film in France and filming in Budapest but the idea was dropped for creative and budgetary reasons.
  • The film began as a screenplay called "The Understudy" and took place in the world of New York theater. [Darren Aronofsky][/p?id=Darren%20Aronofsky] liked the script, but suggested it be changed to ballet.
  • The script took around ten years to make it to the screen.
  • [Mila Kunis][/p?id=1676] was brought into the project after co-star [Natalie Portman][/p?id=1687] suggested her to director [Darren Aronofsky][/p?id=Darren%20Aronofsky]. [Kunis][/p?id=1676] had a video chat with [Aronofsky][/p?id=Darren%20Aronofsky] via Skype and got the role without officially auditioning.
  • [Natalie Portman][/p?id=1687] lost 20 pounds to look more like a ballerina.
  • The budget on this film was so tight that when star [Natalie Portman][/p?id=1687] had a rib dislocated during a lift and she called the producer for help. She was told that the budget was so low they had no medic. She stated that if they needed to cut items from the budget they could take away her trailer, instead of the medic. The next day her trailer was gone.
  • [Winona Ryder][/p?id=Winona%20Ryder] spent 10 days filming her role.
  • After practicing with a ballet instructor for three months, five hours a day, seven days a week, [Mila Kunis][/p?id=1676] learned how to dance en pointe. She had casually practiced ballet as a child.
  • [Winona Ryder][/p?id=Winona%20Ryder] who plays the former leading ballerina, only went to one ballet lesson as a kid. She never returned as she thought her teacher was really mean.
  • The soundtrack, composed by Clint Mansell is a variation on Tchaikovsky's "Swan Lake" ballet, but played backwards and in a distorted manner.
  • According to an radio interview on "Fresh Air with Terry Gross," [Natalie Portman][/p?id=1687] recommended her friend [Mila Kunis][/p?id=1676] for the role of Lily after learning that [Kunis][/p?id=1676] had previously had some experience as a dancer.
  • [Natalie Portman][/p?id=1687] revealed that director [Darren Aronofsky][/p?id=Darren%20Aronofsky] would subtly try to pit her and [Mila Kunis][/p?id=1676] against each other during filming in an attempt to increase the on-screen tension between their characters. This included keeping the two actresses separated during filming and sending each of them intimidating text messages about each others performance that day.
  • [Natalie Portman][/p?id=1687] sustained a twisted rib during the filming of a dance sequence, and a full recovery took six weeks. During the filming of subsequent dance sequences, she was lifted from her arm pits rather than her sides to avoid repeating the injury.
  • Nina's cell phone ring tone is the Theme of the [Black Swan][/f?id=11028].
  • [Darren Aronofsky][/p?id=Darren%20Aronofsky] told journalist Kim Masters in a radio interview (KCRW's "The Business" broadcast February 14, 2011) that [Natalie Portman][/p?id=1687] not only trained for a year as a dancer to prepare for the role, but that she paid for the training out of her own pocket for about seven months until the film found investors. [Aronofsky][/p?id=Darren%20Aronofsky] attributed the fact the film's ever actually getting made to [Portman's][/p?id=1687] dedication and enthusiasm.
  • [Darren Aronofsky][/p?id=Darren%20Aronofsky] had originally hoped for a budget of about $28-30 million. The budget eventually raised for the film was about $13 million.
  • [Natalie Portman][/p?id=1687] stated after the experience she had working with director [Darren Aronofsky][/p?id=Darren%20Aronofsky] that she would work with him again at any point in the future.
  • Both [Mila Kunis][/p?id=1676] and [Natalie Portman][/p?id=1687] have praised choreographer [Benjamin Millepied][/p?id=Benjamin%20Millepied] for altering the choreography enough to allow them to do most of their own dancing and to help them appear like professionals.
  • [Natalie Portman][/p?id=1687] said in an interview with Charlie Rose that one of the most difficult challenges, aside from training and dancing, was her voice in the film. She stated director [Mike Nichols][/p?id=Mike%20Nichols] has given her consistent criticism on how childish her voice sounds and encouraged her to work with vocal coaches to make it sound deeper and more adult. The role of Nina required [Portman][/p?id=1687] to regress backwards to make her voice more child-like. She felt it was taking her back to a point she worked hard to leave behind.
  • The scenes between Nina and Erica were the last scenes filmed.
  • [Parker Posey][/p?id=Parker%20Posey] was strongly considered to play Beth Macintyre, but [Darren Aronofsky][/p?id=Darren%20Aronofsky] eventually decided to cast [Winona Ryder][/p?id=Winona%20Ryder] for the role.
