Visste du?

  • [Howard Hughes][/p?id=Howard%20Hughes] intended to show off the latest in aircraft technology in 1949-50 (when this film was shot). By the time it was finally released to the public in 1957 the aircraft featured were already obsolete.
  • Filmed between December 8, 1949 and February 8, 1950, this long held-back movie finally debuted on September 25, 1957 in Los Angeles, followed by its Manhattan opening at the Palace Theatre on October 4, 1957.
  • The lead actors fretted that the screenplay was "silly", with [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] only taking on the role because he thought it would make a political statement. He soon realized it would become "one of the worst films" he would ever make.
  • [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] would later recall, "The final budget was something like four million dollars. It was just too stupid for words."
  • The film was produced in 1950 by RKO, which was owned by [Howard Hughes][/p?id=Howard%20Hughes]. By the time it was released in 1957, [Hughes][/p?id=Howard%20Hughes] had sold RKO and the film was released by Universal-International.
  • [Richard Rober][/p?id=Richard%20Rober] (FBI Agent George Rivers) was killed in a car crash on May 26, 1952, more than five years before the film was released.
  • [Howard Hughes][/p?id=Howard%20Hughes] intended to make a "jet-age" [Hell's Angels][/f?id=17812] (1930) to the extent that the flying scenes were the most important element, and led to his obsessive re-editing that stretched into years.
  • The only colour movie directed by [Josef von Sternberg][/p?id=Josef%20von%20Sternberg].
  • [Howard Hughes'][/p?id=Howard%20Hughes] alternate choice for the lead role was [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134], but he turned it down due to scheduling conflicts.
  • [Howard Hughes][/p?id=Howard%20Hughes] briefly hired director [Peter Godfrey][/p?id=Peter%20Godfrey], then replaced him with [Josef von Sternberg][/p?id=Josef%20von%20Sternberg], who worked on the film until February 1950. [Robert Stevenson][/p?id=Robert%20Stevenson] was also considered to direct, and **Robert Sparks to produce.
