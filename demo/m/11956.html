Visste du?

  • In the theatrical trailer, Angel Eyes is "The Ugly" and Tuco "The Bad," which is the reverse of their designations in the actual film. This is because the Italian title translated into English is actually The Good, the Ugly, the Bad, not The Good, the Bad and the Ugly, and the Italian trailer had "The Ugly" and "The Bad" in that order. When the trailer was transferred to English, The Ugly and The Bad were not reversed to coincide with the altered title, causing the incorrect designations.
  • After [Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] agreed with [Sergio Leone][/p?id=Sergio%20Leone] that Tuco would carry his pistol on a lanyard, the director asked him to grasp the gun by shaking his neck, thus making the gun land in his hand. [Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] claimed that he wasn't able to do the intended action, and asked [Leone][/p?id=Sergio%20Leone] to demonstrate it. When [Leone][/p?id=Sergio%20Leone] tried, the pistol missed the director's hand and hit his crotch. [Leone][/p?id=Sergio%20Leone] then told [Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] to hold the gun in the belt.
  • Because [Sergio Leone][/p?id=Sergio%20Leone] spoke barely any English and [Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] spoke barely any Italian, the two communicated in French.
  • [Jack Elam][/p?id=1398] turned down the role of [Elam][/p?id=1398], the one-armed gunslinger who attempts to kill Tuco in the bathtub.
  • The three principal actors are the only ones who speak actual English in the film: [Clint Eastwood][/p?id=1184], [Lee Van Cleef][/p?id=Lee%20Van%20Cleef], [Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach], with the exceptions of [Al Mulock][/p?id=Al%20Mulock] (the one-armed man) and [John Bartha][/p?id=John%20Bartha] (the sheriff). Everyone else in the film is really speaking their native language, mostly Italian and Spanish, and was later dubbed into English.
  • During the scene right before the final duel where Tuco ([Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach]) is running frantically through the cemetery, a dog can be seen running on-screen at the beginning of the scene. In reality, that was improvised on the spot. [Sergio Leone][/p?id=Sergio%20Leone], who was afraid that the scene was going to slip into melodrama, released the dog without informing [Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] first - thus, his look of surprise is quite genuine.
  • Ennio Morricone's iconic theme music was designed in places to mimic the sound of crying hyena.
  • [Clint Eastwood][/p?id=1184] wore the same poncho through all three "Man with No Name" movies without replacement or cleaning.
  • [Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] would have been decapitated during the train scene if he had lifted his head up. In the wide-shot, you can see the step that would have impacted his head.
  • [Orson Welles][/p?id=1711] warned [Sergio Leone][/p?id=Sergio%20Leone] not to make this movie on the grounds that Civil War pictures were box office poison.
  • The trim on Confederate soldiers' uniforms identified the type of unit they were assigned to. Blue indicated infantry, gold cavalry and red artillery. Most of the soldiers in the prison camp wore historically accurate uniforms.
  • In the gun store, everything [Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] does with the guns is completely unscripted. Eli knew little about the guns, so he was instructed to do whatever he wanted.
  • Four scenes were cut from the original English-language release and were never dubbed into English from Italian. When American Movie Classics showed the "Extended English Version", the scenes were restored. [Clint Eastwood][/p?id=1184] and [Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] dubbed their voices for the movie, but another actor had to be found to dub Angel Eyes' lines, as [Lee Van Cleef][/p?id=Lee%20Van%20Cleef] had died in 1989.
  • There is no dialog for the first 10-1/2 minutes of the film.
  • [Charles Bronson][/p?id=1144] was offered both the roles of Tuco and Angel Eyes (the latter because [Sergio Leone][/p?id=Sergio%20Leone] feared that audiences would not take kindly to [Lee Van Cleef][/p?id=Lee%20Van%20Cleef] going from the fatherly, likable Col. Mortimer to a sneering villain. He declined both.
  • The film was budgeted at an expensive (for the time) $1.6 million.
  • Although [Clint Eastwood][/p?id=1184] is usually top-billed in this film's credits, [Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] has the most screen-time.
  • [Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] remembered that when he first came to Madrid all the hotels were full. [Clint Eastwood][/p?id=1184] invited him to sleep over at a friend's house and they shared the same bed. [Wallach's][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] wife [Anne Jackson][/p?id=Anne%20Jackson] told him he could boast that he was the only man to sleep with [Clint Eastwood][/p?id=1184].
  • Due to the striking height difference between [Clint Eastwood][/p?id=1184] and [Eli Wallach][/p?id=Eli%20Wallach] of over 9 inches, it was sometimes difficult to include them in the same frame.
