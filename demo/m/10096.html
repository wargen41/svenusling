Visste du?

  • During the helicopter sequence towards the end of the film, the inexperienced pilot flew too close to [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866], almost killing him.
  • The helicopter (carrying director [Terence Young][/p?id=Terence%20Young] during filming) crashed over water, trapping the director below the surface for a considerable time in an air bubble inside the copter's canopy. He was rescued and then immediately went back behind the camera with his arm in a sling.
  • The budget was $2,000,000 (double that of [Dr. No][/f?id=10095] (1962)).
  • When then President [John F. Kennedy][/p?id=John%20F.%20Kennedy] listed Ian Fleming''s book among his top ten favorite novels of all time, a list published in Life Magazine, March 17, 1961, the producers decided to make this the second James Bond movie. According to the book "Death of a President" (1964) by William Raymond Manchester, this was the last motion picture [John F. Kennedy][/p?id=John%20F.%20Kennedy] ever saw, on a private screening in the White House, November 20, 1963.
  • In this film, James Bond does not say "Bond, James Bond" despite the fact that he does say it in the book this film was based on.
  • "Q"/ Major Boothroyd played by [Desmond Llewelyn][/p?id=Desmond%20Llewelyn] appears for the first time. This character was played by [Peter Burton][/p?id=Peter%20Burton] in [Dr. No][/f?id=10095] (1962). When [Burton][/p?id=Peter%20Burton] was unable to return for this film, the role was recast with [Llewelyn][/p?id=Desmond%20Llewelyn] in the part. [Llewelyn][/p?id=Desmond%20Llewelyn] would reprise the role of "Q" in 16 subsequent Bond films (17 performances in all, but he didn't appear in Live and Let Die (1973). Q is referred to by his real name, "Major Boothroyd," only in [Dr. No][/f?id=10095] (1962), this movie, and [The Spy Who Loved Me][/f?id=22030] (1977).
  • James Bond doesn't appear until 17 minutes and 15 seconds into the movie. Red Grant's voice is first heard 1h 20m in.
  • Years earlier, [Alfred Hitchcock][/p?id=1021] was originally considered as director, with James Bond being played by [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] and [Grace Kelly][/p?id=1341] lured out of retirement to play Tatiana Romanova. These ideas were scrapped after [Vertigo ]/f?id=En%20Studie%20I%20Brott failed at the box office. The helicopter chase scene is a homage to [Hitchcock's][/p?id=1021] cropduster sequence in [North by Northwest][/f?id=15005] (1959).
  • Although he had his reservations about the choice of [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866] for the part of James Bond, after seeing him in this film writer Ian Fleming was completely won over by the actor.
  • Albert R. Broccoli once named this film along with [The Spy Who Loved Me][/f?id=22030] (1977) and [Goldfinger][/f?id=14302] (1964) as his three favorite James Bond movies, according to an interview with the Hollywood Reporter's Robert Osborne. [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866] considers this to be the best of the Bond films.
  • [Vladek Sheybal][/p?id=Vladek%20Sheybal] (Kronsteen the chess master) was a well-studied Polish actor hesitant to accept a role in a Bond film because he thought it might not be a good career move, but his friend [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866] persuaded him to sign on and it has helped his career enormously.
  • Much of the film was shot on location, with only a bare minimum of back projection used. Something quite unusual for a film of 1963.
  • [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866] said that this movie was his personal favorite out of the Bond films he did.
