  • The bulk of the picture was shot in Israel, and director [Samuel Fuller][/p?id=Samuel%20Fuller] remarked that it was unsettling after a scene was shot when the German soldiers and SS troops pull would take off their helmets and [Fuller][/p?id=Samuel%20Fuller] would see them wearing yarmulkes, and between takes they would be sitting around the set in full Nazi uniform speaking Hebrew or reading the Torah.
  • Reportedly [Samuel Fuller][/p?id=Samuel%20Fuller] wanted [Martin Scorsese][/p?id=1619] for the role of Pvt. Vinci, 1st Squad, but [Scorsese][/p?id=1619] moved on to do Raging Bull (1980).
  • During the course of filming, [Lee Marvin][/p?id=1572] and [Perry Lang][/p?id=Perry%20Lang] got into an argument. While filming the scene where they come to Crucifix Hill (where the Nazis are planning an ambush), [Lang][/p?id=Perry%20Lang] kept waving and gesturing his arms while he spoke. After the scene was finished, [Lee Marvin][/p?id=1572] growled at [Lang][/p?id=Perry%20Lang] "What the fuck was all that crap about?" To which [Perry Lang][/p?id=Perry%20Lang] cursed out [Marvin][/p?id=1572]. According to the other actors in the Reconstruction Documentary, neither [Marvin][/p?id=1572] nor [Lang][/p?id=Perry%20Lang] spoke to one another for the next week. Finally, [Marvin][/p?id=1572] began talking to [Lang][/p?id=Perry%20Lang] and treated him with respect for standing up to him.
  • Many critics felt that [Lee Marvin][/p?id=1572] was too old at 54 to play the sergeant.
  • Filmed in 1978.
  • [Samuel Fuller][/p?id=Samuel%20Fuller] wanted [Kris Kristofferson][/p?id=1558] for a part, but he had to turn it down due to some music work.
