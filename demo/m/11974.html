Visste du?

  • [Darryl F. Zanuck][/p?id=Darryl%20F.%20Zanuck] was quoted in an interview as saying that he didn't think much of actors forming their own production companies, citing The [Alamo (1960)][/f?id=Alamo%20(1960)], produced by [John Wayne][/p?id=1504], as a failure of such ventures. Wayne found out about this interview before being approached by Zanuck, and refused to appear in the film unless he was paid $250,000 for his role (when the other famous actors were being paid $25,000). Wayne got his requested salary.
  • [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866], who made his debut as James Bond also in 1962, acted in the movie along with [Gert Fröbe][/p?id=Gert%20Fr%C3%B6be] and [Curd Jürgens][/p?id=Curd%20J%C3%BCrgens] - two future Bond villains.
  • Former U.S. President Dwight D. Eisenhower was considered for the role of himself in the film, and he indicated his willingness. However, it was decided that makeup artists couldn't make him appear young enough to play his WWII self.
  • When cost overruns on [Cleopatra][/f?id=11561] (1963) threatened to force 20th Century Fox to shut down production of this film, [Darryl F. Zanuck][/p?id=Darryl%20F.%20Zanuck] flew to New York to save his project. After an impassioned speech to Fox's board, Zanuck regained control of the company he founded, ultimately finishing this picture and getting the production of [Cleopatra][/f?id=11561] (1963) under control.
  • The cameo part of the British Padre was first offered to [Dirk Bogarde][/p?id=Dirk%20Bogarde].
  • With a $10,000,000 budget, this was the most expensive black & white film ever made until [Schindler's List][/f?id=18009] (1993).
  • As would be done again later in the WWII epic, [Patton][/f?id=17390] (1970), the Twentieth-Century Fox logo is never shown onscreen in this film.
  • During the filming of the landings at Omaha Beach, the American soldiers appearing as extras didn't want to jump off the landing craft into the water because they thought it would be too cold. [Robert Mitchum][/p?id=1815], who played General Norm Cota, finally got disgusted with them and jumped in first, at which point the soldiers had no choice but to follow his example.
  • In Italy for the filming of [Cleopatra][/f?id=11561] (1963), [Roddy McDowall][/p?id=Roddy%20McDowall] became so frustrated with the numerous delays during its production, he begged [Darryl F. Zanuck][/p?id=Darryl%20F.%20Zanuck] for a part in this picture just so he could do some work. He ended up with a small role as an American soldier.
  • A number of sources credit [Christopher Lee][/p?id=1169] and [Geoffrey Bayldon][/p?id=Geoffrey%20Bayldon] as being in this project but Lee denies working on the film and Bayldon is nowhere to be seen in the final print.
  • In his memoirs [Christopher Lee][/p?id=1169] recalls being rejected for a role in the movie because he didn't look like a military man (he served in the RAF during WW2).
  • [Alec Guinness][/p?id=1018] was sought for a cameo.
