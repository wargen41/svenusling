Visste du?

  • The scene where [Roman Polanski][/p?id=1838] slits [Jack Nicholson's][/p?id=1402] nose was extremely complex to film, and the two men involved got so tired of explaining how it was done (by using a specially-constructed knife with a short hinge that would be safe as long as it was handled VERY carefully) that they began to claim Nicholson's nose was actually cut.
  • [Roman Polanski][/p?id=1838] eliminated Jake Gittes' voiceover narration, which was written in the script, and filmed the movie so that the audience discovered the clues at the same time Gittes did.
  • The first part of a planned trilogy written by [Robert Towne][/p?id=Robert%20Towne] about J.J. Gittes and L.A. The second part, The Two Jakes (1990), was directed by [Jack Nicholson][/p?id=1402] in 1990.
  • The role of Evelyn Mulwray was originally intended for the producer's wife, [Ali MacGraw][/p?id=Ali%20MacGraw], but she lost the role when she divorced him for [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904].
  • After [Ali MacGraw][/p?id=Ali%20MacGraw] was discarded, producer [Robert Evans][/p?id=Robert%20Evans] wanted [Jane Fonda][/p?id=1430] for the part of [Evelyn][/f?id=13506] Mulwray while [Roman Polanski][/p?id=1838] insisted upon [Julie Christie][/p?id=Julie%20Christie]. When Christie passed on the script, they settled for [Faye Dunaway][/p?id=1295].
  • After several takes that never looked quite right, [Faye Dunaway][/p?id=1295] told [Jack Nicholson][/p?id=1402] to actually slap her. He did, and the scene made it into the movie.
