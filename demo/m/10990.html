Visste du?

  • Bill and Ted playing a game with Death to win back their lives is a reference to the classic [Ingmar Bergman][/p?id=1391] film, [Det Sjunde Inseglet][/f?id=12058] (1957) (aka The Seventh Seal), in which a knight plays chess with Death for his life.
  • The mountain to which Bill and Ted are brought to be killed by the evil robots is the same mountain Captain Kirk climbs in "Arena", the episode of "[Star Trek][/f?id=19154]" (1966) that Bill and Ted watched in their apartment.
  • [William Sadler][/p?id=2007] ("Death"), also plays a bit role as an Englishman when we see various spots around the world when the Battle of the Bands is shown.
  • In the scene where Bill and Ted are addressing God, there are 2 statues at the base of the staircase. One is of [Michael Powell][/p?id=Michael%20Powell] and the other is of [David Niven][/p?id=1221], an homage to [Powell][/p?id=Michael%20Powell] and Emeric Pressburger's afterlife classic A Matter of Life and Death (1946). The stairway itself is the same as the one in that film, which has the USA title "Stairway to Heaven".
  • A third sequel was planned, but was canned after [Keanu Reeves][/p?id=1536], and later, [Alex Winter][/p?id=Alex%20Winter], declined to reprise their respective roles. The script, which had already been written, was instead modified slightly, re-casted, and recycled into [Bio-Dome][/f?id=11001] (1996).
  • [Joss Ackland][/p?id=1517] said in a Radio Times interview he only did this project because of a bet between him and a family member.
