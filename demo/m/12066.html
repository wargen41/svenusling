  • It took [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] six months to write the script.
  • [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] sent the script to [George Lucas][/p?id=George%20Lucas] and was concerned that he would be offended, but [Lucas][/p?id=George%20Lucas] called [Brooks][/p?id=1634] and gave him his trust after seeing [Det Våras För ][/f?id=Det%20V%C3%A5ras%20F%C3%B6r%20]Sheriffen[Blazing Saddles][/f?id=] (1974) and [Young Frankenstein][/f?id=12064] (1974).
  • [John Candy][/p?id=1475] ad-libbed the line, "Oh, that's gonna leave a mark," after standing up without undoing his seat belt.
  • The scene in which Dark Helmet ([Rick Moranis][/p?id=1795]) is playing with his dolls was not in the script. [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] came up with the idea on the set one day, and told it to [Moranis][/p?id=1795], who then improvised the entire scene, including the dialogue.
  • Of all the many jokes in this film, [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] says that the two he is most proud of are the running gag about merchandising ("Spaceballs: the Breakfast Cereal", "Spaceballs: The Tee-Shirt", etc.) and Col. Sandurz's renting Spaceballs (1987) before it was finished.
  • The ape who says "There goes the planet!" is voiced by an unbilled [Michael York][/p?id=1667].
  • Every time Dark Helmet has his face covered, his voice is lower and more basal, similar to [James Earl Jones][/p?id=1417] when he played Darth Vader. In the DVD commentary, [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] says that the idea of Dark Helmet's voice changing whenever his face was covered was actually [Rick Moranis's][/p?id=1795] idea.
  • [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] came up with the idea for this film when he discovered he had yet to spoof space movies, since he already destroyed the western films with [Det Våras För ][/f?id=Det%20V%C3%A5ras%20F%C3%B6r%20]Sheriffen[Blazing Saddles][/f?id=] (1974), made fun of the horror genre with [Young Frankenstein][/f?id=12064] (1974), and gave silent movies the ax with [Silent Movie][/f?id=12069] (1976).
  • The scene where the Megamaid sucks the atmosphere off the Druidian mountain is a parody of the Paramount Studios logo.
