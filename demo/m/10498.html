  • This movie is often considered merely another assignment of [Nicholas Ray's][/p?id=Nicholas%20Ray] at RKO for [Howard Hughes][/p?id=Howard%20Hughes] to prove his political and professional alliance during the Red Scare. A blatant pro-war movie that [Hughes][/p?id=Howard%20Hughes] cared about and [Ray][/p?id=Nicholas%20Ray] did not, [Ray][/p?id=Nicholas%20Ray] disagreed with the film's politics and is said to have, along with [Robert Ryan][/p?id=Robert%20Ryan], intentionally over-act. [Ryan][/p?id=Robert%20Ryan] and [Ray][/p?id=Nicholas%20Ray], who were leftist liberals, constantly fought against [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] and [Jay C. Flippen][/p?id=Jay%20C.%20Flippen], who were conservatives and supported the Blacklist.
  • There was some controversy over the casting, since both [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] and [Robert Ryan][/p?id=Robert%20Ryan] were clearly much older than real pilots were during World War II.
  • This was [Nicholas Ray's][/p?id=Nicholas%20Ray] first film in color.
  • Even though this film is in color, the RKO Studio's film logo seen at the beginning of the movie is in black-and-white.
  • [Robert Ryan][/p?id=Robert%20Ryan] was cast by director [Nicholas Ray][/p?id=Nicholas%20Ray] because he had been a boxer in college and believed that he was the only actor that could play opposite [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] and "kick [Wayne's][/p?id=1504] ass."
