Visste du?

  • In May 2002, became first documentary to compete in the Cannes Film Festival's main competition in 46 years.
  • After two years of trying to get an interview with [Charlton Heston][/p?id=1151], [Michael Moore][/p?id=Michael%20Moore] was going to give up. Showing up at [Heston's][/p?id=1151] house was totally spontaneous - on the way to the airport, a staff member suggested that they find his house using a star map.
  • Highest-grossing documentary until 2004 when [Fahrenheit 9/11][/f?id=13542] (2004) made more in its opening weekend than this movie did in its entire run.
  • Because the film didn't fit neatly into any established categories at Cannes, the Jury created a special 55th Anniversary Award just for the film.
  • At the Cannes film festival, the movie received a standing ovation that lasted 13 minutes.
  • In 2003 became the first documentary to be nominated for and then to win a WGA Award for its screenplay.
  • When the movie won the Best Documentary award at the Oscars, [Michael Moore][/p?id=Michael%20Moore] sparked a controversy by denouncing the [George W. Bush][/p?id=George%20W.%20Bush] administration on stage.
  • The first rough cut of the film ran about six hours.
  • After [Charlton Heston][/p?id=1151] abruptly ended the interview, [Michael Moore][/p?id=Michael%20Moore] and his film crew walked down [Heston's][/p?id=1151] driveway to find themselves temporarily trapped as the gate closed. Fearing that [Heston][/p?id=1151] may have called someone to try and confiscate the interview footage, [Moore][/p?id=Michael%20Moore] had his cameraman pass the film through the bars of the gate to staffers waiting outside. [Moore][/p?id=Michael%20Moore] then told the staffers to depart immediately with the film. [Moore][/p?id=Michael%20Moore] and his crew eventually left [Heston's][/p?id=1151] estate without incident.
  • The quote of [Charlton Heston][/p?id=1151] saying "From my cold dead hands!" was from the NRA convention in Charlotte, North Carolina, in spring 2000.
  • Contrary to popular belief, "South Park" (1997) creator [Trey Parker][/p?id=Trey%20Parker] and co-creator [Matt Stone][/p?id=Matt%20Stone] had nothing to do with the animated section of this film. [Stone][/p?id=Matt%20Stone] was enraged, believing that the impression that they had done the animated section was intentional. For that reason, [Michael Moore][/p?id=Michael%20Moore] was portrayed in an amusing negative fashion in [Parker][/p?id=Trey%20Parker] and [Stone's][/p?id=Matt%20Stone] film [Team America][/f?id=20059]: World Police (2004).
