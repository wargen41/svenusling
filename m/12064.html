  • The film was shot with many of the same props and lab equipment as the original [Frankenstein (1931)][/f?id=14027].
  • The howling wolf sound on the ride to the castle was made by director [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634].
  • The idea of Frederick's dart hitting a cat was ad-libbed on set. When [Gene Wilder][/p?id=1321] threw his dart off camera, director [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] quickly screamed like a cat to create the illusion.
  • The cast and especially [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] had so much fun and were so upset when principal photography was almost completed, that Mel added scenes to continue shooting.
  • The original cut of the movie was almost twice as long as the final cut, and it was considered by all involved to be an abysmal failure. It was only after a marathon cutting session that they produced the final cut of the film, which both [Gene Wilder][/p?id=1321] and [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] considered to be far superior to the original product. At one point they noted that for every joke that worked, there were three that fell flat. So they went in and trimmed all the jokes that didn't work.
  • [Gene Wilder's][/p?id=1321] favourite film of his own.
  • [Gene Wilder][/p?id=1321], [Peter Boyle][/p?id=1736] and [Marty Feldman][/p?id=Marty%20Feldman] appear together in this film by virtue of the fact that their mutual agent had a deal with the movie studio.
  • The Blind Man's parting line "I was gonna make espresso" was not in the script, but was ad-libbed by [Gene Hackman][/p?id=1317] during shooting. [Hackman][/p?id=1317] was uncredited when the movie was originally released in theaters.
  • [Gene Wilder][/p?id=1321] and [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] got into only one fight during the movie's production, but it was a big one with Mel throwing a huge temper tantrum, yelling and raging and eventually storming out of the studio. Shortly thereafter, Gene received a phone call from Mel, saying, "Who was that lunatic yelling and screaming on the set today? You should fire that bum!"
  • At the 1975 Golden Globe Awards, [Cloris Leachman][/p?id=Cloris%20Leachman] was nominated for Best Lead Actress in a Comedy/Musical, while [Madeline Kahn][/p?id=1599] was nominated for Best Supporting Actress for their work in this movie.
