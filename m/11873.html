Visste du?

  • [Robert Montgomery][/p?id=Robert%20Mont] was a real-life PT skipper in World War 2. He helped direct some of the PT sequences for the film when [John Ford][/p?id=1483] was unavailable due to health reasons.
  • This movie was based on William L. White's book "They Were Expendable", which covered the exploits of Lieutenants John Bulkeley and [Robert Kelly][/p?id=Robert%20Kelly]. Lt. [Robert Kelly][/p?id=Robert%20Kelly] and U.S. Army Nurse "Peggy Smith" sued MGM, [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] and [Donna Reed][/p?id=Donna%20Reed] for their portrayal of them in the film. Although the film follows the book fairly closely, it does portray Lt. [Kelly][/p?id=Robert%20Kelly] as impetuous and "hell bound for glory." Nurse Smith is shown romantically involved with Lt. [Kelly][/p?id=Robert%20Kelly]. [Wayne][/p?id=1504], [Reed][/p?id=Donna%20Reed] and MGM settled out of court for a nominal sum (less than $5,000.00).
  • The real-life Motor Torpedo Boat Squadron 3 in the Philippines at the beginning of World War II was equipped with six 77-foot Elco PT boats, all either lost in combat or destroyed to prevent capture. In the film, the Squadron 3 boats are represented by two 80-foot Elco and four 78-foot Huckins PT boats.
  • During the shooting of this movie, [John Ford][/p?id=1483] had put [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] down every chance he got, because [Wayne][/p?id=1504] had not enlisted to fight in World War II. [Ford][/p?id=1483] commanded a naval photographic unit during the war rising to the rank of captain and thought [Wayne][/p?id=1504] a coward for staying behind. After months of heaping insults on [Wayne's][/p?id=1504] head, costar [Robert Montgomery][/p?id=Robert%20Mont] finally approached the director and told him that if he was putting [Wayne][/p?id=1504] down for [Montgomery's][/p?id=Robert%20Mont] benefit ([Montgomery][/p?id=Robert%20Mont] had served in the war), that he needed to stop immediately. This brought the tough-as-nails director to tears and he stopped abusing [Wayne][/p?id=1504].
  • First war movie feature of [John Ford][/p?id=1483].
  • This is one of a handful of feature film movies that have featured the story of the World War II Battle of Bataan. They include [Bataan][/f?id=Bataanpatrullen] (1943), [So Proudly We Hail!][/f?id=Vi%20H%C3%A4lsa%20Livet] (1943); They Were Expendable[ (1945) and ][/f?id=%20(1945)%20and%20]Djungelpartisaner||Back to Bataan:: (1945).
