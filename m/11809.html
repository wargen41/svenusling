  • After seeing his performance in [Thank You For Smoking][/f?id=Thank%20You%20For%20Smoking] (2005), [Christopher Nolan][/p?id=1170] thought that [Aaron Eckhart][/p?id=Aaron%20Eckhart] would be perfect for the role of District Attorney [Harvey][/f?id=Harvey] Dent.
  • This is the first [Batman][/f?id=10692] feature film that doesn't incorporate the word "[Batman][/f?id=10692]" in its title.
  • Despite endless speculation on which actor had been chosen to portray The Joker, [Heath Ledger][/p?id=1366] had always been [Christopher Nolan's][/p?id=1170] one and only choice for the role. When asked the reason for this unexpected casting, [Nolan][/p?id=1170] simply replied, "Because he's fearless." He later said that the two had met up to discuss it before there was even a script for the film.
  • Even though [Christopher Nolan][/p?id=1170] offered her the part, [Katie Holmes][/p?id=Katie%20Holmes] decided not to reprise her role as Rachel Dawes. Instead, she opted to co-star with [Diane Keaton][/p?id=1242] and [Queen Latifah][/p?id=Queen%20Latifah] in [Mad Money][/f?id=Mad%20Money] (2008) that same year. [Isla Fisher][/p?id=Isla%20Fisher], [Emily Blunt][/p?id=1279] and [Rachel McAdams][/p?id=1765] were all considered for the role before [Maggie Gyllenhaal][/p?id=1600] stepped in.
  • In preparation for his role as The Joker, [Heath Ledger][/p?id=1366] hid away in a motel room for about six weeks. During this extended stay of seclusion, [Ledger][/p?id=1366] delved deep into the psychology of the character. He devoted himself to developing The Joker's every tic, namely the voice and that sadistic-sounding laugh (for the voice, [Ledger's][/p?id=1366] goal was to create a tone that didn't echo the work [Jack Nicholson][/p?id=1402] did in his 1989 performance as the Joker). [Ledger's][/p?id=1366] interpretation of The Joker's appearance was primarily based off of the chaotic, disheveled look of punk rocker Sid Vicious combined with the psychotic mannerisms of [Malcolm McDowell's][/p?id=1604] character, Alex De Large, from A [Clockwork Orange][/f?id=11568] (1971).
  • [Christopher Nolan][/p?id=1170] and his co-writers, Jonathan [Nolan][/p?id=1170] and [David S. Goyer][/p?id=David%20S.%20Goyer], made the decision very early on not to explore The Joker's origins. This was so the character could be presented as an "absolute".
  • It's Sir [Michael Caine's][/p?id=1643] opinion that [Heath Ledger][/p?id=1366] beat the odds and topped [Jack Nicholson's][/p?id=1402] Joker from [Batman][/f?id=10692] (1989): "Jack was like a clown figure, benign but wicked, maybe a killer old uncle. He could be funny and make you laugh. Heath's gone in a completely different direction to Jack, he's like a really scary psychopath. He's a lovely guy and his Joker is going to be a hell of a revelation in this picture." [Caine][/p?id=1643] bases this belief on a scene where the Joker pays a visit to Bruce Wayne's penthouse. He'd never met [Ledger][/p?id=1366] before, so when [Ledger][/p?id=1366] arrived and performed he gave [Caine][/p?id=1643] such a fright he forgot his lines.
  • [David S. Goyer][/p?id=David%20S.%20Goyer] and [Christopher Nolan][/p?id=1170] collaborated on the story of the film. The script itself was written by [Nolan][/p?id=1170] and his brother Jonathan [Nolan][/p?id=1170]. After watching [The Dark Knight][/f?id=11809], Goyer stated "I can't believe my name is on a movie this good".
  • Just as it was when filming [Batman Begins][/f?id=10694] (2005), director [Christopher Nolan][/p?id=1170] oversaw every shot himself as there was no second unit.
