  • The film was originally meant to be an adaptation of Elmore Leonard's novel "Bandits", to which [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] owns the film rights, but the producers felt it was too weak and brought in writer Harley Peyton to write a new script from scratch.
  • [Val Kilmer][/p?id=1973] was originally considered for the part of Joe, and [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] was originally going to play the part of Terry, but due to scheduling conflicts, [Kilmer][/p?id=1973] backed out, and [Willis][/p?id=1117] took over the part of Joe.
  • [Billy Bob Thornton][/p?id=1085] has a phobia of antique furniture in real life, and the producers wrote it in as part of his character.
  • Hans Zimmer was the only choice for the music composing. However, Zimmer was just finishing [Hannibal][/f?id=Hannibal] (2001) and [Pearl Harbor][/f?id=17400] (2001) at the time in London, and he already committed himself to [Black Hawk Down][/f?id=11018] (2001), which was recorded in the US. [Barry Levinson][/p?id=1066] said that he would move the film for a later release, waiting for the German composer, since they are good friends since [Rain Man][/f?id=17589] (1988). Zimmer politely refused it.
  • The movie is based on two actual people who used to rob banks the same way Joe and Terry did.
