  • Plans for a revival of the 1970s TV series date back to the late 1990s when original series star [Richard Hatch][/p?id=Richard%20Hatch] went so far as to produce a short film suggesting what an updated Galactica might look like. In 2000-01 plans to film a new Galactica movie were shelved at the last minute. This new version is described as a "reimagining" of the original story as opposed to a continuation and includes many obvious and subtle references to the 1978 series.
  • The characters of Starbuck and Boomer are female in this version; they were male in the original TV series. None of the characters in the original series had last or family names.
  • Roslin calls Apollo "Captain Apollo", a nod to the original series.
  • On the original "[Battlestar Galactica][/f?id=Battlestar%20Galactica]" (1978), Apollo, Starbuck and Boomer were the characters' real names. Here, they're their "call signs" (pilot nicknames).
  • During the first Viper/Cylon engagement, the lead pilot calls for his wingman, "Jolly". Jolly was one of the pilots on the original "[Battlestar Galactica][/f?id=Battlestar%20Galactica]" (1978).
  • The officer aboard the contact station at the beginning of the first episode is reading a file on the Cylons which features the design of a Cylon Centurion from the original series. The shuttle which transports him to the station also closely resembles the shuttle craft from the original series.
  • Commander Adama's final speech to his crew (including his repeated use of the phrase "So say we all!" to rally them to action) was largely improvised by [Edward James Olmos][/p?id=Edward%20James%20Olmos].
  • [Grace Park][/p?id=Grace%20Park] originally auditioned for both Dualla and Starbuck.
  • According to the DVD commentary, the agriculture ship (with all the plants) and the little girl was intended to reference the agro ship from the movie [Silent Running][/f?id=12013] (1972). However, reused footage from that movie was used in the original [Battlestar Galactica][/f?id=Battlestar%20Galactica] series to depict their agro ship, thus becoming a double homage.
  • When Roslin finds out that she has cancer, there are ships flying above the Doctors office. One is making a landing and is the '[Serenity'][/f?id=18242] from [Joss Whedon's][/p?id=Joss%20Whedon] "Firefly" (2002).
  • [Tricia Helfer][/p?id=Tricia%20Helfer] based her performance on [Daryl Hannah][/p?id=Daryl%20Hannah] in [Blade Runner][/f?id=11038] (1982). [Edward James Olmos][/p?id=Edward%20James%20Olmos] gave her a copy of the film to watch.
  • [Richard Hatch][/p?id=Richard%20Hatch] was offered the role of the doctor who delivers the bad news to Laura Roslin but declined the offer.
