Visste du?

  • Roy Chanslor's original novel was a serious western. The comedy elements were added for the film.
  • When filming the scene where Kid Shaleen takes a bath and dons his costume, director [Elliot Silverstein][/p?id=Elliot%20Silverstein] had all actions timed to the beat of a metronome, its pace increasing when Shaleen takes his guns. He planned to have the scene scored with Spanish guitars following this beat, but the producer was adamantly opposed to anything Spanish in a Western. In the end electric, guitars were used.
  • The film's horse trainer told [Elliot Silverstein][/p?id=Elliot%20Silverstein] that the scene where a horse leans against a wall with its front legs crossed could not be shot because horses don't cross their legs, then that it might be possible if he had a couple of days. [Silverstein][/p?id=Elliot%20Silverstein] invoked his rank as director and gave him an hour. The trainer plied the horse with sugar cubes while repeatedly pushing its leg into position, and they were able to get the shot.
  • [Kirk Douglas][/p?id=1554] turned down the role of Shaleen. [Jack Palance][/p?id=1403] desperately wanted the role but was never offered it.
  • [Ann-Margret][/p?id=Ann-Margret] was first choice for the title role but turned it down.
  • At his acceptance of the Oscar, [Lee Marvin][/p?id=1572] opened by saying, "Half of this probably belongs to a horse out in the Valley somewhere".
  • Like several other Columbia releases of the 1960s (i.e., [Bye Bye Birdie][/f?id=Bye%20Bye%20Birdie] (1963), [Strait-Jacket][/f?id=Strait-Jacket] (1964)), this film includes a spoof of the studio logo, in this case with the Torch Lady doffing her robes in animation and turning into a cartoon cowgirl.
