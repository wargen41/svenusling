Visste du?

  • [Myrna Loy][/p?id=Myrna%20Loy] turned down the role of Ellie Andrews because a recent film set on a bus had failed, and she didn't think that this one would succeed.
  • At the Miami bus station, friends of Peter Warne ([Clark Gable][/p?id=1178]) refer to him as "the King" - [Gable's][/p?id=1178] nickname in real life.
  • [Friz Freleng's][/p?id=Friz%20Freleng] unpublished memoirs mention that this was one of his favorite films, and that it contains at least three things upon which the character "Bugs Bunny" was based: - The character Oscar Shapely's ([Roscoe Karns][/p?id=Roscoe%20Karns]) personality - The manner in which Peter Warne ([Clark Gable][/p?id=1178]) was eating carrots and talking quickly at the same time - An imaginary character mentioned once to frighten Oscar Shapely named "Bugs Dooley." Other mentions of "Looney Tunes" characters from the film include Alexander Andrews ([Walter Connolly][/p?id=Walter%20Connolly]) and King Westley ([Jameson Thomas][/p?id=Jameson%20Thomas]) being the inspirations for Yosemite Sam and Pepé LePew, respectively.
  • [Robert Montgomery][/p?id=Robert%20Mont] turned down the male lead, saying the script was the worst thing he had ever read.
  • This was the first film to win the Oscar "grand slam" (Best Picture, Best Actor, Best Actress, Best Director and Screenplay).
  • When [Clark Gable][/p?id=1178] showed up for work on the first day, he reportedly said grimly, "Let's get this over with."
  • While shooting the scene where he undresses, [Clark Gable][/p?id=1178] had trouble removing his undershirt while keeping his humorous flow going and took too long. As a result the undershirt was abandoned altogether. It then became cool to not wear an undershirt which resulted in a large drop in undershirt sales around the country. [Legend][/f?id=15678] has it that in response, some underwear manufacturers tried to sue Columbia.
  • Is often credited as the very first screwball comedy.
  • [Clark Gable][/p?id=1178] gave his Oscar for It Happened One Night (1934) to a child who admired it, telling him it was the winning of the statue that had mattered, not owning it. The child returned the Oscar to the Gable family after Clark's death.
  • She was so convinced that she would lose the Oscar competition in 1935 to write-in nominee [Bette Davis][/p?id=Bette%20Davis], that [Claudette Colbert][/p?id=Claudette%20Colbert] decided not to attend the awards ceremony. When she, contrary to her belief, won, she was summoned from a train station to pick up her Oscar.
  • It is widely believed that MGM ensured their contracted star [Clark Gable][/p?id=1178] received the Best Actor Oscar to promote his career at the studio. Only three actors were nominated that year, and it was widely believed [Charles Laughton][/p?id=1147] would have easily won for his highly acclaimed performance as the tyrannical father in The Barretts of Wimpole Street (1934) had he received a nomination.
