Visste du?

  • The photo of a wife and child that one of the soldiers is looking at is actually a photo of [Eric Bana's][/p?id=Eric%20Bana] wife and child. The props department forgot to take a photo of a wife and child with them, so asked [Bana's][/p?id=Eric%20Bana] wife and child who were traveling with him if they could use a photo of them in the movie.
  • Disney passed on distributing this film because of its violent R rating. Its eventual distributor, [Revolution][/f?id=17686], is run by ex-Disney studios chairman Joe Roth.
  • This project was originally the idea of director [Simon West][/p?id=Simon%20West] who urged producer Jerry Bruckheimer to obtain the rights of the book with a view to directing it himself. However, [West][/p?id=Simon%20West] abandoned the project to direct [Lara Croft: Tomb Raider][/f?id=15640] (2001).
  • The movie opens with a quote from Plato: "Only the dead have seen an end to war." An earlier cut of the movie opened with a quote from TS Eliot: "All our ignorance brings us closer to death."
  • When [Orlando Bloom][/p?id=1710] auditioned for the role, he informed the casting directors that he knew what it was like to break his back (as he had done so only a couple of years before when climbing out on a drain pipe from a friend's flat). His character in this movie breaks his back after falling from the helicopter.
  • A large number of the actors who played American soldiers are actually from different countries. The list includes: [Ewan McGregor][/p?id=1292] (Scottish), '[Eric Bana'][/p?id=Eric%20Bana] (Australian), [Kim Coates][/p?id=Kim%20Coates] (Canadian), [Ioan Gruffudd][/p?id=1394] (Welsh), '[Ewen Bremner'][/p?id=Ewen%20Bremner] (Scottish), 'Jason Issacs' (English), [Zeljko Ivanek][/p?id=Zeljko%20Ivanek] (Slovenian), [Nikolaj Coster-Waldau][/p?id=Nikolaj%20Coster-Waldau] (Danish), [Tom Hardy][/p?id=Tom%20Hardy] (English), [Matthew Marsden][/p?id=Matthew%20Marsden] (English) and [Orlando Bloom][/p?id=1710] (English).
  • [Eric Bana's][/p?id=Eric%20Bana] US film debut. He found the experience to be an ultra-realistic one and said he frequently forgot that they were only making a movie.
  • [Josh Hartnett][/p?id=Josh%20Hartnett] was cast largely at the suggestion of Jerry Bruckheimer who had just worked with him on [Pearl Harbor][/f?id=17400] (2001). [Hartnett][/p?id=Josh%20Hartnett] was not overly keen on appearing in another blockbuster so soon after his film with [Michael Bay][/p?id=Michael%20Bay] but the strength of the material and the opportunity of working with [Ridley Scott][/p?id=1797] soon persuaded him otherwise.
  • The nickname given to the Somalis by the Rangers, "Skinnies," does not actually refer to the famine and rampant malnutrition in Somalia. It is the nickname given to an alien race in Robert Heinlein's novel, [Starship Troopers][/f?id=19149], which was a popular book passed around the battalion, and is on the required reading list at [West][/p?id=Simon%20West] Point. The Rangers felt that their culture was so strange, that they seemed to be from another planet. The "Skinnies" do not appear in the film version of [Starship Troopers][/f?id=19149] (1997).
