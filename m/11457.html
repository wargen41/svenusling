  • [Orson Welles][/p?id=1711] reportedly insisted on including magic tricks into his scenes, a possible source of the friction between him and [Peter Sellers][/p?id=1751].
  • In his book "[Woody Allen][/p?id=2010]: A Biography", John Baxter says that in addition to the commonly credited contributors to the movie's script, several more individuals also helped in the writing. They include [Allen][/p?id=2010] collaborator Mickey Rose, Frank Buxton, [Orson Welles][/p?id=1711], [Joseph McGrath][/p?id=Joseph%20McGrath], [John Huston][/p?id=1489], and former MGM studios head Dore Schary.
  • Cameos by [Frank Sinatra][/p?id=1302], [Sophia Loren][/p?id=1887], and [Barbra Streisand][/p?id=Barbra%20Streisand] were planned.
  • [Peter Sellers][/p?id=1751] and [Orson Welles][/p?id=1711] hated each other so much that the filming of the scene where both of them face each other across a gaming table actually took place on different days with a double standing in for one of the actors.
  • [Peter Sellers][/p?id=1751] often caused interruptions by leaving the set for days at a time.
  • The rift between [Orson Welles][/p?id=1711] and [Peter Sellers][/p?id=1751] was partly caused by the arrival on set of Princess Margaret, sister of the Queen. [Sellers][/p?id=1751] knew her of old and greeted her in an ostentatious manner to ensure all cast and crew noticed. However, the Princess walked straight past him and made a big fuss over [Welles][/p?id=1711]. Nonplussed, [Sellers][/p?id=1751] stormed off the set and refused to film with [Welles][/p?id=1711] again.
  • [Orson Welles][/p?id=1711] attributed the success of the film to a marketing strategy that featured a naked tattooed lady on the film's posters and print ads.
  • A carpet beater can be seen hanging from the side of [Orson Welles's][/p?id=1711] chair. This is a link to the original [Casino][/f?id=11456] Royale novel, in which Le Chiffre tortures Bond by thrashing his testicles with a carpet beater.
  • [Ian Hendry][/p?id=Ian%20Hendry] was cut out of this project. All that's left of his role is a dead body being got rid of.
  • According to interviews with director [Val Guest][/p?id=Val%20Guest], [Peter Sellers][/p?id=1751] became such a problem during the filming that the decision was made to fire him before he had finished all of his scenes. As a result, the end of the marching band torture scene was noticeably altered and [Sellers'][/p?id=1751] subsequent scenes were written out.
  • Apparently, when the majority of the stars were cast, most of them were not aware when they signed on that this was a comedy-spoof and not a straight James Bond movie.
  • The first film of [David Prowse][/p?id=1224].
  • Producer Charles K. Feldman originally intended to make the film as a co-production with official Bond series producers Harry Saltzman and Albert R. Broccoli. Saltzman and Broccoli had just co-produced the previous Bond film Thunderball (1965) with Kevin McClory, and did not want to do so again. United Artists supposedly offered Feldman $500,000 for the rights to "[Casino][/f?id=11456] Royale" in 1965 but the offer was rejected. Forced to produce the film on his own, Feldman approached [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866] to star as Bond. Unwilling to meet [Connery's][/p?id=1866] $1-million salary demand, Feldman decided to turn the film into a spoof, and cast [David Niven][/p?id=1221] as Bond instead. After the film went through numerous production problems and a spiraling budget, Feldman met [Connery][/p?id=1866] at a Hollywood party and reportedly told him it would have been cheaper to pay him the $1 million.
  • The scenes with [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] were shot in London. Producers delayed his final day of shooting so many times that out of frustration [Allen][/p?id=2010] left the set, went directly to Heathrow Airport and flew back to New York City without changing out of his costume.
  • According to [John Huston][/p?id=1489] the role of M was intended for [Robert Morley][/p?id=1816] but he was unavailable.
  • [John Bluthal][/p?id=John%20Bluthal] was to play more roles but [Peter Sellers][/p?id=1751] had him sacked.
