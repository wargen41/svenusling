Visste du?

  • [Marcel Marceau][/p?id=Marcel%20Marceau], the famous mime, has the only speaking line in this movie: "Non!" (when refusing a role in the silent film). As a result, the movie has been listed in the Guinesss Book Of World Records as having the fewest spoken lines of any sound movie.
  • One scene shows the skyline of New York City. The orchestra begins playing "San Francisco", and the music comes to a sudden and noisy halt. The orchestra then goes into "I'll Take Manhattan".
  • [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] claimed that he was able to get all of the big star cameos ([Burt Reynolds][/p?id=1121], [James Caan][/p?id=1412], [Liza Minnelli][/p?id=Liza%20Minnelli], [Paul Newman][/p?id=1727], etc.) for under $300 a day, far below there normal salaries. Though, of course, they didn't have to learn any lines.
  • The first of [Mel Brooks's][/p?id=1634] films in which he plays the lead role.
  • The cameo in this film was the last movie appearance for [Harry Ritz][/p?id=Harry%20Ritz], whom [Mel Brooks][/p?id=1634] described as "the funniest man on Earth" and one of his strongest comedic influences.
  • [Brooks][/p?id=1634] and his writers concoct a sight gag they loved, in which the customers at a seafood restaurant would be human-sized lobsters, who pick terrified humans out of an aquarium to be cooked for dinner. However, the gag bombed at sneak previews and was deleted.
  • According to [Carl Reiner's][/p?id=Carl%20Reiner] book, "My Anecdotal Life", he taught [Anne Bancroft][/p?id=1048] how to cross her eyes one at a time. She does this in her scene with [Marty Feldman][/p?id=Marty%20Feldman] and again during the credits.
