  • "Mahatma Kane Jeeves" (the pseudonym used by [W.C. Fields][/p?id=W.C.%20Fields] as screenwriter) is a play on words from stage plays of the era. "My hat, my cane, Jeeves!" And in fact, at the end of the film his butler does hand him his hat and his cane.
  • Universal's censors initially objected to [W.C. Fields'][/p?id=W.C.%20Fields] script and demanded many changes. Director [Edward F. Cline][/p?id=Edward%20F.%20Cline] suggested that [Fields][/p?id=W.C.%20Fields] should go ahead and film it their way, ignoring the censors' changes, and that the front office wouldn't notice the difference. They didn't.
  • The scene in which a motorcycle cop drives through a trench full of ditch-diggers and knocks them out of the trench was developed by [Buster Keaton][/p?id=Buster%20Keaton] and director [Edward F. Cline][/p?id=Edward%20F.%20Cline] for [Keaton's][/p?id=Buster%20Keaton] film [Sherlock Jr.][/f?id=Fart%2C%20Flickor%20Och%20Faror] (1924), which [Keaton][/p?id=Buster%20Keaton] and Cline co-directed. Cline recycled the gag for this film.
