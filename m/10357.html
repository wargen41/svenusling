Visste du?

  • The rights for Annie (1982) were sold in 1978 for $9.5 million (approximately $30 million in 2006 dollars), a record that still stands, breaking the record set when Warner Bros. paid $5.5 million for the screen rights to [My Fair Lady][/f?id=16556] (1964). Paramount Pictures was also involved in the bidding war, and they made [Popeye][/f?id=15396] (1980) to make up for being outbid.
  • The voice heard on Miss Hannigan's radio (saying, "I love you, I love you, I love you") just before the "Little Girls" number belongs to director [John Huston][/p?id=1489].
  • [John Huston's][/p?id=1489] first and last attempt at directing a movie musical.
  • [Carol Burnett][/p?id=Carol%20Burnett] had chin implant surgery after principal filming was completed, believing her work on the film was done. But after the surgery the cast was called back to do re-shoots of certain scenes, and the work done can be seen in the final film.
  • [Aileen Quinn's][/p?id=Aileen%20Quinn] red curly wig was so itchy that the property masters gave her a special comb with which to scratch her head.
  • [Drew Barrymore][/p?id=1252] auditioned for the role of Annie.
  • A young [Elizabeth Berkley][/p?id=Elizabeth%20Berkley] auditioned for the role of Annie but was told that she was too tall for the part.
  • [Bette Midler][/p?id=Bette%20Midler] was offered and declined the role of Miss Hannigan.
  • [Jack Nicholson][/p?id=1402] was originally signed to play Daddy Warbucks, but dropped out after the original producer departed the project.
  • [Tim Curry][/p?id=1938] claims to have based his performance as Rooster on a stagehand he knew while performing a play in New York City.
  • [Steve Martin][/p?id=1903] was offered the role of Rooster, but when he heard he would be working alongside [Bernadette Peters][/p?id=Bernadette%20Peters] he turned the role down. As they were breaking up at the time and [Steve][/p?id=1903] felt it would be too painful to work with her for several months.
