  • [Sylvester Stallone's][/p?id=1915] character is named James "Jimmy Bobo" Bonomo. In [Oscar][/f?id=17298] (1991), [Stallone's][/p?id=1915] character talked about his first job, working for James "Jumpin' Jimmy" Bonomo. In both movies, [Stallone][/p?id=1915] has a daughter named Lisa, and both films include hitmen as central characters.
  • [Thomas Jane][/p?id=Thomas%20Jane] was brought into the project by original director [Wayne Kramer][/p?id=Wayne%20Kramer]. When [Kramer][/p?id=Wayne%20Kramer] left the project, [Jane][/p?id=Thomas%20Jane] suggested [Sylvester Stallone][/p?id=1915] to hire [Walter Hill][/p?id=1986]. After [Hill][/p?id=1986] took over directorial duties, [Joel Silver][/p?id=Joel%20Silver] came on board the project and fired [Jane][/p?id=Thomas%20Jane] because he wanted an ethnic guy for the other lead role. [Sung Kang][/p?id=Sung%20Kang] was subsequently cast.
  • [Sylvester Stallone][/p?id=1915] and [Walter Hill][/p?id=1986] performed uncredited re-writes on the screenplay.
  • [Christian Slater's][/p?id=1164] first major role in a theatrical film since [Alone In The Dark][/f?id=10272] (2005).
  • This was [Sylvester Stallone's][/p?id=1915] worst opening weekend gross in 32 years, and his second-lowest opening weekend gross of all time.
  • [Walter Hill][/p?id=1986] told [Sylvester Stallone][/p?id=1915] "to play things more casually. I wanted him to play his natural personality as much as possible. He's a very engaging guy. I told him, "I'm not interested in you inventing a character as much as imagining yourself as character." He went right with that."
  • [Walter Hill][/p?id=1986] said he wanted to have fun with the genre: "We're not breaking new ground. We're trying to be entertaining within a format that's familiar. There's a kind of ice skating that goes on where you must let the audience know that you're not taking yourself too seriously. But at the same time, the jokes are funny but the bullets are real. The jeopardy has to be real. When it gets outlandish, there needs to be no drift into parody - self-parody, maybe inevitable for old directors".
  • Shortly before the film's release, many trade and industry publications published stories that the studio had taken the final cut away from director [Walter Hill][/p?id=1986] and they had given the film to [Sylvester Stallone][/p?id=1915] to make an all new cut. [Hill][/p?id=1986] and [Stallone][/p?id=1915] have both denied the story.
  • [Walter Hill][/p?id=1986] said the film would be called a "buddy movie" but that he made "anti-buddy movies".
