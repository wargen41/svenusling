  • Many real-life NASCAR drivers (including [Rusty Wallace][/p?id=Rusty%20Wallace]) appear in the film.
  • NASCAR driver Greg Sacks did most of [Tom Cruise's][/p?id=1949] stunt driving. [Cruise][/p?id=1949] wanted to do his own stunt driving, but wasn't allowed to for insurance reasons. The Chevrolets were prepared by Rick Hendrick's racing team, which later used some of the movie cars in real races. 35 cars were wrecked during filming.
  • [Tom Cruise][/p?id=1949] received a speeding ticket for doing 85 in a 55 mph zone while working on this movie.
  • The scene where Cole and Rowdy race rental cars on the beach shows birds scattering out of the way. The birds were lured onto the beach by birdseed, and in the first take most of them were run over.
  • It was during the filming of this movie that [Tom Cruise][/p?id=1949], then married to [Mimi Rogers][/p?id=Mimi%20Rogers], fell in love with [Nicole Kidman][/p?id=1697]. They were married later the same year.
  • [Robin Wright][/p?id=1829] was first suggested for the role of Claire Lewicki but was unavailable.
  • Production began without a finished script; scenes were often written the day of filming. During one driving sequence, [Tom Cruise][/p?id=1949] actually had to read his lines off cue cards attached to his windshield, which resulted in a minor car accident. For subsequent driving sequences, [Cruise][/p?id=1949] was fitted with a special earpiece to have lines fed to him.
  • Most of the cars used in this film were actually Chevrolets outfitted with special fiberglass bodies made to resemble stock cars. The vehicles routinely broke down from the strain of the racing or had their bodies greatly damaged. At one point, half the fleet was in the repair shop.
  • Producer [Don Simpson][/p?id=Don%20Simpson] originally intended to take a supporting role as a fellow driver in this film but his role was reduced to only one line.
  • [Tom Cruise][/p?id=1949] and [Robert Duvall's][/p?id=1810] characters are (very) loosely based on former driver Tim Richmond and his crew chief Harry Hyde. Richmond was known as an overnight sensation, and Hyde was the veteran crew chief. The scene where [Duvall's][/p?id=1810] character teaches [Cruise][/p?id=1949] about tire management is based on an actual incident between Hyde and Richmond, who died of AIDS complications the year before the film was released.
  • In an effort to give a more realistic atmosphere, professional racing broadcasters were brought in to play the broadcast reporters and track announcers. Key among these were members of ESPN's racing crew, including booth announcer [Bob Jenkins][/p?id=Bob%20Jenkins] and pit reporter Dr. [Jerry Punch][/p?id=Jerry%20Punch].
  • First feature film of [Margo Martindale][/p?id=Margo%20Martindale].
  • Reputedly [Tom Cruise][/p?id=1949] handpicked [Nicole Kidman][/p?id=1697] to be his love interest in the film after seeing her performance in [Dead Calm][/f?id=15837] (1989).
  • [Alison Doody][/p?id=Alison%20Doody], [Sarah Jessica Parker][/p?id=Sarah%20Jessica%20Parker], [Molly Ringwald][/p?id=Molly%20Ringwald], [Meg Ryan][/p?id=1633], [Kelly McGillis][/p?id=Kelly%20McGillis], [Brooke Shields][/p?id=Brooke%20Shields], [Michelle Pfeiffer][/p?id=1668], [Sharon Stone][/p?id=1874], [Kim Basinger][/p?id=Kim%20Basinger], [Jodie Foster][/p?id=1467] and [Ally Sheedy][/p?id=Ally%20Sheedy] all turned down the role of Claire Lewicki.
