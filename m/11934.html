  • Producer Sam Spiegel had acquired the property this film was based on in the 1950s and originally wanted [Marlon Brando][/p?id=1616] to play the role of Jason "Jake" Rogers and [Marilyn Monroe][/p?id=Marilyn%20Monroe] to play his lover, Anna Reeves. By the time production began in 1965, however, [Brando][/p?id=1616] was too old to play the role of the son, and took the part of Sheriff Calder instead. [Brando][/p?id=1616] was paid $750,000 and his production company Pennebaker was paid a fee of $130,000 ([Brando's][/p?id=1616] sister [Jocelyn Brando][/p?id=Jocelyn%20Brando] also was cast in the small role of Mrs. Briggs).
  • [Marlon Brando][/p?id=1616] did not like the part of Sheriff Calder, and complained that all he did in the picture was wander around. He began referring to himself as "The Old Lamplighter".
  • [Steve McQueen][/p?id=1904] was the producer's early choice for the role played by [Robert Redford][/p?id=1821].
  • The casting director rejected [Faye Dunaway][/p?id=1295], telling her she wasn't pretty enough for movies and should stick to theater.
  • [Robert Duvall][/p?id=1810] earned $30,000 for his performance in the film, his biggest paycheck at the time.
  • [Marlon Brando's][/p?id=1616] pronouncement of "Bubba's" name as "Bubber" was his own interpretation of the accent.
  • [Marlon Brando][/p?id=1616] would later cite the scene where he gets beaten up as an example of Method acting.
  • Producer Sam Spiegel brought Lillian Hellman into the project, but had her script rewritten. He then refused to show her a final cut before the film's release. He was also responsible for signing the then relatively unknown [Robert Redford][/p?id=1821] as Bubber and lighting cameraman Joseph LaShelle, who replaced Robert Surtees after he dropped out due to illness. The Academy Award-winning Lashelle and director [Arthur][/f?id=10470] Penn, who was not consulted by Spiegel, did not have a harmonious working relationship, which Penn thought hurt the film. Later, Spiegel did not allow Penn to edit the film himself, which Penn believed ruined the rhythm of the film, which he had wanted to suggest hysteria. The director has disowned the film. Released with great expectations due to all the talent involved, this film proved a critical and financial disappointment and signaled the beginning of the end of Spiegel's success as a film producer.
  • [Paul Williams][/p?id=Paul%20Williams] thought this movie would be his big break, but after working on the film for 3 months, he had just a small part.
  • [Jane Fonda][/p?id=1430] and [Robert Redford][/p?id=1821] enjoyed working with each other. They had a lot in common and [Redford's][/p?id=1821] humor helped [Fonda][/p?id=1430] relax on set.
