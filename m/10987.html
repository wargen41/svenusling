  • Instead of making the cars' headlights the eyes, as is done on most cartoons, the Pixar artists decided to put the eyes up on the windshield, because that made the characters more expressive. This idea was largely influenced by the Disney cartoon [Susie the Little Blue Coupe][/f?id=Bara%20En%20Utsliten%20Bil] (1952), one of director [John Lasseter's][/p?id=John%20Lasseter] favorite cartoons.
  • Lightning McQueen's original number was to be 57, director [John Lasseter's][/p?id=John%20Lasseter] birth year. It was later changed to 95 to represent the year that [Toy Story][/f?id=20712] (1995) was released. The car in the final film who has the number 57 (who wins the race in the first teaser) vaguely resembles McQueen, and is probably an earlier production design for that character.
  • The tires of Lightning McQueen are Buzzard models manufactured by Lightyear, a reference both to the real Goodyear "Eagle" tires used in NASCAR and character Buzz Lightyear from [John Lasseter's][/p?id=John%20Lasseter] [Toy Story][/f?id=20712] (1995) and [Toy Story 2][/f?id=20713] (1999).
  • If you look closely at one of the racing cars, it's white, has the Apple logo, and the number is 84. 1984 was the year Apple released the Macintosh, the computer that revolutionized Apple as a company. Pixar was previously owned by Steve Jobs, the CEO of Apple.
  • The original idea was about an electric car living in a gas-guzzling world.
  • [Joe Ranft's][/p?id=Joe%20Ranft] final film.
  • Though not credited as such, [Michael Keaton][/p?id=1655] is the voice of the security guard who yells "Hey, no press!" during the instant replay sequence after the first race.
  • This was the last feature film for [Paul Newman][/p?id=1727] before his death of lung cancer in 2008. It also turned out to be the highest-grossing movie of his career.
  • [John Lasseter's][/p?id=John%20Lasseter] first directorial effort since [Toy Story 2][/f?id=20713] (1999).
  • The final film made by Pixar before it was bought by Disney.
  • [Paul Newman][/p?id=1727] considered his performance as Doc to be the best he'd done since [The Verdict][/f?id=12833] (1982). [Newman][/p?id=1727] was known as a skilled racing driver, owner, and enthusiast.
  • The longest Pixar movie to date.
  • Unusually for a Pixar film, the lead marketing focused on the voice talent, namely [Owen Wilson][/p?id=1714] and [Paul Newman][/p?id=1727]. Unlike other animation companies, Pixar tends not to draw attention to which stars are providing the voices.
  • The release of the film was held up by Pixar boss Steve Jobs for a year as it acted as good leverage in his negotiations with Disney.
  • Every now and then, Lightning McQueen's tongue pops out when he's thinking about something. This is actually a characteristic of director [John Lasseter][/p?id=John%20Lasseter] which his animators gleefully incorporated into the film.
