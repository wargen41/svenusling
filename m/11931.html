  • [Hobbs][/p?id=Roy%20Hobbs] breaking the scoreboard clock with a home run was inspired by Bama Rowell of the Boston Braves doubling off the Ebbets Field scoreboard clock on May 30, 1946, showering Dixie Walker with glass. Though he'd been promised a free watch by Bulova for hitting the company's scoreboard sign, Rowell had to wait until 1987 to receive it.
  • While [Darren McGavin][/p?id=1211] had a major supporting role as the bookmaker Gus Sands, he received no credit. In the recent retrospective documentary on the Special Edition DVD of this movie, [Robert Prosky][/p?id=1820] (the Judge) claimed that [McGavin][/p?id=1211] was cast late in the picture, and would have received a lesser billing than the other stars. As a result, [McGavin][/p?id=1211] chose to go uncredited. [Prosky][/p?id=1820] noted that [McGavin][/p?id=1211] wound up "drawing more attention to himself" as a result.
  • Boston Red Sox great [Ted Williams'][/p?id=Ted%20Williams] single goal while playing baseball was for people to say, "There goes the greatest hitter who ever lived" (a sentiment echoed by [Roy Hobbs][/p?id=Roy%20Hobbs] in this movie). Like [Williams][/p?id=Ted%20Williams], [Hobbs][/p?id=Roy%20Hobbs] wears number 9 on his uniform, and [Williams][/p?id=Ted%20Williams] and [Hobbs][/p?id=Roy%20Hobbs] hit home runs in their last career at-bats. [Hobbs][/p?id=Roy%20Hobbs] was also an outfielder, like [Williams][/p?id=Ted%20Williams] was. According to Roger Angell of the New Yorker, Redford modeled his swing on [Williams'][/p?id=Ted%20Williams]. Angell added that Redford plays so authentically, "you want to sign him up".
  • Iris' ([Glenn Close's][/p?id=1336]) initial appearance at the ballpark was carefully presented to give her the appearance of a guardian angel. For filming purposes, they waited until a clear day when the setting sun would be just at the right spot in the background, so that it would shine through her translucent hat and make it appear as a halo around her head.
  • Author Bernard Malamud based his baseball tale on the story of [King ][/f?id=King%20]Arthur[[ and the Knights of the Round Table seeking the Holy Grail. The name "Roy" means "King", and Roy takes his bat, Wonder Boy, from the oak tree that was struck by lightning, just as ][/f?id=%3A%3A%20and%20the%20Knights%20of%20the%20Round%20Table%20seeking%20the%20Holy%20Grail.%20The%20name%20%22Roy%22%20means%20%22King%22%2C%20and%20Roy%20takes%20his%20bat%2C%20Wonder%20Boy%2C%20from%20the%20oak%20tree%20that%20was%20struck%20by%20lightning%2C%20just%20as%20]Arthur][/f?id=%20and%20the%20Knights%20of%20the%20Round%20Table%20seeking%20the%20Holy%20Grail.%20The%20name%20%22Roy%22%20means%20%22King%22%2C%20and%20Roy%20takes%20his%20bat%2C%20Wonder%20Boy%2C%20from%20the%20oak%20tree%20that%20was%20struck%20by%20lightning%2C%20just%20as%20%5D%5B%2Ff%3Fid%3D%253A%253A%2520and%2520the%2520Knights%2520of%2520the%2520Round%2520Table%2520seeking%2520the%2520Holy%2520Grail.%2520The%2520name%2520%2522Roy%2522%2520means%2520%2522King%2522%252C%2520and%2520Roy%2520takes%2520his%2520bat%252C%2520Wonder%2520Boy%252C%2520from%2520the%2520oak%2520tree%2520that%2520was%2520struck%2520by%2520lightning%252C%2520just%2520as%2520%5DArthur] pulls the sword from the stone. Pop Fischer is the wounded Fisher King, though in this story, it is Roy who has the wound that will not heal. There is the Lady Without Mercy, who gives [Hobbs][/p?id=Roy%20Hobbs] the wound. He rallies the "Knights" of the Round Table to be the best in the land. The original Malamud story has the tragic conclusion that better reflects La Morte d'[Arthur][/f?id=10470].
  • "Shoeless" [Joe Jackson][/p?id=Joe%20Jackson] was also an inspiration for the character of [Roy Hobbs][/p?id=Roy%20Hobbs], particularly [Jackson's][/p?id=Joe%20Jackson] connection to the Black Sox scandal. This can been seen when the Judge attempts to bribe Roy to throw the game. Also, like "Shoeless" Joe, [Hobbs][/p?id=Roy%20Hobbs] has a special name for his bat.
  • The second film released by TriStar Pictures. It was supposed to be the first, but they felt that baseball movies don't do well. So instead, [Where the Boys Are][/f?id=Beach%20Bunnies] (1984) was released first, in April, and this movie followed in May. Director [Barry Levinson][/p?id=1066] said this during an appearance on Costas at the Movies (2013).
  • When [Roy Hobbs][/p?id=Roy%20Hobbs] first joins the Knights, mid-season, the equipment manager declines to give him a uniform with number 11 on it, asserting that the number 11 is "bad luck", and [Hobbs][/p?id=Roy%20Hobbs] winds up with number 9. There is no specific curse or jinx baseball recognizes about the number 11, but the sixteenth century scholar, Petrus Bungus, said that the number 11 "has no connection with divine things, no ladder reaching up to things above, nor any merit." Rather, he concluded that the number 11 was stuck between the divine numbers 10 and 12, and therefore 11 was pure evil, and represented sinners.
