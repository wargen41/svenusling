Visste du?

  • [Blade II][/f?id=11033] (2002) director [Guillermo del Toro][/p?id=Guillermo%20del%20Toro] passed on the film when [Hellboy][/f?id=14499] (2004), his dream project, was greenlighted.
  • This marks [David S. Goyer's][/p?id=David%20S.%20Goyer] directorial debut for a major motion picture.
  • [Ryan Reynolds][/p?id=1849] gained 25 pounds of muscle for his role as Hannibal King.
  • [Colin Farrell][/p?id=Colin%20Farrell] was offered the role of Hannibal King, but turned it down.
  • An early idea of [David S. Goyer][/p?id=David%20S.%20Goyer] was to include not only Hannibal King, but a female character called Rachel [Van Helsing][/f?id=21273] from the Tomb of Dracula comics, but then he heard about the movie [Van Helsing][/f?id=21273] (2004) and decided against it. He ended up creating the character of Abigail Whistler, Whistler's daughter, in her place.
  • [Jessica Biel][/p?id=1455] inadvertently destroyed a camera, costing more than $300,000, when she fired an arrow directly into the camera's lens. She was directed to "aim for the camera", which had a Plexiglas shield in front of it to protect it, except for a small opening in front of the lens. [Biel][/p?id=1455] had perfected her archery skills while training for this role to such a degree that when she fired the arrow - at a distance of approximately 50 feet - at the camera, as she was directed, it went directly through the lens and into the camera itself, destroying it. The footage of the incident is included in the DVD extras.
  • The vampire "final Solution" in this film originally came from an idea in Blade (1998), the first film in the trilogy. In a deleted scene Deacon Frost shows Karen Jensen a prototype of harvesting human bodies. This can be found on the "Blade" DVD.
  • [Wesley Snipes][/p?id=1998] and '[Kris Kristofferson'][/p?id=1558], who at the time had become good friends after working on the two previous Blade-installments, were reportedly unhappy with this movie and with [David S. Goyer's][/p?id=David%20S.%20Goyer] script decisions. They felt that too many new characters were added to the universe, and that Blade did not need any sidekicks besides Whistler.
  • There was much on set drama between [Wesley Snipes][/p?id=1998] and director [David S. Goyer][/p?id=David%20S.%20Goyer] in regards to certain decisions made with the film. This is evident in the making of featurettes as [Wesley Snipes][/p?id=1998] has little to no participation.
