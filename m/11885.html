Visste du?

  • The scene where Sister Helen was pulled over is based on an incident that happened to [Helen Prejean][/p?id=Helen%20Prejean] during filming. She thought it was so funny that she asked to have it put into the film.
  • After being told that she would be played in the film by "a famous actress from [Thelma & Louise][/f?id=20335] (1991)", Sister [Helen Prejean][/p?id=Helen%20Prejean] was introduced to [Susan Sarandon][/p?id=1911] and said "Thank God, she's Louise."
  • The title "[Dead Man Walking][/f?id=11885]" is a slang term used by prison guards when escorting death row prisoners from their cells to the execution chambers.
  • When director [Tim Robbins][/p?id=1942] needed songs written for the film, he simply sent a cut of the film to several noted composers, among them [Bruce Springsteen][/p?id=Bruce%20Springsteen] and Steve Earle and asked for a song.
  • Because [Peter Sarsgaard][/p?id=1750] and [Missy Yager's][/p?id=Missy%20Yager] brutal rape scene was filmed in the mud, every time they did a new take they had to get up, get showered, get dressed, put new makeup on and do it again. It took all night to film.
  • In reality, Elmo Patrick Sonnier and [Robert Lee][/p?id=Robert%20Lee] Willie, both inmates on whom [Sean Penn's][/p?id=1868] character Matthew Poncelet is based on, were executed by the electric chair in Louisiana in 1984; by 1993, Louisiana switched that penalty to lethal injection as the most humane way to execute. Both [Tim Robbins][/p?id=1942] and [Helen Prejean][/p?id=Helen%20Prejean] opted to use lethal injection in the film instead of the electric chair because, according to [Prejean's][/p?id=Helen%20Prejean] interview, "We don't want to give people the moral (of the most humane death) out whereby people could say 'Oh well, we used to do electrocution but that's too barbaric so now we are humane and inject them'."
  • First cinema feature of [Peter Sarsgaard][/p?id=1750].
  • The film was very much a family affair for [Tim Robbins][/p?id=1942]. Aside from directing his long term companion [Susan Sarandon][/p?id=1911] to an Oscar, he also cast his father, [Gil Robbins][/p?id=Gil%20Robbins] (Bishop Norwich), mother [Mary Robbins][/p?id=Mary%20Robbins] (aide to the governor), sister [Adele Robbins][/p?id=Adele%20Robbins] (nurse), and son [Jack Henry Robbins][/p?id=Jack%20Henry%20Robbins] (opossum kid) and [Miles Robbins][/p?id=Miles%20Robbins] (boy in church). His brother, [David Robbins][/p?id=David%20Robb], composed the soundtrack.
