  • Because of [Rachel Roberts'][/p?id=Rachel%20Roberts] fragile psychological health, the producers had another actress on standby just in case [Roberts][/p?id=Rachel%20Roberts] became unwell to the point she could no longer work.
  • Final feature film of actress [Rachel Roberts][/p?id=Rachel%20Roberts].
  • [Sterling Hayden][/p?id=Sterling%20Hayden] was cast in the film at a salary of $250,000 ($50,00 per week for five weeks), but broke his contract to go to Yugoslavia to cover the death of Josip Broz Tito for "Rolling Stone" magazine on spec, with no money up front. He told [Tom Snyder][/p?id=Tom%20Snyder] in an interview on Tomorrow Coast to Coast (1973) that he never did finish the article.
  • Reportedly, Chinese-American protesters tried to prevent this movie from being made in San Francisco. They picketed locations where the film was shot then later demonstrated outside cinemas in the USA where the picture was playing. The opposition centered around the Charlie Chan character being considered a "racist stereotype", the opponents disapproving of the character's "Chop Suey pidgin English, fortune-cookie proverbs, and Uncle Tom bowing and obsequiousness".
  • One of actress [Michelle Pfeiffer's][/p?id=1668] early roles in a theatrical feature film.
  • The "Dragon Queen" character played by actress [Angie Dickinson][/p?id=1044] is only ever so-called "The Dragon Queen" and is never ever known by a personal name.
