Visste du?

  • Producer [Charles H. Schneer][/p?id=Charles%20H.%20Schneer] claimed that he chose this story after reading an article stating that Jules Verne's "Mysterious Island" was the most-looked-at book at public libraries.
  • Originally, there was going to be a scene featuring a man-eating plant, but it was never shot.
  • The armature for the crab is covered with the shell of a real crab instead of the usual latex. [Ray Harryhausen][/p?id=Ray%20Harryhausen] bought three crabs for the production, having one humanely killed by a museum employee as boiling it would have changed the color of the shell. The other two were used for close-ups of the crabs' mandibles, which would otherwise have required a huge amount of time to properly animate. These crabs subsequently served as a dinner entrée for [Harryhausen][/p?id=Ray%20Harryhausen] and producer [Charles H. Schneer][/p?id=Charles%20H.%20Schneer].
  • [Ray Harryhausen][/p?id=Ray%20Harryhausen] has related the story of watching a cut of the film with composer [Bernard Herrmann][/p?id=Bernard%20Herrmann]. In a sequence involving a giant bird, [Herrmann][/p?id=Bernard%20Herrmann] told [Harryhausen][/p?id=Ray%20Harryhausen] that he was going to score it with "Turkey in the Straw" (he was only kidding).
  • One of the factors that led to the green-lighting of this project was the huge success of a film with a similar story, [Swiss Family Robinson][/f?id=Skeppsbrott%20I%20S%C3%B6derhavet], that was made by Walt Disney.
