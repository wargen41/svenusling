Visste du?

  • The movie's original director was [Phillip Noyce][/p?id=Phillip%20Noyce]. He was allegedly fired due to creative differences with the film's producers, John McCallum and [Lee Robinson][/p?id=Lee%20Robinson], who replaced him with [Tim Burstall][/p?id=Tim%20Burstall]. According to the book "The Avocado Plantation" by David Stratton, [Noyce][/p?id=Phillip%20Noyce] "...was unhappy with the producers' choice for the lead actor, a second-rate Hollywood star called [John Phillip Law][/p?id=John%20Phillip%20Law]... Before production began, [Noyce][/p?id=Phillip%20Noyce] quarrelled with [Robinson][/p?id=Lee%20Robinson] and McCallum about his attitude to the material. He was fired, and [Burstall][/p?id=Tim%20Burstall] was brought into to replace him."
  • The cast including [Sam Neill][/p?id=1852], [Mel Gibson][/p?id=1635], [John Waters][/p?id=1503] and [Chris Haywood][/p?id=Chris%20Haywood] were hired by the film's original director [Phillip Noyce][/p?id=Phillip%20Noyce] and were not hired by the movie's replacement director, [Tim Burstall][/p?id=Tim%20Burstall], who came on board afterwards.
  • Replacement director [Tim Burstall][/p?id=Tim%20Burstall] re-wrote and re-worked original director [Phillip Noyce's][/p?id=Phillip%20Noyce] screenplay.
  • After this film was made, the movie's producers, John McCallum and [Lee Robinson][/p?id=Lee%20Robinson], went onto to make another Australian World War II war film called [The Highest Honor][/f?id=En%20officers%20%C3%A4ra] (1982) (aka Heroes of the Krait aka Southern Cross) which also was commercially unsuccessful.
  • According to the book "The Avocado Plantation" by David Stratton, the movie's original director, [Phillip Noyce][/p?id=Phillip%20Noyce] "... had wanted to make more than just a war film: he also wanted to explore aspects of colonialism."
  • The shooting of the movie during production was plagued by constant rain.
  • The movie found more popularity upon video cassette release in Australia than when released theatrically. The movie's eventual success is attributed to its casting of four prominent Australian actors, [Chris Haywood][/p?id=Chris%20Haywood], [John Waters][/p?id=1503] and two actors who were rising stars at the time, [Sam Neill][/p?id=1852] and [Mel Gibson][/p?id=1635]. [Sam Neill][/p?id=1852] had appeared as the lead in the American film, [The Final Conflict][/f?id=De%20sju%20knivarna%3A%20Omen%20III] (1981) whilst [Mel Gibson][/p?id=1635] was fast becoming a big Hollywood star. Ironically, the casting of [John Phillip Law][/p?id=John%20Phillip%20Law], an American actor, is not believed to have contributed much to the movie's success on video in Australia.
  • This is actually the third war film or TV production that [Mel Gibson][/p?id=1635] appeared in. [Gibson][/p?id=1635] had featured in the World War II Australian home front television soap "The Sullivans" (1976) and the World War I film [Gallipoli][/f?id=Gallipoli] (1981) prior to starring in this movie. He would later appear in such modern war or war-related movies as [Air America][/f?id=10100] (1990); [We Were Soldiers][/f?id=21905] (2002) and [The Year of Living Dangerously][/f?id=11339] (1982).
