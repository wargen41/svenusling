  • Alice Sebold, who wrote the novel, said that she preferred an unknown actress for the role of Susie Salmon. After she was cast for this film, the relatively unknown [Saoirse Ronan][/p?id=1858] was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actress for [Atonement][/f?id=F%C3%B6rsoning] (2007).
  • [Helen Hunt][/p?id=1368] was reportedly considered for the role of Abigail Salmon. Instead, [Rachel Weisz][/p?id=1766] accepted the role, and was the first actor cast in the film.
  • [Hugh Jackman][/p?id=1379] and [Rachel Weisz][/p?id=1766] were the first actors who got an offer to appear in the film, which would have been their second collaboration together (after [The Fountain][/f?id=14015] (2006)). However, while [Weisz][/p?id=1766] accepted her part, [Jackman][/p?id=1379] had to turn his down. [Ryan Gosling][/p?id=1848] was cast instead, although he would drop out as well, three days before the shooting started. Eventually, [Mark Wahlberg][/p?id=1614] got the part.
  • [Mark Wahlberg][/p?id=1614] replaced [Ryan Gosling][/p?id=1848] just days before shooting began. In preparation for the role, [Gosling][/p?id=1848] had gained 20 pounds and grew out a beard. However, he vacated the role due to creative differences. [Wahlberg][/p?id=1614], who had just completed shooting [The Happening][/f?id=14436] (2008), another production in Pennsylvania, became available just in time to accept [Gosling's][/p?id=1848] role.
  • Despite being a last-minute replacement, [Mark Wahlberg][/p?id=1614] was always one of [Peter Jackson's][/p?id=1744] first choices for the leading role.
  • [Saoirse Ronan][/p?id=1858] landed the role of Susie Salmon based on an audition tape she had sent in. They were so impressed by the tape that no meetings or further auditions were necessary before offering her the lead role in the film.
  • Lynne Ramsay was interested in directing the film before [Peter Jackson][/p?id=1744] acquired the rights to the novel.
  • For his role as George Harvey, [Stanley Tucci][/p?id=1892] had his skin lightened, his chest and arm hair dyed to match his blondish-brown comb-over wig, and wore false teeth to alter his jaw line. He also wore blue contact lenses and a lentil-filled fat suit to widened his girth. All topped off with square-frame eyeglasses, a fake mustache and sideburns.
  • The main reason '[Ryan Gosling'][/p?id=1848] quit his role as Jack before filming start was mainly because during read through sessions with [Peter Jackson][/p?id=1744] and the rest of the cast he felt that he was too young for the role, given he was 26, whereas Jack was supposed to be in his late 30s. Despite repeated assurances from [Jackson][/p?id=1744] that he can portray Jack under proper makeup, [Gosling][/p?id=1848] insisted that he could not portray the character well given of him being a method-actor and finally was let go.
  • This was one of the two films besides [Up In The Air][/f?id=21165] (2009/I) that Paramount used its remaining marketing budget of 2009 to promote both film and for marketing and for Academy Awards consideration. This forced the other studio film, [Shutter Island][/f?id=18335] (2010) to be postponed its release to February 2010.
  • In Alice Sebold's original novel, a disturbing rape scene is recounted in great detail, an experience Sebold had personally experienced herself as a young woman. Director [Peter Jackson][/p?id=1744] chose to omit this section of the book, feeling that the re-enactment of the ordeal would have, not just overwhelmed the film, but been too traumatic a sequence for the young [Saoirse Ronan][/p?id=1858] to endure.
