  • The Illinois license plate on [Dan Aykroyd's][/p?id=1196] car is BDR-529; this is the same plate that was on the Bluesmobile in The [Blues Brothers][/f?id=11076].
  • [Dan Aykroyd][/p?id=1196] married [Donna Dixon][/p?id=Donna%20Dixon] after working with her on this project.   • The end of the movie promises "[Doctor Detroit][/f?id=12186] II, the Wrath of Mom" which was in the works. In fact, [Dan Aykroyd][/p?id=1196] was writing the script. But when this movie became a box office dud the studio pulled the plug.
  • Film debut of [Lynn Whitfield][/p?id=Lynn%20Whitfield].
  • First movie that actor [Dan Aykroyd][/p?id=1196] made after his comedic buddy [John Belushi][/p?id=1472] had passed away.
  • Although listed by character name in the credits as Miss Debbylike and shown rather prominently in the classroom scene (seated behind [Peter Aykroyd][/p?id=Peter%20Aykroyd], directly in the shot), [Glenne Headly][/p?id=1339] has no speaking part in the movie.
  • One of a few pictures in the short-lived 1980s Hollywood cycle of cleancut-guy-becomes-pimp movies. The others include [Night Shift][/f?id=%C3%85rets%20Man] and [Risky Business][/f?id=14169].
  • The first picture in which [Dan Aykroyd][/p?id=1196] got top billing.
  • Mom's two main henchmen in the movie are named Johnny and [Carson][/p?id=Johnny%20Carson] as in [Johnny Carson][/p?id=Johnny%20Carson].
  • [James Brown][/p?id=James%20Brown] and [Dan Aykroyd][/p?id=1196] previously worked together on The [Blues Brothers][/f?id=11076]. [Doctor Detroit][/f?id=12186] was the second of three collaborations, [Blues Brothers 2000][/f?id=11077] being the other.
  • [James Brown][/p?id=James%20Brown] is called by his actual name during the film but in the closing credits is only billed as Bandleader.
  • Informally known as the movie that [Dan Aykroyd][/p?id=1196] starred in without [John Belushi][/p?id=1472].
  • [Fran Drescher][/p?id=Fran%20Drescher] has said in her autobiography "Enter Whining" that the movie was expected to be a box-office hit, but it wasn't, by the time the film finished its run it had only made US $ 10.8 million from its budget outlay of US $ 8 million.
