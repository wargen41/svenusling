  • The long shot when the Fiat Multipla is attacked by terrorists, with all the passengers inside, demanded a camera rig that could rotate within the car. They used a rig developed by Doggicam Systems, and controlled by a stunt driver. The single shot was shot in six takes over four locations, requiring a lot of transition work from the VFX-house Double Negative as it pans around inside the car. The cocktail, stunt driver and motorcycle (from the moment it hits the car), windshield, blood, and roof, were all computer generated with 3-D animation.
  • After seeing Charlie Hunnam's performance in [Cold Mountain][/f?id=%C3%85ter%20Till%20Cold%20Mountain], [Alfonso Cuarón][/p?id=Alfonso%20Cuar%C3%B3n] called him up and offered him the role of Patric.
  • [Michael Caine][/p?id=1643] based his performance on [John Lennon][/p?id=John%20Lennon].
  • Just before the car is attacked, Miriam peels an orange in the back seat. Just before the refugee camp falls into chaos, Kee and Marichka share orange slices over the baby. In films, oranges often represent impending danger or tragedy. This motif is also prominently featured in [The Godfather][/f?id=14369].
  • [Alfonso Cuarón][/p?id=Alfonso%20Cuar%C3%B3n] said in one interview that he wanted to shoot this film like [La battaglia di Algeri][/f?id=Slaget%20Om%20Alger] rather than [Blade Runner][/f?id=11038], almost like a documentary about something that happened back in 2024.
  • After screenwriter Paul Chart wrote the first version of the screenplay, it was re-written a few times by other screenwriters including Mark Fergus, Hawk Ostby, Timothy J. Sexton and director [Alfonso Cuarón][/p?id=Alfonso%20Cuar%C3%B3n] himself. Finally the final version was written by David Arata, [Alfonso Cuarón][/p?id=Alfonso%20Cuar%C3%B3n] and Timothy J. Sexton. [Clive Owen's][/p?id=Clive%20Owen] insights greatly impressed [Cuarón][/p?id=Alfonso%20Cuar%C3%B3n] and he contributed a great deal of uncredited work to the final script.
  • In the movie it is women who have become infertile, while in the book it was men.
  • [Clive Owen's][/p?id=Clive%20Owen] character, Theo, does not use or even touch a gun at any point throughout the entire movie.
