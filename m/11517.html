  • Seven studios rejected the original screenplay. [Peter Stone][/p?id=Peter%20Stone] turned it into a novel which was serialized in Redbook, and it was then turned back into a screenplay - which had interest from all 7 studios.
  • In the scene where [Audrey Hepburn][/p?id=1064] is smoking a cigarette alone in her empty apartment and [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] enters, the backs of his ears had to be covered with masking tape since the backlighting made them appear red.
  • [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134], who celebrated his 59th birthday during filming, decided it was time to stop playing the romantic lead after reviews focused on the 26-year age difference between him and [Audrey Hepburn][/p?id=1064], who was only 33 when the movie was made.
  • The character played by [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] quotes a line from [My Fair Lady][/f?id=16556] ("On the street where you live"), the film version of which would star [Audrey Hepburn][/p?id=1064] the following year.
  • After finishing this film, [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] was quoted as saying, "All I want for Christmas is to make another movie with [Audrey Hepburn][/p?id=1064]," Although this sadly never happened, [Grant][/p?id=1134] was offered the role of Higgins in [My Fair Lady][/f?id=16556] (1964), and requested [Hepburn][/p?id=1064] as his co-star in [Father Goose][/f?id=Stora%20Vargen%20Anropar] (1964).
  • [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] initially turned down the film, after which it was briefly considered a possible vehicle for [Warren Beatty][/p?id=1991] and [Natalie Wood][/p?id=Natalie%20Wood].
  • [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] initially turned down the movie because he felt he would be too much of a predator pursuing the much-younger [Audrey Hepburn][/p?id=1064]. In a last-ditch attempt to sign [Grant][/p?id=1134], [Peter Stone][/p?id=Peter%20Stone] worked all night on the script and presented it to [Grant][/p?id=1134] to look over just once more. [Grant][/p?id=1134] gleefully accepted the role, prompting producers to demand to know what [Stone][/p?id=Peter%20Stone] had done. [Stone][/p?id=Peter%20Stone] had simply moved all of the romantically aggressive lines from [Grant's][/p?id=1134] character to [Audrey Hepburn's][/p?id=1064], making her the predator.
  • It was agreed that [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] would not remove his shirt in the shower scene since he was nearly sixty and slightly overweight.
  • Due to the suspense, the stars, and the frequent plot twists, many people believe that this is an [Alfred Hitchcock][/p?id=1021] film. He was not involved in the making of the film at all. This confusion has prompted fans of the film to call it "the best [Hitchcock][/p?id=1021] film that [Hitchcock][/p?id=1021] never made".
  • While much is/has been made about the age difference between the [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134]/[Audrey Hepburn][/p?id=1064] characters, If one does the math, (her husband - who never appears in the film - had some rank in the Second World War - nearly 20 years earlier), and looks at the characters who played her husband's contemporaries, it is clear that [Hepburn's][/p?id=1064] character had a thing for older men.
