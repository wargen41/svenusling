  • The scene in which Gruber and McClane meet was inserted in to the script after [Alan Rickman][/p?id=1012] (Hans Gruber) was found to be proficient at mimicking American accents. The filmmakers had been looking for a way to have the two characters meet prior to the climax and capitalized on [Rickman's][/p?id=1012] talent.
  • The original poster for the film did not feature [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] likeness, just the building. The producers originally thought it might deter non-[Willis][/p?id=1117] fans from seeing the movie. Posters were later altered after the early box office success.
  • [Alan Rickman's][/p?id=1012] feature film debut.
  • [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] received a then unheard of $5 million, a fee that was OK'd by Rupert Murdoch. Bruce and [Demi Moore][/p?id=1235] tied the knot at the Golden Nugget hotel in Las Vegas during this shoot, [Moore][/p?id=1235] having recently broken her engagement to actor [Emilio Estevez][/p?id=1278]. [Little Richard][/p?id=Little%20Richard] presided over the ceremony and former brat packer [Ally Sheedy][/p?id=Ally%20Sheedy] was a bridesmaid.
  • [Richard Gere][/p?id=1787] was considered for the role of John McClane.
  • [Don Johnson][/p?id=Don%20Johnson] and [Richard Dean Anderson][/p?id=1784], both established action stars in television, were considered for the role of John McClane.
  • The scene where McClane falls down a shaft was a mistake by the stuntman, who was supposed to grab the first vent, as it originally was planned. He slipped and continued to fall, but the shot was used anyway; it was edited together with one where McClane grabs the next vent down as he falls.
  • Director [John McTiernan][/p?id=John%20McTiernan] found it necessary to smash cut away from Hans Gruber's face whenever he fired a gun, because of [Alan Rickman's][/p?id=1012] uncontrollable habit of flinching from the noise and muzzle flash. If you look at [Rickman's][/p?id=1012] face when he shoots Takagi, you can see him wincing.
  • It is often said that [Bruce Willis's][/p?id=1117] lines during the scene when he pulls the glass out of his feet were ad-libbed. Indeed, it is said that upon learning this, [Terry Gilliam][/p?id=1926] cast [Willis][/p?id=1117] as the lead in [Twelve Monkeys][/f?id=20685] (1995). However when comparing the original script, it appears that [Willis][/p?id=1117] only veered very slightly from the original written dialog.
  • [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] was the sixth choice for the main character. It originally went to [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062], then [Sylvester Stallone][/p?id=1915], then [Burt Reynolds][/p?id=1121], then [Richard Gere][/p?id=1787], then [Harrison Ford][/p?id=1358], then [Mel Gibson][/p?id=1635] before [Willis][/p?id=1117] got it.
  • [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] personally recommended [Bonnie Bedelia][/p?id=Bonnie%20Bedelia] for the role of his estranged wife.
  • The scene where [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] and [Alan Rickman][/p?id=1012] meet up was unrehearsed to create a greater feeling of spontaneity between the two actors.
