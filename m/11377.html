  • The movie was filmed on location in North Carolina in October and November, 1987, which is why the grass had to be touched up with green paint. It is also why the breath of the actors can be seen in many of the night scenes.
  • [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544] (Crash Davis) is a switch hitter; he is shown hitting both left and right-handed at different points in the film.
  • In the scene where the bat boy tells Crash Davis "Get a hit, Crash", [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544] ad-libbed the response of "Shut up." Since the kid actor playing the bat boy obviously didn't know this response was coming, he started crying.
  • [Kurt Russell][/p?id=1562] helped [Ron Shelton][/p?id=Ron%20Shelton] develop the script and was originally penciled in to play Crash, the part that went to [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544]. After the film was made, [Russell][/p?id=1562] was so impressed, he actually wrote fan letters to [Costner][/p?id=1544] and [Shelton][/p?id=Ron%20Shelton].
  • [Ron Shelton][/p?id=Ron%20Shelton] was a former minor league baseball player and used his experience as the basis for the story.
  • [Kurt Russell][/p?id=1562], who helped [Ron Shelton][/p?id=Ron%20Shelton] develop the script, also played minor league baseball in the early-1970s.
  • [Ron Shelton][/p?id=Ron%20Shelton] reportedly based the character of Nuke LaLoosh on a minor league teammate from his playing days named Steve Dalkowski. Dalkowski is something of a legend among baseball fans and is widely regarded as the fastest throwing pitcher ever. Unlike the character in the film, Dalkowski never made the major leagues.
  • [Tim Robbins][/p?id=1942] and [Susan Sarandon][/p?id=1911] first met during the filming of this movie.
  • Real-life couple [Susan Sarandon][/p?id=1911] and [Tim Robbins][/p?id=1942] have stated that of all the films they have made during their respective careers, this remains their personal favorite.
  • There really was a ballplayer named "Crash" Davis. [Ron Shelton][/p?id=Ron%20Shelton] found his name in a baseball listing as a minor leaguer and American Legion player. Realizing that he would have to have the permission of the real Davis to use his name (and thus avoid a lawsuit). When [Shelton][/p?id=Ron%20Shelton] approached Davis, he was asked "Do I (Meaning [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544]) get the girl in the end?" [Shelton][/p?id=Ron%20Shelton] told him he does and Davis signed off his permission.
  • Although [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544] plays the 'older' more experienced ball player, in real life he's only three years older than [Tim Robbins][/p?id=1942] (33 and 30 respectively during filming), and [Susan Sarandon][/p?id=1911] was actually in her early forties (42).
  • [Kay Lenz][/p?id=Kay%20Lenz] was originally offered the female lead role but turned it down.
  • [Kim Basinger][/p?id=Kim%20Basinger], [Mary Steenburgen][/p?id=Mary%20Steenburgen] and [Pamela Stephenson][/p?id=Pamela%20Stephenson] auditioned for the role of Annie.
  • [Harrison Ford][/p?id=1358] was considered for the male lead role.
  • [Cybill Shepherd][/p?id=Cybill%20Shepherd] and [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117] were considered to play the parts of Annie and Crash but declined due to their commitment to the TV series "[Moonlighting][/f?id=Par%20I%20Brott]" (1985).
  • Both [Carrie Fisher][/p?id=1131] and [Debra Winger][/p?id=Debra%20Winger] auditioned for the role of Annie but backed out.
  • [Glenn Close][/p?id=1336] auditioned for the role of Annie but was forced to turn the role down due to her commitment to the film [Dangerous Liaisons][/f?id=Farligt%20Beg%C3%A4r] (1988).
  • Both [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441] and [Don Johnson][/p?id=Don%20Johnson] turned down the role of 'Crash'.
  • [Nick Nolte][/p?id=1693], one of the biggest male stars at the time, turned down the lead because he had just finished several movies back-to-back, and, most importantly, because he isn't a fan of baseball.
  • [Tom Berenger][/p?id=1948] was considered to play Crash. He later starred in [Major League][/f?id=21796] (1989).
