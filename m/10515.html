  • [Edward Norton][/p?id=1266] was originally set to reprise his role from [The Incredible Hulk][/f?id=15058] but negotiations between him and Marvel Studios broke down. [Norton][/p?id=1266] was replaced with [Mark Ruffalo][/p?id=1611].
  • The first Marvel film to be distributed by Walt Disney Pictures.
  • Before [Mark Ruffalo][/p?id=1611] was cast as The Hulk, [Joaquin Phoenix][/p?id=1466] was rumored for the part.
  • [Morena Baccarin][/p?id=Morena%20Baccarin], [Jessica Lucas][/p?id=Jessica%20Lucas], [Mary Elizabeth Winstead][/p?id=1623] and [Cobie Smulders][/p?id=Cobie%20Smulders] screen tested for the role of Agent Maria Hill. [Smulders][/p?id=Cobie%20Smulders] was eventually cast.
  • According to [Joss Whedon][/p?id=Joss%20Whedon], the film is strongly influenced by the early 1960s Avengers comics, which he was a fan of while growing up: "In those comics these people shouldn't be in the same room let alone on the same team - and that is the definition of family."
  • [Tom Hiddleston][/p?id=Tom%20Hiddleston] spoke of his role as Loki in an interview by saying, "I can tell you that it's all of them against me. I am the super villain. So it's [Iron Man][/f?id=15119], the Incredible Hulk, [Captain America][/f?id=11441], Thor, Hawkeye, Black Widow and [Nick Fury][/f?id=17077] forming a team because I've got so bad ass."
  • Has an unusually high number of Academy Award nominees in the cast and crew for a comic book movie - or most movies for that matter: [Robert Downey Jr.][/p?id=1809]; [Gwyneth Paltrow][/p?id=1349]; [Samuel L. Jackson][/p?id=1856]; [Jeremy Renner][/p?id=1452]; [Mark Ruffalo][/p?id=1611]; and Cinematographer Seamus McGarvey; possibly many others. Topping the five in [Iron Man 2][/f?id=15120]: [Downey Jr.][/p?id=1809]; Jackson; [Gwyneth Paltrow][/p?id=1349]; [Mickey Rourke][/p?id=1671]; and [Don Cheadle][/p?id=Don%20Cheadle].
  • Legal rights issues prevented a number of "Avengers" characters from their inclusion in this film. Most notably, these include Quicksilver and The Scarlet Witch, the twin children of X-Men) villain Magneto, and frequent adversaries Doctor Doom (nemesis of the [Fantastic Four][/f?id=13724]) and Norman Osbourne/Green Goblin (the primary antagonist of [Spider-Man][/f?id=18668]). Though all characters are owned by Marvel/Disney, the "X-Men" and "[Fantastic Four][/f?id=13724]" characters had all been licensed to Fox Studios, and those of "[Spider-Man][/f?id=18668]" to Sony before work began on an "Avengers" film. Marvel has said that in the future they hope to regain the rights to all licensed properties, that the aforementioned characters might have a role in subsequent "Avengers" films.
