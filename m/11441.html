  • The original mask used had holes that allowed [Matt Salinger's][/p?id=Matt%20Salinger] ears to be seen, but as this caused uncomfortable chafing, plastic ears were used.
  • The film did not get a US theatrical release but was released internationally.
  • They considered getting two different actors for the role of [Captain America][/f?id=11441]. One for the role of the weak pre-superhero Steve Rogers and another for the superhero [Captain America][/f?id=11441], but decided to get an actor that could play both roles instead.
  • Before this movie was introduced, Marvel Comics head honcho [Stan Lee][/p?id=Stan%20Lee] insisted that it was good. He wrote, "Director [Albert Pyun][/p?id=1016] did it so well and so excitingly that everyone in the audience [at the screening] kept clamoring for more," to account for the film being scheduled for a re-shoot.
  • Before [Salinger][/p?id=Matt%20Salinger] was given the part, both [Dolph Lundgren][/p?id=1246] and [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] were up for the part of [Captain America][/f?id=11441]. However, Arnold's accent lost him the part and [Lundgren][/p?id=1246] was busy filming The [Punisher (1989)][/f?id=17552].
  • [Val Kilmer][/p?id=1973] was also considered for the title role, but declined in favor of [The Doors][/f?id=The%20Doors] (1991).
  • In an online interview with the Planet Origo website, director [Albert Pyun][/p?id=1016] mentioned his experience with the "[Captain America][/f?id=11441]" movie. He said that the film was shelved for two years because "it wasn't the movie the studio wanted plus the producing studio was undergoing financial issues." He also said the most challenging part about shooting the film was that they lacked proper funding. [Pyun][/p?id=1016] recalled, "It's pretty difficult to make a film when there were times we actually had no money in the bank."
