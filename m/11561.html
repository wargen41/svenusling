Visste du?

  • [Joseph L. Mankiewicz][/p?id=Joseph%20L.%20Mankiewicz] hoped that the film would be released as two separate pictures, "Caesar and [Cleopatra][/f?id=11561]" followed by "Antony and [Cleopatra][/f?id=11561]." Each was to run approximately three hours. 20th Century-Fox decided against this, and released the film we know today. It runs just over four hours. It is hoped that the missing two hours will be located and that one day a six-hour 'director's cut' will be available.
  • Filming began in 1960.
  • At $194,800, the budget for [Elizabeth Taylor's][/p?id=1275] costumes in this film was the highest ever for a single screen actor. Her 65 costumes included a dress made from 24-carat gold cloth.
  • [Martin Landau][/p?id=1618] was booked to play Euphranor, but when they could not find anyone to play Ruffo, Landau was recast.
  • [Marlon Brando][/p?id=1616] was sought for the role of Marc Antony but was attached to Mutiny on the Bounty (1962).
  • Director [Joseph L. Mankiewicz][/p?id=Joseph%20L.%20Mankiewicz] was fired during the editing/post-production phase. Since there was no actual shooting script (Mankiewicz was writing as he was shooting), Twentieth Century-Fox soon realized that only Mankiewicz knew how the story fit together. He was brought back to complete the project.
  • Director [Joseph L. Mankiewicz][/p?id=Joseph%20L.%20Mankiewicz] referred to this as "the toughest three pictures I ever made".
