Visste du?

  • In the beginning of the movie, whilst Gail ([Jacqueline Bisset][/p?id=Jacqueline%20Bisset]) is diving, she reaches under a part of the shipwreck and gets her arm yanked by the giant green moray eel, which causes her to scream in pain. In real-life, her stunt double, [Jackie][/f?id=15185] Kilbride, dislocated her shoulder doing this scene. A diver was told to pull the stick attached to her wrist from inside the wreck. When the stick appeared, he pulled with all of his might (as instructed). The scene was done in one take, with multiple cameras, as there was no chance (or need) for repeating it.
  • [Robert Shaw][/p?id=1822] was paid six hundred fifty thousand dollars, plus percentage points, for starring in this movie. [Shaw][/p?id=1822] once said: "This is the first time in my career that I've committed myself to a movie before reading the script. So why this time? Just instinct. This production has terrific potential. It has smelled like a winner from the beginning."
  • The picture was notable for its opening underwater diving sequence featuring [Jacqueline Bisset][/p?id=Jacqueline%20Bisset] in a black bikini bottom and see-through wet t-shirt, thus launching her as a Hollywood sex symbol, and contributing big word-of-mouth for the movie, assisting with its box-office success. According to the book "Hit and Run: How [Jon Peters][/p?id=Jon%20Peters] and [Peter Guber][/p?id=Peter%20Guber] Took Sony for a Ride in Hollywood" (1996), Producer [Peter Guber][/p?id=Peter%20Guber] allegedly once said, "That t-shirt made me a rich man!"
  • This movie soundtrack was notable for being pressed onto a colorful bright see-through ocean-blue vinyl, so as to evoke this movie.
  • [Jacqueline Bisset][/p?id=Jacqueline%20Bisset], [Nick Nolte][/p?id=1693], [Robert Shaw][/p?id=1822], and Director [Peter Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] had to learn how to scuba dive for this movie.
  • The character of lighthouse keeper and diving and treasure expert Romer Treece ([Robert Shaw][/p?id=1822]) was inspired by real-life treasure seeker diver [Teddy Tucker][/p?id=Teddy%20Tucker], who helped [Peter Benchley][/p?id=Peter%20Benchley] write the novel. [Tucker][/p?id=Teddy%20Tucker] was hired as a special consultant to the production, and appeared in the movie playing The Harbor Master.
  • Originally, [Peter Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] didn't think [Nick Nolte][/p?id=1693] was right for the film. Neither was [Nolte][/p?id=1693] particularly interested in doing in what he perceived as a bland lead part. Nor were Columbia Pictures studio executives interested in [Nolte][/p?id=1693], as they believed he wasn't a big enough star with enough box-office clout to headline an expensive blockbuster movie. Producer [Peter Guber][/p?id=Peter%20Guber] wanted [Nolte][/p?id=1693]. [Guber][/p?id=Peter%20Guber] once said of the tense meeting with all the parties: "I walked into a conference that could not have started on shakier ground if we'd held it on the San Andreas fault. Here was an actor that the studio didn't want, sitting down to discuss a part he'd already rejected, with a filmmaker who'd decided he wasn't right for the role anyway. Nick immediately sensed that [Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] wasn't too thrilled with him for the character. But as they talked, Nick became intrigued with the changing character, and with the challenge of working underwater, and [Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] became intrigued with Nick. Finally, after two hours, [Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] reached across the table to Nick and said, 'You're the man for me,' knocking Nick for a loop."
  • A feeding frenzy by a pack of grey sharks took seven hundred twenty dives, and one thousand eighty hours to shoot. The sequence was shot in the Great Barrier Reef off Australia, where it was said could not be duplicated anywhere else in the world.
  • The ninth highest-grossing film of 1977 at the box-office.
  • After [Jaws][/f?id=14407] (1975), this was the second of two adaptations of novels by [Peter Benchley][/p?id=Peter%20Benchley] that co-starred [Robert Shaw][/p?id=1822]. Columbia Pictures studio executives were initially reluctant to have [Shaw][/p?id=1822] headline the film of another [Benchley][/p?id=Peter%20Benchley] book. Producer [Peter Guber][/p?id=Peter%20Guber] said: "There was no reluctance on my part at all, nor on Robert's. Neither he nor I was concerned about prior associations. He's too damn good an actor to repeat himself, and besides, the roles are entirely different. This is '[The Deep'][/f?id=11906] not '[Jaws II][/f?id=14408]'."
  • The movie has been credited as making big stars out of its two leads, [Nick Nolte][/p?id=1693] and [Jacqueline Bisset][/p?id=Jacqueline%20Bisset].
  • Reportedly, [Jacqueline Bisset][/p?id=Jacqueline%20Bisset] and [Nick Nolte][/p?id=1693] didn't get along well when the movie began, but ended up having a romantic relationship for most of the production schedule. This made producer [Peter Guber][/p?id=Peter%20Guber] very happy because he felt that the more chemistry the two leads had, the better the perilous dramatic scenes involving them would work in the movie.
  • Directors considered and short-listed to direct this movie included [John Boorman][/p?id=John%20Boorman], [Steven Spielberg][/p?id=1907], and [John Frankenheimer][/p?id=John%20Frankenheimer], but all had already been contracted to other productions at the time. On the short-list too was [Peter Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] who became the film's director. [Yates][/p?id=Peter%20Yates] was hired on the basis of [Bullitt][/f?id=11380] (1968) and [Murphy's War][/f?id=Murphys%20Krig] (1971).
  • Columbia Pictures' top highest-grossing movie at the box-office for 1977.
  • First lead starring role in a major American movie for [Nick Nolte][/p?id=1693].
  • [Candice Bergen][/p?id=1126], [Katharine Ross][/p?id=Katharine%20Ross], and [Charlotte Rampling][/p?id=Charlotte%20Rampling] were considered for the role of Gail Berke.
  • After resigning as Vice President of Production at Columbia Pictures to go and make his own movies, within thirty-six hours of [Peter Guber][/p?id=Peter%20Guber] setting up his own production company "[Casablanca][/f?id=11455] Filmworks", [Guber][/p?id=Peter%20Guber] had secured the screen rights to this movie and obtained financing for this film from Columbia Pictures.
  • The only theatrical feature film related to the story works of [Peter Benchley][/p?id=Peter%20Benchley] that has not been made by Universal Pictures.
  • The movie's main movie poster mimicked the poster for [Jaws][/f?id=14407] (1975). Instead of a killer shark at the bottom of the sea and a girl atop the water swimming, the poster featured a girl where the shark had been positioned and looking like she was struggling to swim to the top. The blurb evoked [Jaws][/f?id=14407] (1975) suspense with the tagline, "Is anything worth the terror of [The Deep][/f?id=11906]?"
  • Sir [Sean Connery][/p?id=1866], [Robert Mitchum][/p?id=1815], [Burt Lancaster][/p?id=1120], and [Charlton Heston][/p?id=1151] were considered for the role of Romer Treece.
  • [Cameron Mitchell][/p?id=Cameron%20Mitchell] played the Captain of the "Goliath", but all of his scenes were deleted from the final cut.
  • [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441] and [Ryan O'Neal][/p?id=Ryan%20O'Neal] were touted for the role of [David Sanders][/p?id=David%20Sander].
