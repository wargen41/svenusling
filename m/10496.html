  • Screenwriter '[Joe Halpin'][/p?id=Joe%20Halpin] is quoted in the book "[Seagalogy][/p?id=1906]: The Ass Kicking Films of [Steven Seagal][/p?id=1906]" as saying that "[Attack Force][/f?id=10496]" had originally been written and filmed as "Harvester," a sci-fi/horror movie with [Seagal][/p?id=1906] and his men battling an invading force of vampire space aliens. After [Seagal][/p?id=1906], [Halpin][/p?id=Joe%20Halpin] and director [Michael Keusch][/p?id=Michael%20Keusch] had delivered the finished sci-fi film, the production and distribution company decided to eliminate the sci-fi space-alien elements and replace them with another plot having about a super-addictive drug that turns its users into superhuman killers. They called back a few of the lower-paid actors for re-shoots, re-dubbed the majority of [Seagal's][/p?id=1906] dialog via ADR, totally re-edited the film and changed it into the "[Attack Force][/f?id=10496]" we know today. [Seagal][/p?id=1906] and [Halpin][/p?id=Joe%20Halpin] were not involved.
