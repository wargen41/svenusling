Visste du?

  • The squadron's mascot, "Nigger", was given beers to drink as depicted in the movie. However, the dog had a habit of urinating on people's legs which made the dog unpopular with many of the squadron's personnel. Also, the dog was killed when hit by a car, but unlike in the movie, the driver did attempt to avoid the dog and the occupants of the car were injured as a result. Any reports of Nigger's grave being opened, and the bones revealed proving not to be of a dog, are false. The grave was never opened and is protected to this day.
  • Footage used to show the bombs as they skipped on the surface of the water towards the dams was drawn from footage of the bombs being tested. Backspinning the bombs gave them gyroscopic stability when skipping across the water, then held them against the dams as they sank. To conceal the backspin, which was still a state secret at the time of filming, the bombs in the footage were painted over frame by frame.
  • According to [Richard Todd's][/p?id=Richard%20Todd] autobiography, the scene that upset him most during filming was the finale where his character Guy Gibson goes off to write letters to the families of the men killed on the raid. [Todd][/p?id=Richard%20Todd], a paratrooper combat veteran of World War II, had written such letters for real. The scene brought it all back for him.
  • Gibson's dog "Nigger" was dubbed into "Trigger" for the U.S. market.
  • Critics believed the raid was oversold, its achievements exaggerated and other Bomber Command raids unfairly ignored.
  • An estimated one thousand six hundred civilians - about six hundred Germans and one thousand mainly Soviet prisoners of war - died in the raid.
  • This is one of the movies from which [George Lucas][/p?id=George%20Lucas] used clips to edit the rough cut of [Star Wars][/f?id=19851] (1977) (which utilized many features of the finale of this movie quite closely, notably the briefing, the ground staff waiting for news, the troika formation of the attacking aircraft and so on). In addition, the following exchange from this movie was reproduced almost verbatim (with the exception of the characters' names): Gibson: "How many guns d'you think there are, Trevor?" Trevor: "I'd say there's about ten guns, some in the field and some in the tower."
  • Uncredited theatrical movie debut of [Patrick McGoohan][/p?id=Patrick%20McGoohan] (Guard on Door).
  • This movie was used as inspiration for the final battle scenes in Star Wars (1977). Gilbert Taylor, Director of Photography and Stuart Freeborn, Make-up Supervisor for [Star Wars: Episode IV - A New Hope][/f?id=19851] (1977) also worked on this movie. This was suggested by [Alan Ladd][/p?id=Alan%20Ladd], Jr. when Lucus initially presented the idea to him for studio approval. "I'll shoot it like The Dam Busters".
  • [Richard Todd][/p?id=Richard%20Todd] ([Wing Commander][/f?id=21952] Guy Gibson, V.C., D.S.O., D.F.C.) also played Major [John Howard][/p?id=John%20Howard] in [The Longest Day][/f?id=11974] (1962). Major [Howard][/p?id=John%20Howard] led the first action on D-Day, the attack and seizure of the bridges over the Orne River and Caen Canal, now known as Pegasus Bridge. In reality, [Todd][/p?id=Richard%20Todd], who was a paratrooper Captain on D-Day, led the first of the main force paratroopers sent to relieve the Pegasus Bridge attackers.
  • Most of the damage to the dams were repaired within two weeks, although production did not return to normal until September 1943.
  • This movie premiered twelve years to the day of the original raid.
  • [Richard Todd][/p?id=Richard%20Todd] was an established star at the time with the right to refuse the studio's choice of directors. It was only after dinner with [Michael Anderson][/p?id=Michael%20Anderson] (and their respective spouses) that the two men realized they got along quite well, and [Todd][/p?id=Richard%20Todd] approved [Anderson][/p?id=Michael%20Anderson] as director.
  • Guy Gibson was famously arrogant and aloof. He was not a popular officer, especially with lower ranks. His dismissive attitude to [Leonard][/f?id=15707] Cheshire's VC was considered typical. He also expressed racist and anti-Semitic views.
