  • [Christopher McQuarrie's][/p?id=Christopher%20McQuarrie] inspiration for the character of Keyser Soze was a real-life murderer by the name of John List, who murdered his family and then disappeared for 17 years.
  • The line-up scene was scripted as a serious scene, but after a full day of filming takes where the actors couldn't keep a straight face, director [Bryan Singer][/p?id=Bryan%20Singer] decided to use the funniest takes. A making-of documentary shows [Singer][/p?id=Bryan%20Singer] becoming furious at the actors for the constant cracking-up. In an interview (on the Special Edition DVD), [Kevin Pollak][/p?id=Kevin%20Pollak] states that the hilarity came about when [Benicio Del Toro][/p?id=Benicio%20Del%20Toro] "farted, like 12 takes in a row." [Del Toro][/p?id=Benicio%20Del%20Toro] himself said "somebody" farted, but no one knew who.
  • When Redfoot flicks his cigarette into the face of McManus, it was originally intended to hit his chest, so McManus' reaction is actually [Stephen Baldwin's][/p?id=1894] real unscripted reaction that [Bryan Singer][/p?id=Bryan%20Singer] decided to keep in the movie.
  • The role of Dave Kujan was offered to [Robert De Niro][/p?id=1808] and [Christopher Walken][/p?id=1173], who both turned it down.
  • [Michael Biehn][/p?id=1642] turned down the role of McManus because of his previous commitment to filming Jade.
  • The idea for this movie started only with the concept of a movie poster of five of guys in a lineup.
  • In a poll on IMDb, the movie was voted as having the best plot twist, beating out [The Sixth Sense][/f?id=18486], [The Crying Game][/f?id=11729], and [Witness for the Prosecution][/f?id=22018].
  • Originally, Keyser Soze was supposed to have the name Keyser Sume, named after [Christopher McQuarrie's][/p?id=Christopher%20McQuarrie] old boss. He allowed his old boss to read the script, and decided he did not want to be associated with an inherently evil villain, so requested a change be made.
  • [Kevin Spacey][/p?id=1550] had the fingers on his left hand glued together to make his character's disability more realistic, and filed down his shoes to make them look worn by his character's gimp.
  • [Al Pacino][/p?id=1008] also read the part of Dave Kujan, but had to pass due to scheduling conflicts. [Pacino][/p?id=1008] has since noted that this is the film he regrets turning down the most.
  • The part of Verbal Kint was always intended for actor [Kevin Spacey][/p?id=1550].
  • [Gabriel Byrne][/p?id=Gabriel%20Byrne] originally turned down the film, not believing that the filmmakers could pull it off. He was convinced after a sit-down meeting with [Christopher McQuarrie][/p?id=Christopher%20McQuarrie] and [Bryan Singer][/p?id=Bryan%20Singer], impressed by their enthusiasm and vision. As the start date approached, Bryne backed out. He was undergoing personal issues at the time and wasn't able to leave Los Angeles. Consequently [Singer][/p?id=Bryan%20Singer] reshuffled the schedule so that the entire film could be made in the LA area over a period of five weeks, all to accommodate his lead actor.
  • [Stephen Baldwin][/p?id=1894] was wary about joining the production as he was tired of taking part in independent films that turned out to be merely exploitative.
  • [Benicio Del Toro][/p?id=Benicio%20Del%20Toro] was cast at the suggestion of [Kevin Spacey][/p?id=1550]. His part was originally written for someone like [Harry Dean Stanton][/p?id=1361].
  • [Christopher Walken][/p?id=1173], [Tommy Lee Jones][/p?id=1957], [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441], [Charlie Sheen][/p?id=1149], [James Spader][/p?id=James%20Spader], [Al Pacino][/p?id=1008] and [Johnny Cash][/p?id=Johnny%20Cash] were all offered the part of Redfoot, the LA fence.
  • All the actors worked for less than their usual fees.
  • [Kevin Spacey][/p?id=1550] met with doctors and experts on cerebral palsy to discuss how it might affect his characterization.
  • The film was promoted by a teaser campaign with the tagline "Who is Keyzer Soze?".
  • [Kevin Spacey][/p?id=1550] had to read the script twice when he first received it to make sure he fully understood it.
  • Neither [Bryan Singer][/p?id=Bryan%20Singer] or [Christopher McQuarrie][/p?id=Christopher%20McQuarrie] realized that the film's famous line, "the greatest trick the Devil ever pulled was convincing the world he didn't exist", was actually a quote from French poet Baudelaire.
  • All the actors were encouraged to ad lib perplexed reactions to [Benicio Del Toro's][/p?id=Benicio%20Del%20Toro] oddball vocal stylings.
