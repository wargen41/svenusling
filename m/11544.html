Visste du?

  • [Simon Ward][/p?id=Simon%20Ward] was the second choice for Sir [Winston Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill] after [Malcolm McDowell][/p?id=1604] repeatedly declined the role.
  • Three actors portrayed Sir [Winston Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill]: Russell Lewis at the age of seven, [Michael Audreson][/p?id=Michael%20Audreson] at the age of thirteen, and [Simon Ward][/p?id=Simon%20Ward] as a young man. [However][/p?id=Ian%20Ho], [Audreson][/p?id=Michael%20Audreson] was dubbed by [Ward][/p?id=Simon%20Ward] in all of his scenes.
  • Writer and producer Carl Foreman was so impressed with Sir [Richard Attenborough's][/p?id=1780] directorial debut, [Oh! What a Lovely War][/f?id=Oh%2C%20Vilket%20Makal%C3%B6st%20Krig] (1969), that he offered him the opportunity to direct this movie and play Lord Randolph [Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill]. He declined the latter offer, and then cast [Robert Shaw][/p?id=1822].
  • [Simon Ward][/p?id=Simon%20Ward] was a predominantly unknown actor when he was cast as the central character of Sir [Winston Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill] in this movie. [Richard Attenborough][/p?id=1780] threatened to quit the film if producer Carl Foreman (who didn't want [Ward][/p?id=Simon%20Ward]) didn't agree to his casting.
  • At the time that the film was made, [Jane Seymour][/p?id=Jane%20Seymour] (Pamela Plowden) was the daughter-in-law of the director [Richard Attenborough][/p?id=1780], as she was married to his son Michael [Attenborough][/p?id=1780] from 1971 to 1973.
  • Although [Anne Bancroft][/p?id=1048] (Lady Jennie [Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill]) played the mother of [Simon Ward][/p?id=Simon%20Ward] (Sir [Winston Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill]), she was only ten years older than him.
  • [Robert Shaw][/p?id=1822] (Lord Randolph [Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill]) was the third choice for the part.
  • [Simon Ward][/p?id=Simon%20Ward] portrayed the young Sir [Winston Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill] in this movie, as well as providing the voice of the older [Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill].
  • Sir [Ian Holm][/p?id=1386], Sir [John Mills][/p?id=1494], [Jack Hawkins][/p?id=1399], [Patrick Magee][/p?id=Patrick%20Magee], Sir [Anthony Hopkins][/p?id=1053], and [Edward Woodward][/p?id=Edward%20Woodward] received "special appearance" credits.
  • One of several biographical movies directed by Sir [Richard Attenborough][/p?id=1780], with his other biopics including [Chaplin][/f?id=11492] (1992), [Gandhi][/f?id=Gandhi] (1982), [Grey Owl][/f?id=Grey%20Owl] (1999), [Shadowlands][/f?id=18253] (1993), and [In Love And War][/f?id=15051] (1996).
  • Though this movie was not directed by screenwriter Carl Foreman, the credits declare that this Sir [Richard Attenborough][/p?id=1780] movie is: "A Film by Carl Foreman and [Richard Attenborough][/p?id=1780]".
  • Writer and producer Carl Foreman wanted [Albert Finney][/p?id=1015] to play Sir [Winston Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill]. [Finney][/p?id=1015] played [Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill] in old age in [The Gathering Storm (2002)][/f?id=The%20Gathering%20Storm%20(2002)].
  • [Dirk Bogarde][/p?id=Dirk%20Bogarde] turned down the role of Lord Randolph [Churchill][/p?id=Winston%20Churchill].
