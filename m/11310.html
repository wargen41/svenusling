  • The bar in which Montgomery and Spike start a brawl is called Torchy's, the same name of the bar [Eddie Murphy][/p?id=1261] shook down in director [Walter Hill's][/p?id=1986] [48 Hrs.][/f?id=14130] (1982). The Torchy's waitress in this film who phones in the brawl to the police is played by [Margot Rose][/p?id=Margot%20Rose], who appeared in "48 Hrs." as the girlfriend of a character who (we're told) used to tend bar at Torchy's. In yet another nod to [Hill's][/p?id=1986] 1982 box-office hit, the car driven by Brewster's personal photographer is a sky-blue Cadillac convertible, the same type of heap driven by [Nick Nolte][/p?id=1693] in that earlier film.
  • This is the seventh film adaptation of George Barr McCutcheon's 1902 novel.
  • As a nod to [48 Hrs.][/f?id=14130] (1982), the bookie who lays down all the bets for [Richard Pryor][/p?id=1790] wears the exact same suit that [Eddie Murphy][/p?id=1261] also wore.
  • [Jennifer Beals][/p?id=Jennifer%20Beals] was up for the Angela role before [Lonette McKee][/p?id=Lonette%20McKee] was cast.
