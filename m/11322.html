Visste du?

  • The reason [Mia Farrow][/p?id=Mia%20Farrow] wears sunglasses most of the film is that [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] did not feel she could pass herself as a tough Italian "broad", so he had her wear the sunglasses most of the film to hide her eyes, making her seem more sultry and mysterious.
  • The movie makes mention of Weinstein's Majestic Bungalow Colony resort in the Catskills (Catskill Mountains). This is where [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] got his start in show-business performing magic tricks there at sixteen years of age.
  • [Woody Allen's][/p?id=2010] manager and producer, Jack Rollins, was the inspiration for the Danny Rose character. Rollins appears in the movie as himself.
  • This is Nick Apollo Forte's only film appearance.
  • Actors [Danny Aiello][/p?id=1206], [Robert De Niro][/p?id=1808] and [Sylvester Stallone][/p?id=1915] were considered for the role of Lou Canova. Apparently, [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] initially offered the part to [Stallone][/p?id=1915]. In the end, Nick Apollo Forte was cast.
  • During their relationship, [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] loved to write roles for [Mia Farrow][/p?id=Mia%20Farrow] that she had always wanted to play but couldn't because no one would believe her as this or that character. The character on this movie is a perfect example of that because she wanted to play an Italian broad, and [Allen][/p?id=2010] was so sure of her talent that wrote it for her.
  • Dissatisfied with the original take of the scene, director [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] re-shot the scene in which Danny Rose tries to sell acts to Phil Chomsky, re-casting comedian and singer David I. Kissel in the role of the owner of "Weinstein's Majestic Bungalow Colony". Kissel persistently called it "Mrs. Weinberg's Bungalow Colony" during filming, and [Allen][/p?id=2010], exasperated, asked the actor why he kept saying this. Kissel explained that his wife always bought a brand of kosher chopped liver called "Mrs. Weinberg's Chopped Liver," and he kept thinking of that. [Allen][/p?id=2010], amused, but ever the perfectionist, taped the words "Weinstein's Majestic Bungalow Colony" to the top of a filing cabinet in the office. As you view this scene, early in the film, you will notice that Dave Kissel faces away from the camera as he says those words, and speaks with his back to the camera: he is reading the words "Weinstein's Majestic Bungalow Colony" off that "crib sheet" on the filing cabinet.
  • Third of thirteen cinema movie collaborations of actress [Mia Farrow][/p?id=Mia%20Farrow] and actor-writer-director [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010].
  • Actress [Mia Farrow][/p?id=Mia%20Farrow] wore padding under her clothes so as to appear larger.
  • The thirteenth feature film directed by [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010].
