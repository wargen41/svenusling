  • [Quentin Tarantino][/p?id=1763] wanted [James Woods][/p?id=1426] to play a role in the film, and made him five different cash offers. [Woods'][/p?id=1426] agent refused the offers without ever mentioning it to [Woods][/p?id=1426] as the sums offered were well below what [Woods][/p?id=1426] would usually receive. When [Tarantino][/p?id=1763] and [Woods][/p?id=1426] later met for the first time, [Woods][/p?id=1426] learned of the offer and was annoyed enough to get a new agent. [Tarantino][/p?id=1763] avoided telling [Woods][/p?id=1426] which role he was offered "because the actor who played the role was magnificent anyway". It is widely accepted that the role that [Tarantino][/p?id=1763] was referring to was Mr. Orange.
  • The film contains 272 uses of the word "fuck".
  • [Quentin Tarantino][/p?id=1763] originally wrote the role of Mr. Pink for himself. [Steve Buscemi][/p?id=1900] originally auditioned for the part of Mr. White. [Michael Madsen][/p?id=1657] originally auditioned for the part of Mr Pink. [George Clooney][/p?id=1325] read for the role of Mr. Blonde/Vic Vega but was turned down, and [Christopher Walken][/p?id=1173] refused the same role. [Vincent][/f?id=21951] Gallo turned down the role of Mr. Pink. [Samuel L. Jackson][/p?id=1856] auditioned for the role of Mr. Orange. Once [Tim Roth][/p?id=Tim%20Roth] was cast, [Quentin Tarantino][/p?id=1763] originally wanted him to play Mr. Blonde or Mr. Pink. [Robert Forster][/p?id=1812] and [Timothy Carey][/p?id=Timothy%20Carey] auditioned for the part of Joe Cabot, and the film is dedicated to [Carey][/p?id=Timothy%20Carey].
  • [David Duchovny][/p?id=1214] auditioned for a part.
  • During filming, a paramedic was kept on the set to make sure that Mr. Orange's ([Tim Roth][/p?id=Tim%20Roth]) amount of blood loss was kept consistent and realistic to that of a real gunshot victim.
  • To avoid alienating the film's backers, producer [Lawrence Bender][/p?id=Lawrence%20Bender] had the tamer scenes shot first, so that the dailies would strengthen the backers' confidence before getting to the nasty, violent scenes.
  • [Michael Madsen][/p?id=1657] had difficulty filming the torture scenes. He was particularly reluctant when he was required to hit actor [Kirk Baltz][/p?id=Kirk%20Baltz]. When the cop, pleading for his life, says that he has a child at home (a line not in the script), [Madsen][/p?id=1657], himself a new father at the time, was so disturbed by the idea of leaving a child fatherless that he couldn't finish the scene.
  • All the actors who portrayed the criminals have spent time in jail.
  • Armed with $30,000 and a 16mm camera, [Quentin Tarantino][/p?id=1763] was all set to make the film with a bunch of friends, including his producing partner [Lawrence Bender][/p?id=Lawrence%20Bender] who was going to play Nice Guy Eddie. It was then that [Tarantino][/p?id=1763] received an answerphone message from [Harvey][/f?id=Harvey] Keitel, asking if he could not only be in the film but help produce it. Keitel had gotten involved via the wife of [Bender's][/p?id=Lawrence%20Bender] acting class teacher, who had managed to get a copy of the script to him. Keitel's involvement helped raise the budget to $1.5 million.
  • [Terry Gilliam][/p?id=1926] is thanked in the credits in gratitude for advice he gave to [Quentin Tarantino][/p?id=1763] during a Sundance workshop.
  • [Quentin Tarantino][/p?id=1763] was originally going to play Mr Pink, although he made a point of letting all the other actors audition for the part. When [Steve Buscemi][/p?id=1900] came in to read for it, [Tarantino][/p?id=1763] told him that he really wanted the part for himself and that the only way [Buscemi][/p?id=1900] could possibly wrest it from him was to do a killer audition. [Buscemi][/p?id=1900] duly complied.
