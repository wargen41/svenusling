  • [Jet Li][/p?id=Jet%20Li] was originally cast to play [Li][/p?id=Jet%20Li] Mu Bai, but turned the part down to appear in [Romeo Must Die][/f?id=Romeo%20Must%20Die] (2000). The role was next offered to Hong Kong singer/actor Leon Lai but he, too, turned it down.
  • [Ziyi Zhang][/p?id=2017] studied calligraphy for several months along with her other training for the movie.
  • [Michelle Yeoh][/p?id=Michelle%20Yeoh] did not speak Mandarin, and the script was presented to her phonetically with help from Mandarin-speaking crew members - her Malaysian accent can be heard throughout. [Yun-Fat Chow][/p?id=Yun-Fat%20Chow] did speak Mandarin (his first language is Cantonese) but native Mandarin speakers thought his accent strained and overdone.
  • [Michelle Yeoh][/p?id=Michelle%20Yeoh] tore her ACL (anterior cruciate ligament) during the shooting of an early fighting sequence and had to be flown to the U.S. for knee surgery. She returned to the set at different times to film non-action scenes until the knee had recovered.
  • "Crouching tiger hidden dragon" is a quote from Chinese mythology. It refers to hiding your strength from others; advice which is followed too well by the characters in the film.
  • In Chinese, Lo's name is "[Little][/p?id=Jet%20Li] Tiger", and Jen's name is "Gorgeous Dragon".
  • In 2001, this became the first foreign language film to earn over $100 million in the United States.
  • Tan Dun had only two weeks to compose and record the musical score.
  • The four main actors all spoke Mandarin, but with different accents. [Yun-Fat Chow][/p?id=Yun-Fat%20Chow] had a Cantonese accent, [Michelle Yeoh][/p?id=Michelle%20Yeoh] had a Malaysian/English accent, [Ziyi Zhang][/p?id=2017] had a Beijing accent, and [Chen Chang][/p?id=Chen%20Chang] had a Taiwanese accent. Because of the difficulty some Chinese-speaking markets had with the voices, some markets actually had a dubbed version (into standard Mandarin) of the soundtrack.
  • While [Ziyi Zhang's][/p?id=2017] character is obviously highly trained & skillful in martial arts, the actress herself has never had any official martial arts training at all. Instead she uses her dance techniques to learn her moves in these scenes, as if they were a dance rather than a fight (which in terms of creating & filming them is actually not that far from the truth).
  • The only martial arts film to date to be nominated for an Academy Award for Best Picture.
  • Foreign language film nominated for Best Picture to date with the most number of Academy Award nominations.
