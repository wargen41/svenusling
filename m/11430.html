  • [Richard Harris][/p?id=1789] desperately sought the lead role, despite being repeatedly refused due to his limited singing ability. At one point he even paid a man to carry a board down the Strand that said, "[Harris][/p?id=1789] Better than [Burton][/p?id=1782], Only [Harris][/p?id=1789] for [Camelot][/f?id=11430]". When [Vanessa Redgrave][/p?id=Vanessa%20Redgrave] was cast as Guenivere, [Harris][/p?id=1789] sent a note to producer Jack L. Warner, which read, "Height of [Vanessa Redgrave][/p?id=Vanessa%20Redgrave]: 5 feet 11 inches. [Richard Burton][/p?id=1782]: 5 feet 10 inches. [Richard Harris][/p?id=1789]: 6 feet 1 inch."
  • [Julie Andrews][/p?id=1525] was asked to reprise her stage role of Guenevere, but had become such a popular film star by this time that she was unable to accept the role. Ironically, Jack L. Warner, who produced the movie version of [Camelot][/f?id=11430] (1967), was the same man who produced the film version of [My Fair Lady][/f?id=16556] (1964), and who had given the role of Eliza Dolittle to [Audrey Hepburn][/p?id=1064] because he thought that [Julie Andrews][/p?id=1525] would not be a big enough box-office name. Warner apologized to [Andrews][/p?id=1525] on his troubles about the [My Fair Lady][/f?id=16556] casting and the two became in good standing with each other from that moment forward. [Andrews][/p?id=1525], ultimately, did not get to reprise her role, because the film's director [Joshua Logan][/p?id=Joshua%20Logan] wanted [Vanessa Redgrave][/p?id=Vanessa%20Redgrave] for the role, instead.
  • In later years, [Richard Harris][/p?id=1789] would buy the rights to the stage version of "[Camelot][/f?id=11430]" for $1 million, revamped the musical and took it on tour. This made him a very rich man.
  • At one point, while filming on a Warner Bros. soundstage, [Richard Harris][/p?id=1789] and producer Jack L. Warner were at odds over how to do a scene. Warner took [Harris][/p?id=1789] out onto the studio lot, and showed him the famous water tower with the Warner Bros. logo on it. "What does that tower say, Richard?" asked Warner. "It says 'Warner Brothers,'" [Harris][/p?id=1789] replied. "Right," said Warner. "Now when it says '[Harris][/p?id=1789] Brothers,' then we'll do it your way."
  • Jack L. Warner was ultimately unable to persuade director [Joshua Logan][/p?id=Joshua%20Logan] to cast [Richard Burton][/p?id=1782], [Julie Andrews][/p?id=1525], and [Robert Goulet][/p?id=Robert%20Goulet] in the leading roles, because [Logan][/p?id=Joshua%20Logan] was belligerent to him on the casting, as he insisted on selecting [Richard Harris][/p?id=1789], [Vanessa Redgrave][/p?id=Vanessa%20Redgrave], and [Franco Nero][/p?id=Franco%20Nero] - a move that was one of the major contributions to the film's critical and commercial failure. Among the other factors was that [Logan][/p?id=Joshua%20Logan] was able to manipulate Warner's ego to persuade him from cutting the screenplay's length, despite the fact that the studio executive had already agreed with the film's unofficial producer, Joel Freeman, that it was overlong.
