Visste du?

  • Set in Colorado, but filmed in Italy. The American Environmental Protection Agency wouldn't allow filming in America for fear of the damage that could be left by the film crew. Italy was chosen because it had spectacular mountains that are similar to the Colorado Rockies. The production crew paid a very large deposit against clean-up costs.
  • To demonstrate his faith in the safety equipment, director [Renny Harlin][/p?id=1777] put on a harness and flung himself out on a cable over a cliff.
  • Electrical storms hit during filming, knocking down five crew members. Climber Earl Wiggins was hit three times, but was only slightly injured. During a later storm, crew members had fun taking pictures of each other with their hair standing on end while the climbers pointed out the wisdom of evacuating.
  • [Christopher Walken][/p?id=1173] was originally cast as Qualen but left the production before filming began, so [John Lithgow][/p?id=1492] was cast at the very last minute.
  • Sneak-preview audiences saw a scene where a rabbit gets killed by gunfire. Their reaction was strong enough for [Sylvester Stallone][/p?id=1915] to invest $100,000 of his own money to have the scene re-shot so that the rabbit escaped.
  • During one climbing-scene director [Renny Harlin][/p?id=1777] complained that the safety-lines were visible, so the stuntman performed the climbing without any safety-lines.
  • Carolco had originally signed [Sylvester Stallone][/p?id=1915] to appear opposite [John Candy][/p?id=1475] in a comedy directed by [John Hughes][/p?id=John%20Hughes] about feuding neighbors. When the project was dropped, [Stallone][/p?id=1915] was persuaded to appear in Cliffhanger.
  • Three writers claimed that Cliffhanger was their idea. To avoid jeopardizing the film's release, they were paid $250,000 each to drop the case.
