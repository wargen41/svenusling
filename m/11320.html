Visste du?

  • A French postman felt off his bicycle on the Champs Elysées, when he saw some extras dressed as German soldiers crossing the avenue during a lunch pause. He ran out screaming, "They're back! They're back!"
  • Some of the French actors, such as [Charles Boyer][/p?id=1143], [Leslie Caron][/p?id=Leslie%20Caron] and [Jean-Pierre Cassel][/p?id=Jean-Pierre%20Cassel], dubbed their own dialogue into English. The American theatrical release of this film had all the French dialogue parts of the film dubbed into English language.
  • One of the main reasons for the movie being filmed in B&W was the Nazi flags. The French authorities refused to allow red and black Nazi flags to fly in Paris, even for a movie. They agreed only to have black and gray flags.
  • During this movie a Nazi propaganda film is shown, saying that Poland had attacked Germany and started the war. The truth is that the Germans staged a "Polish attack" by dressing Polish prisoners in military uniforms and filming the "invasion" as an excuse to invade Poland.
  • [Roddy McDowell][/p?id=Roddy%20McDowell] was booked for a major cameo role but was replaced before filming.
  • The meaning and relevance of this film's title Is Paris Burning? relates to the order given by Nazi high-command to bomb and destroy the city of Paris during the Second World War. During the film, when the resistance and Allied forces take back the city of Paris, on the phone can be heard a high-ranking Nazi (probably [Adolf Hitler][/p?id=Adolf%20Hitler] played by [Billy Frick][/p?id=Billy%20Frick]) ask the question: Is Paris Burning? Apparently, during August 1944 in the actual Second World War, [Adolf Hitler][/p?id=Adolf%20Hitler] ordered a response to this Is Paris Burning? question from the Nazi German Commander of Paris, General Dietrich von Choltitz (played by [Gert Fröbe][/p?id=Gert%20Fr%C3%B6be] in this film). Other reports state that the origin of the Is Paris Burning? question was that it was [Hitler's][/p?id=Adolf%20Hitler] question to his Chief of Staff of Operations Alfred Jodl and not to von Choltitz.
  • French actor [Claude Rich][/p?id=Claude%20Rich] actually plays two characters in this film. [Rich][/p?id=Claude%20Rich] plays the young Lieutenant Pierre de la Fouchardière and the mustached Général Leclerc, the Commandant de la Fouchardière, but [Rich][/p?id=Claude%20Rich] is only credited in the film's closing credits for playing Leclerc. In real life, as a teenage boy during the Second World War, whilst looking at soldiers in a street, [Rich][/p?id=Claude%20Rich] had been protected by the real de la Fouchardière when he motioned him to come into a building where he was.
