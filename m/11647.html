  • [John McTiernan][/p?id=John%20McTiernan] was offered the chance to direct this film, and turned it down. Later, while editing [Nomads][/f?id=Fientliga%20Fr%C3%A4mlingar] (1986), his feature debut, he was offered the chance to direct [Arnold Schwarzenegger's][/p?id=1062] second film for 20th Century Fox, Predator (1987), which he accepted.
  • [Vernon Wells][/p?id=Vernon%20Wells] wasn't the original actor cast to play Bennett. He did audition for the role, but another actor got it. On the first day of filming however, director [Mark L. Lester][/p?id=Mark%20L.%20Lester] fired the other actor and hired [Wells][/p?id=Vernon%20Wells]. This is the reason that Bennett's clothes seem so tight, because [Wells][/p?id=Vernon%20Wells] was bigger than the original actor, and the production didn't have time to make a new costume. Over the years, a rumor has developed that [Wells][/p?id=Vernon%20Wells] originally auditioned for the part of [Matrix][/f?id=16078], but this is false.
  • In 1986, a sequel was written by Steven E. de Souza, and revised by [Frank Darabont][/p?id=Frank%20Darabont], with an eye to having [John McTiernan][/p?id=John%20McTiernan] direct. The script was based on the book '[Nothing][/f?id=17168] Lasts Forever' (1979) by Roderick Thorp, but [Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] wasn't interested in reprising the role. The script was then reworked with a new central character, eventually played by [Bruce Willis][/p?id=1117], and became [Die Hard][/f?id=12136] (1988).
