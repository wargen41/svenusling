  • The word "humbug" is misunderstood by many people, which is a pity since the word provides a key insight into Ebenezer [Scrooge's][/f?id=18028] ([Alastair Sim's][/p?id=Alastair%20Sim]) hatred of Christmas. The word "humbug" describes deceitful efforts to fool people by pretending to a fake loftiness or false sincerity. So when [Scrooge][/f?id=18028] calls Christmas a humbug, he is claiming that people only pretend to be charitable and kind in an effort to delude him, each other, and themselves. In [Scrooge's][/f?id=18028] eyes, he is the one man honest enough to admit that no one really cares about anyone else, so for him, every wish for a Merry Christmas is one more deceitful effort to fool him and take advantage of him. This is a man who has turned to profit because he honestly believes everyone else will someday betray him or abandon him the moment he trusts them.
  • Sir [Michael Hordern][/p?id=1650] was not on-set when the "Marley's Ghost" segment was filmed. He was added in later through the use of an optical printer. He only appeared together with [Alastair Sim][/p?id=Alastair%20Sim] in the two scenes at the end of the "Ghost of Christmas Past" sequence, the latter of the two being the scene where Jacob Marley dies. This was also true of [Michael Dolan][/p?id=Michael%20Dolan] (Spirit of Christmas Past). He never actually played any scenes on the set with [Sim][/p?id=Alastair%20Sim].
  • Changes to the screenplay from the Charles Dickens book were made, mostly in the Christmas Past sequence. Among these changes are: Reversing the birth order of [Scrooge][/f?id=18028] and his sister, so as to add that [Scrooge's][/f?id=18028] mother died giving birth to him. Creating a character named "Mr. Jorkin", who does not appear in the book. Flashbacks of several incidents in [Scrooge's][/f?id=18028] past (his sister's death, meeting Jacob Marley, taking over Fezziwig's warehouse, and Marley's death) which do not appear in the book.
  • Just after Jacob Marley (Sir [Michael Hordern][/p?id=1650]) dies, the Ghost of Christmas Past ([Michael Dolan][/p?id=Michael%20Dolan]) calls Ebenezer [Scrooge][/f?id=18028] ([Alastair Sim][/p?id=Alastair%20Sim]) a "'squeezing, wrenching, grasping, covetous old sinner." This is how Charles Dickens famously described [Scrooge][/f?id=18028] in the novel.
  • Although this movie is widely regarded as the best version of Charles Dickens' story, it is the only one which omits Ebenezer [Scrooge's][/f?id=18028] famous line: "If I could work my will, every idiot who goes about with 'Merry Christmas' on his lips should be boiled in his own pudding and buried with a stake of holly through his heart." [Alastair Sim][/p?id=Alastair%20Sim] would eventually get a chance to say it, however, when he reprised his role in the animated [A Christmas Carol][/f?id=A%20Christmas%20Carol] (1971), which also featured Sir [Michael Hordern][/p?id=1650] returning as Jacob Marley.
