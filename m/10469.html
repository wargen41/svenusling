Visste du?

  • [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] considered his acting in this film to be horribly over the top and often called it his least favorite of all his movies.
  • [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] donated his entire salary, $100,000, to the U.S. War Relief Fund.
  • On stage, [Boris Karloff][/p?id=1095] played the monstrous Jonathan Brewster, [Raymond Massey's][/p?id=Raymond%20Ma] film character, who, in eerie-looking screen makeup, resembled [Karloff][/p?id=1095], which was a running gag throughout the picture. Because [Karloff][/p?id=1095] was still appearing in the Broadway play during the film's production, he was unable to do the picture.
  • [Frank Capra][/p?id=Frank%20Capra] pushed his actors to the broadest comedy takes, a fact that did not sit well with [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134]. As a result, his (and [Jack Carson's][/p?id=Jack%20Carson]) performances were singled out by reviewers for going dangerously over the top, while [Raymond Massey][/p?id=Raymond%20Ma] and the stage performers managed to look rather restrained by comparison. [Grant][/p?id=1134] hated working this way, although in his more generous moments he credited [Capra][/p?id=Frank%20Capra] with helping him to get the comic effect he was unable to do on his own (it may have been his subtle way of blaming the director).
  • [Ronald Reagan][/p?id=1840] and [Jack Benny][/p?id=Jack%20Benny] were offered the role of Mortimer Brewster, but turned it down. [Bob Hope][/p?id=Bob%20Hope] was offered the part and was eager to do it but Paramount Pictures refused to loan him out to Warner Bros. for the project.
  • [Boris Karloff][/p?id=1095] did not abandon his commitment to the Broadway version of Arsenic and Old Lace in order to appear in this film. He did, however, graciously allow Warner Bros. to use his name and likeness, an issue that the studio had considered a potentially-devastating legal obstacle had he not done so.
  • [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] later said in interviews that his role would have been better accomplished by [Allyn Joslyn][/p?id=Allyn%20Joslyn], the Broadway play's lead, or even by [James Stewart][/p?id=1425].
  • For his role as Jonathan, [Boris Karloff][/p?id=1095] got top billing in the Broadway production. For this film version, [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] got top billing for his role as Mortimer.
