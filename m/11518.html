  • The script was re-written at least 30 times until one was deemed "acceptable" by the producers and director.
  • A total of 18 different writers worked on this film.
  • An argument between [Bill Murray][/p?id=1081] and [Lucy Liu][/p?id=Lucy%20Liu] shut down filming for a day.
  • The role of Alex was originally offered to [Angelina Jolie][/p?id=1043] who turned it down after admitting she was not a fan of the original series. It was then offered to [Jada Pinkett Smith][/p?id=Jada%20Pinkett%20Smith] who declined it to film [Bamboozled][/f?id=Bamboozled] (2000) instead. [Thandie Newton][/p?id=1929] was finally cast but had to leave owing to freak weather which caused the [Mission: Impossible II][/f?id=16331] (2000) schedule to overrun. The role was eventually taken by [Lucy Liu][/p?id=Lucy%20Liu].
  • [Milla Jovovich][/p?id=Milla%20Jovovich], [Alyssa Milano][/p?id=Alyssa%20Milano] and [Julia Roberts][/p?id=1522] were among the many actresses who were forerunners for the parts of Natalie, Dylan and Alex.
  • The original cast of "Charlie's Angels" (1976) - [Jaclyn Smith][/p?id=Jaclyn%20Smith], Kate Jackson and [Farrah Fawcett][/p?id=Farrah%20Fawcett] - were invited to make cameo appearances, but declined. Reportedly, [Fawcett][/p?id=Farrah%20Fawcett] said she'd only do it if she were allowed to be the voice of Charlie, and Jackson insisted on playing the villainous role that eventually went to [Kelly Lynch][/p?id=Kelly%20Lynch]. Ultimately, [John Forsythe][/p?id=John%20Forsythe] and [Smith][/p?id=Jaclyn%20Smith] did appear in the sequel, [Charlie's Angels: Full Throttle][/f?id=11519] (2003)). [Smith's][/p?id=Jaclyn%20Smith] role was uncredited, however.
  • [Jenny McCarthy][/p?id=Jenny%20McCarthy] auditioned for the role of Natalie.
  • [Frank Coraci][/p?id=Frank%20Coraci] was once attached to direct.
  • [Drew Barrymore][/p?id=1252] was a huge "Harry Potter" fan, even going so far as to read bits of the books to the cast and crew during production. At the beginning of the film, during the clips of previous jobs, she can be seen wearing a Harry Potter disguise, complete with robes, black hair and round black glasses.
  • Although the "bad guys" use guns in the film, the Angels do not. [Drew Barrymore][/p?id=1252], who was also one of the producers, insisted that the Angels be able to do all their fighting without firearms.
