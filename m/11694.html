Visste du?

  • The concert scenes where Bad opens for Tommy Sweet were filmed at a Toby Keith concert at the Journal Pavilion in Albuquerque. Keith is thanked in the credits.
  • Both [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441] and [Colin Farrell][/p?id=Colin%20Farrell] did their own singing in this film, with the assistance of voice coach Roger Love.
  • [Ryan Bingham][/p?id=Ryan%20Bingham], who plays Tony (the musician Bad talks to at the bowling alley) is a real-life rising country-rock star. [Bingham][/p?id=Ryan%20Bingham] co-wrote "The Weary Kind" with T-Bone Burnett and [Bingham's][/p?id=Ryan%20Bingham] recording of "The Weary Kind" won a Golden Globe and an Oscar for Best Original Song. So, the real [Ryan Bingham][/p?id=Ryan%20Bingham] won an Oscar while a fictional [Ryan Bingham][/p?id=Ryan%20Bingham], played by [George Clooney][/p?id=1325] in [Up in the Air][/f?id=Up%20in%20the%20Air] (2009/I), who was nominated the same year, did not.
  • In the first scene with Bad and Buddy, Bad is teaching him how to "kick" a paper football, a popular table top game played mostly by grade school boys for many decades.
  • Bad's music and songs were loosely modeled after those of [Don Williams][/p?id=Don%20Williams] and secondarily, Leonard Cohen.
  • [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441] modeled his character after [Stephen Bruton][/p?id=Stephen%20Bruton], who was the music producer for this movie, [Kris Kristofferson][/p?id=1558] and [John Goodwin][/p?id=John%20Goodwin].
  • Filmed in only 24 days.
  • [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441] has always been musically inclined, and actually released an album titled Be Here Soon in 2000.
  • [Robert Duvall][/p?id=1810] previously appeared in [Tender Mercies][/f?id=P%C3%A5%20N%C3%A5d%20Och%20On%C3%A5d] (1983), in which he too played an aging country music singer. Both he and [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441] received Best Actor Awards for their respective roles.
  • The film is dedicated to the loving memory of [Stephen Bruton][/p?id=Stephen%20Bruton], died before of the opening of the film.
  • [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441] only agreed to play the role if his music producer friend T-Bone Burnett agreed to contribute a track for Bad to play on screen.
  • Bad Blake's drink of choice, McClure's, is fictional.
  • Director [Scott Cooper][/p?id=Scott%20Cooper] quipped to [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441] that, had his character Bad Blake been a real-life musician, he would have been the 5th member of "The Highwaymen" - alongside [Willie Nelson][/p?id=Willie%20Nelson], [Kris Kristofferson][/p?id=1558], [Waylon Jennings][/p?id=Waylon%20Jennings] and [Johnny Cash][/p?id=Johnny%20Cash].
  • In preparation for the climactic concert scene, writer-director [Scott Cooper][/p?id=Scott%20Cooper] approached country singer Toby Keith about filming a live segment of his film during Keith's half-time break in Albuquerque's Journal Pavilion. The production had 10 minutes in which to film [Jeff Bridges][/p?id=1441] and [Colin Farrell][/p?id=Colin%20Farrell] performing in front of 5 cameras and an audience of 12,000 fans. [Cooper's][/p?id=Scott%20Cooper] insistence on an element of surprise paid off when the audience went ballistic upon discovering the unexpected "star" guest spot. [Farrell's][/p?id=Colin%20Farrell] appearance on stage further fueled the mass hysteria as cell phone cameras lit up the auditorium. The whole extravaganza was up on YouTube within a matter of hours.
