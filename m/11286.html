  • Unlike the James Bond franchise, all the devices that Bourne uses are real and can be purchased by the average citizen.
  • To give this movie its gritty, documentary-style appearance, director [Paul Greengrass][/p?id=Paul%20Greengrass] used mostly handheld cameras, and a muted color palette. [Greengrass][/p?id=Paul%20Greengrass] also made sure to avoid computer graphics at all costs, and all of the stunts shown in the movie were achieved practically.
  • Producer [Frank Marshall][/p?id=Frank%20Marshall] selected [Paul Greengrass][/p?id=Paul%20Greengrass] as director after he'd seen [Greengrass's][/p?id=Paul%20Greengrass] Bloody Sunday (2002). [Marshall][/p?id=Frank%20Marshall] was after a director who wasn't intrinsically associated with the action genre, feeling that [Greengrass][/p?id=Paul%20Greengrass] would impart an original spin of his own to the script.
  • Jason Bourne never smiles in the movie.
  • The film was made with no intention of making a third movie after this one; the final scene was also meant to give the Bourne character some closure and properly end the series. When the third film was green-lit, the writers had to write the story around this epilogue.
