Visste du?

  • [Paul W.S. Anderson][/p?id=Paul%20W.S.%20Anderson] stepped down from directing [Resident Evil: Apocalypse][/f?id=17671] (2004) (although he did stay on as producer) and directing [Mortal Kombat][/f?id=16460] (2013) to write and direct this film.
  • Was rumored to be in development ever since a skull from the title characters in the Alien film series appeared in the spaceship trophy room in [Predator 2][/f?id=17793] (1990).
  • When Charles Bishop Weyland is sitting in his office on the ship, we can very briefly see him playing with his pen in a similar manner to the way the android Bishop (also played by [Lance Henriksen][/p?id=1565]) is playing with a knife in [Aliens][/f?id=10227] (1986).
  • At one stage both [Peter Weller][/p?id=1756] and [Gary Busey][/p?id=1312] was approached to do a cameo as John Yutani, the other half of the infamous "Weyland-Yutani" Company from the "Alien" films, but was written out of the script. The character was later used in the sequel, [AVPR: Aliens vs Predator - Requiem][/f?id=Aliens%20vs.%20Predator%202] (2007), this time as a female.
  • First Predator movie to feature a left-handed predator.
  • The first film in the "Alien" franchise to not feature [Sigourney Weaver][/p?id=1882], who has said in interviews the idea of the crossover "sounded awful".
  • While this film languished in so-called "development hell" for years, 20th-Century Fox considered producing a fifth film in the "Alien" franchise instead. [James Cameron][/p?id=1414], who wrote and directed [Aliens][/f?id=10227] (1986), had written a script and even approached [Sigourney Weaver][/p?id=1882] to star and [Ridley Scott][/p?id=1797] to direct, both of whom expressed interest. When the studio decided to use the Alien/Preadator crossover story instead, [Cameron][/p?id=1414], [Weaver][/p?id=1882] and [Scott][/p?id=1797] all distanced themselves from the project, and later, declared they would never work on either franchise again.
  • In an interview, director [Paul W.S. Anderson][/p?id=Paul%20W.S.%20Anderson] said that [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] offered to reprise his role as Dutch Schaeffer (from [Predator][/f?id=17792] (1987)) at the end of this movie as a cameo, but only if he lost the election for California governor.
  • [Guillermo del Toro][/p?id=Guillermo%20del%20Toro] was offered the director's chair but opted to [Hellboy][/f?id=14499] (2004) instead.
  • [Lance Henriksen][/p?id=1565] was first to be cast to maintain some kind of continuity with the previous films.
