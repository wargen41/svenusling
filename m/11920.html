  • The President of the University of Oregon only allowed this movie to be filmed on that campus because he decided he did not know how to read screenplays. In 1967 he had received the screenplay for a movie but had denied it permission to film there. That movie was [The Graduate][/f?id=Mandomsprovet] (1967) and he liked that movie so much that he decided he didn't want to miss another opportunity, so he allowed [Animal House][/f?id=11920] (1978) to be filmed on the University of Oregon campus. But he insisted that the college's name not be listed in the film's credits.
  • Before [Donald Sutherland][/p?id=1251] was brought on board, [John Belushi][/p?id=1472] was the highest-paid actor in the cast at $40,000.
  • [Donald Sutherland][/p?id=1251] was so convinced of the movie's lack of potential, that, when offered a percent of the gross or a flat fee of $75,000 for his three days' work, he took the upfront payment. Had he taken the gross percentage he would have been worth an additional $3-4 million.
  • [Chevy Chase][/p?id=1152], [Bill Murray][/p?id=1081], [Brian Doyle-Murray][/p?id=Brian%20Doyle-Murray], and [Dan Aykroyd][/p?id=1196] all turned down roles in the film due to prior commitments.
  • Director [John Landis][/p?id=1490] has stated that his original choices for the roles of Boon and Otter were [Bill Murray][/p?id=1081] and [Chevy Chase][/p?id=1152]. Neither could be signed on for the movie because of scheduling conflicts.
  • Film debut of [Kevin Bacon][/p?id=1543].
  • [John Belushi][/p?id=1472] was flying between Eugene, Oregon and New York City twice a week in order to finish the movie, and rehearse/film for "Saturday Night Live" (1975).
  • The role of D-Day was written for [Dan Aykroyd][/p?id=1196] and based on his motorcycle-loving personality.
  • [Verna Bloom][/p?id=Verna%20Bloom] mentioned that her scene with Dean Wormer where she is drunk and he is on the phone with the mayor, was completely improvised as [John Landis][/p?id=1490] was not happy with the lines the scene had.
  • [Meat Loaf][/p?id=Meat%20Loaf] was the second choice for Bluto in case [John Belushi][/p?id=1472] dropped out of his role.
  • [Karen Allen's][/p?id=Karen%20Allen] first movie.
  • More money was spent on advertising and promotion for the film than the film itself.
  • All of [John Belushi's][/p?id=1472] behavior in the cafeteria was improvised. He was not told to pile all the food on his tray and when he did director [John Landis][/p?id=1490] urged the camera operator to "stay with him." The infamous "zit scene" was also improvised. The reaction from the cast is genuine.
  • [Ivan Reitman][/p?id=Ivan%20Reitman] wanted to direct but the studio wanted a more experienced director. [Richard Lester][/p?id=Richard%20Lester] and [Bob Rafelson][/p?id=Bob%20Rafelson] were approached before [John Landis][/p?id=1490] was hired.
