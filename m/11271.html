  • The only film in the original series of five that does not star [Roddy McDowall][/p?id=Roddy%20McDowall], who was committed to another project. Archive footage of [McDowall][/p?id=Roddy%20McDowall] as Cornelius is played at the start, and [David Watson][/p?id=David%20Watson] plays the character in the film proper. Despite this, [McDowall][/p?id=Roddy%20McDowall] is often pictured on video and DVD packaging for this film.
  • [Orson Welles][/p?id=1711] was offered the role of General Ursus, which he turned down. [Burt Reynolds][/p?id=1121] was considered for the lead role of [John Brent][/p?id=John%20Brent] before [James Franciscus][/p?id=James%20Franciscus] was cast due to his resemblance to [Charlton Heston][/p?id=1151]. [Don Medford][/p?id=Don%20Medford] agreed to direct the movie, but walked out after the budget was halved.
  • An early draft of the script, called "Planet of the Men", and written by Pierre Boulle in his native French, featured a messianic Taylor 14 years after the events of the 1968 original. It involved an uprising against the apes, following which they revert back to their primal states.
  • Due to the smaller budget of this film, many of the extras cast as apes wore masks instead of the famous ape make up.
  • This was the first appearance of [Natalie Trundy][/p?id=Natalie%20Trundy] in the "Apes" series. She is the only person to play both a human and an ape in the series.
  • As of January 2008 the total domestic gross for "Beneath the Planet of the Apes" is estimated at $18,999,718. Because of its modest budget of just $3 million, percentage-wise "Beneath," despite wide agreement that it's one of the worst of the series, has remained the most profitable of all the "Apes" films, including the 2001 remake.
