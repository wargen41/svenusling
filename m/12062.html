Visste du?

  • [Edward Dmytryk][/p?id=Edward%20Dmytryk], the film's editor, said that [Charles Laughton][/p?id=1147] became so emotional during the scene in the saloon where he recites the [Gettysburg][/f?id=14246] Address that it took director [Leo McCarey][/p?id=1579] 1-1/2 days to complete shooting it. According to [Dmytryk][/p?id=Edward%20Dmytryk], the preview audiences found [Laughton's][/p?id=1147] close-ups in the scene embarrassing and tittered through the speech. When substitute shots of [Laughton][/p?id=1147] from behind were inserted, the audience found the reaction shots of the other people reacting to him very moving, and the second preview was extremely successful.
  • [Charles Laughton][/p?id=1147] referred to his reading of the "[Gettysburg][/f?id=14246] Address" in the film as "one of the most moving things that ever happened to me" [Laughton][/p?id=1147] recited the address to the cast and crew of [Mutiny on the Bounty][/f?id=Myteri] (1935) on the last day of shooting on Catalina Island and again on the set of [The Hunchback of Notre Dame][/f?id=17706].
  • The [Gettysburg][/f?id=14246] address had great personal significance to [Charles Laughton][/p?id=1147] as he was considering taking up American citizenship.
  • Nazi Germany banned the release of any German-dubbed version of this film because of the [Gettysburg][/f?id=14246] Address speech.
  • [Charles Laughton][/p?id=1147] personally chose [Leo McCarey][/p?id=1579] to direct the film. He wanted his first comedic role to be in the hands of a master of comedy and, seeing as [McCarey][/p?id=1579] had directed the Marx Brothers success, [Duck Soup][/f?id=14126] (1933), he was eminently qualified.
  • [Leila Hyams][/p?id=Leila%20Hyams] retired from the film business the following year at the age of 31. She still remained active in Hollywood circles thanks to her marriage to leading agent, Phil Berg.
  • While rehearsing for this movie, [Charles Laughton][/p?id=1147] was hospitalized for several weeks for a rectal abscess.
  • [Charles Laughton's][/p?id=1147] favorite film in which he performed.
