  • The original working title of the film was "Birds of a Feather" and the first drafts of the script still bear this title.
  • Filmmaker and PBS producer Rick McKay was hired by director [Mike Nichols][/p?id=Mike%20Nichols], months before filming of "Birdcage" began, to go to Paris, London, San Francisco and Atlanta to make a feature length documentary about drag queens. McKay did thorough research to find drag queens all over the world to interview and to film in performance. This finished documentary was used to train [Nathan Lane][/p?id=1689] and [Robin Williams][/p?id=1828].
  • Agador Spartacus is based upon [Hank Azaria's][/p?id=1353] grandmother.
  • [Hank Azaria][/p?id=1353] created two different voices for the character of Agador Spartacus, one being somewhat of a more masculine voice and the other one being higher pitched. He was worried about the second one being too stereotypical until he asked a gay friend of his, who thought it was more realistic.
  • [Robin Williams][/p?id=1828] was originally cast as Albert, but he wanted a change from flamboyant characters, and asked to be cast as Armand. [Mike Nichols][/p?id=Mike%20Nichols] agreed, and re-cast him.
  • [Mike Nichols][/p?id=Mike%20Nichols] required that [Nathan Lane][/p?id=1689] and [Robin Williams][/p?id=1828] filmed at least one good take of each scene sticking to the script before he would allow them to improvise (something both of the two actors are known for).
  • Director [Mike Nichols][/p?id=Mike%20Nichols] had to be covered by a sound blanket during the toast scene because he was laughing too loud.
  • In the movie, [Calista Flockhart's][/p?id=Calista%20Flockhart] character Barbara is "not even 18". [Flockhart][/p?id=Calista%20Flockhart] was actually 31 at the time of the filming. [Dan Futterman's][/p?id=Dan%20Futterman] character Val is supposed to be 20, but in reality, he was actually 27 when the film was being shot.
