  • Filming continued until the week before this movie debuted in theatres.
  • [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] thought the script was one of the best he'd ever read. He especially liked all of the elements of comedy, action, drama, and satire in it.
  • [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] wanted this movie to be PG-13, so it would appeal to a broader audience. Being executive producer (for the first time) he approved script, director, cast, studio financing, distribution, marketing, budget, PR firm, planning a foreign release, et cetera. [Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] enjoyed the added responsibility, because he could be involved in every facet of production.
  • [Charles Dance][/p?id=Charles%20Dance] said in interviews that, after being told that he had won a part turned down by [Alan Rickman][/p?id=1012] because of the salary, he wore a t-shirt on set which read; "I'm cheaper than [Alan Rickman][/p?id=1012]!'
  • [Sharon Stone's][/p?id=1874] casting in a brief cameo as her [Basic Instinct][/f?id=10688] (1992) character was somewhat surprising because she and [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] had clashed on the set of [Total Recall (1990)][/f?id=20707]. She was reluctant to do the cameo but did it as a favour to [Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062], who reminded her that [Total Recall (1990)][/f?id=20707] had helped her career.
  • Just after Frank's house explodes, the black cop says, "Two days to retirement" as a nod to Roger Murtaugh ([Danny Glover][/p?id=1208]) from the Lethal Weapon film franchise. At that point, a soundtrack excerpt from the Lethal Weapon theme plays in the background.
  • [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] considered this movie his first real failure after an unbroken string of successes. He also considered it the beginning of the end of his movie career.
  • [John McTiernan][/p?id=John%20McTiernan] said this movie began as a wonderful Cinderella story, with a nine-year-old boy and a pretty good script. But the movie didn't end as it began, referring to the movie's troubled production, and the fact that it opened in theatres a week after [Jurassic Park][/f?id=15341] (1993). After its failure, [McTiernan][/p?id=John%20McTiernan] retreated to his house in Wyoming and took a break from his career.
  • The movie ran so far behind schedule that they had just one test screening, which ran for two hours and twenty minutes with a lot of inaudible dialogue, boring the audience. The studio refused to delay the opening, so as not to send the message the movie was in trouble, a decision [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] agreed with.
  • The first time [Arnold Schwarzenegger][/p?id=1062] took a producing credit on a movie.
  • Sir [Ian McKellen][/p?id=1387] played Death, a character out of [Ingmar Bergman's][/p?id=1391] [The Seventh Seal][/f?id=12058] (1957). In that movie, Death was played by [Bengt Ekerot][/p?id=Bengt%20Ekerot].
  • [Tori Spelling][/p?id=Tori%20Spelling] had a cameo as herself but it was eventually left on the cutting room floor.
  • [Robert Zemeckis][/p?id=Robert%20Zemeckis] was in contention to direct at one point.
