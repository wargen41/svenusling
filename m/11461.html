Visste du?

  • [Colin Farrell][/p?id=Colin%20Farrell] said in an interview that he "think[s] [he] did as many takes for this whole film as I did for one scene in [Miami Vice][/f?id=16155]."
  • This is the first [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] film to be released with a stereo soundtrack.
  • Unusually for a [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] film, the music is provided by a composer - in this case, [Philip Glass][/p?id=Philip%20Glass] - instead of being a collection of old standards.
  • The third consecutive [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] film to be made in the UK (after [Match Point][/f?id=16075] and Scoop), and the first one not to have any Americans in the cast.
  • Originally this was scheduled to open in late 2007 to qualify for Academy Award consideration. After some indifferent previews however, it was given a January 2008 release, traditionally the dumping ground for bad films.
  • When [Woody Allen][/p?id=2010] first approached [Philip Glass][/p?id=Philip%20Glass] about doing the film's score, they had an initial meeting in which [Glass][/p?id=Philip%20Glass] played him a particularly ominous piece of music. [Allen][/p?id=2010] remarked that it was a very heavy section of music and that it would be perfect for establishing the dark mood of the film. [Glass][/p?id=Philip%20Glass] interjected with the fact that the music he'd just played was actually the love theme and he hadn't gotten round to writing anything ominous yet!
  • [Sally Hawkins][/p?id=Sally%20Hawkins] dyed her hair blonde for the film.
