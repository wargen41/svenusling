  • When the scene where Grover puts the shoe polish on George's face to make him appear to be black was first filmed, a white man walked in and believed George was black. [Richard Pryor][/p?id=1790] was uncomfortable with the scene and felt it would be funnier if a black man walked in and is not fooled at all. [Pryor][/p?id=1790] asked [Arthur Hiller][/p?id=Arthur%20Hiller] for a re-shoot but [Hiller][/p?id=Arthur%20Hiller] refused. [Pryor][/p?id=1790] walked off the set and refused to return to filming until the scene was changed. [Hiller][/p?id=Arthur%20Hiller] relented and [Pryor's][/p?id=1790] idea was used for the final cut.
  • [Robert Vaughn][/p?id=1826] received the script in the mail, and loved it. He wanted to play Roger Devereau, but was dismayed to discover that [Patrick McGoohan][/p?id=Patrick%20McGoohan] had already accepted the part. He contacted [Arthur Hiller][/p?id=Arthur%20Hiller] and discovered that it was sent by mistake. He was invited to watch the production, and became friends with star [Gene Wilder][/p?id=1321].
  • When meeting [Gene Wilder][/p?id=1321] after having seen Silver Streak (1976), [Cary Grant][/p?id=1134] asked him if the script had been in anyway inspired by [North by Northwest][/f?id=15005] (1959). As [Wilder][/p?id=1321] admitted it was correct, [Grant][/p?id=1134] then added, "I knew it! Have you noticed that each time you take ordinary people, say, like you and me, then take them in a situation way above their heads, it makes a great thriller?"
  • Although you do not see his face, the baggage valet at the very beginning of the movie is '[Morgan Freeman'][/p?id=1682] (recognizable by his distinct voice).
  • [Patrick McGoohan][/p?id=Patrick%20McGoohan] was only cast after the first choice died.
