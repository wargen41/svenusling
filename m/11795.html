  • In the opening scene where two doctors are examining [John Dunbar][/p?id=John%20Dunbar], the man on the table is [Kevin Costner's][/p?id=1544] stand-in. The two people playing the doctors are actually the film's producer, [Jim Wilson][/p?id=Jim%20Wilson] on the left and director/star [Costner][/p?id=1544] on the right. The voices were dubbed by other actors.
  • [Graham Greene][/p?id=Graham%20Greene], who plays Kicking Bird, also plays Edgar Montrose in "The Red Green Show" (1991). In one episode of that series, Edgar mentions "Dances With Wolves", and says the "native guy" (Kicking Bird) should have gotten the Oscar.
  • Many of the native Indians were visibly moved by the scene where the Sioux encounter a field full of dead, skinned buffalo.
  • Only one take a day could be made of the buffalo stampede as the animals would often run a distance of 10 miles. It would take the wranglers all day to round them up again.
  • [Viggo Mortensen][/p?id=1977] was originally cast to play [John Dunbar][/p?id=John%20Dunbar]. [Mortensen][/p?id=1977] will, however, be playing [John Dunbar][/p?id=John%20Dunbar] in the upcoming sequel to this movie, The Holy Road (2011), as [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544] has stated that he will not be reprising his character.
  • Because of budgetary overruns and general industry reluctance to invest in a Western, [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544] was forced to dig deep into his own pockets to make up the film's $18 million budget. As it then went on to gross over $100 million, he himself earned an estimated $40 million from his original investment.
  • To add realism to the movie, a language coach was brought in to teach Lakota to cast members who did not know how to speak it. Because of the difficulty in learning the language, the "gendered speech" aspects of the language were omitted from the lessons. When native speakers of Lakota saw the finished film, they found it amusing to hear Lakota warriors talking like women.
  • The highest grossing Western of all time, with a domestic take of $184 million. It achieved this figure without ever reaching #1 on the box-office charts.
  • Michael Blake wrote a spec screenplay in the early 1980s. When [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544] came across the project in 1986, he suggested to Blake that he should turn it into a novel, thereby increasing his chances of getting it made into a film. Blake did so and, after many rejections, found a publisher in 1988. [Costner][/p?id=1544] immediately snapped up the movie rights with an eye to directing it himself.
  • [Pope ][/p?id=Pope%20]John Paul[ II][/p?id=%20II] once mentioned that [John Barry's][/p?id=John%20Barry] score was one of his favorite pieces of music.
  • [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544] originally considered casting [Marlon Brando][/p?id=1616] in the role later played by [Maury Chaykin][/p?id=1631].
  • The studio wanted the final cut to be 2 hours 20 minutes. They had to settle for [Kevin Costner's][/p?id=1544] cut of 3 hours.
  • [Kevin Costner's][/p?id=1544] spreading out of his arms while doing his suicide run at the start of the film was a completely spontaneous gesture that took his stunt coordinator by surprise.
  • [John Barry][/p?id=John%20Barry] agreed to score the film immediately after reading the script.
  • Director [Kevin Reynolds][/p?id=Kevin%20Reynolds] received "special thanks" in the credits due to his helping [Kevin Costner][/p?id=1544] direct the famous buffalo hunt scene.
  • [Tom Berenger][/p?id=1948] was considered as possibility for the role of [Dunbar][/p?id=John%20Dunbar] by the producers before [Costner][/p?id=1544] came on board.
