  • The only film for which [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] ever won an Oscar.
  • Contrary to popular belief, [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] did not jump over the fence himself at the end of the movie. In fact, according to biographer Garry Wills in his book "[John Wayne's][/p?id=1504] America", [Wayne][/p?id=1504] was not healthy enough to do such stunts. It should be remembered that [Wayne][/p?id=1504] had an entire lung removed four years prior to making the film and actually had trouble walking more than thirty feet without breathing heavily.
  • [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] had initially promised the role of Mattie Ross to his daughter [Aissa Wayne][/p?id=Aissa%20Wayne], but director [Henry Hathaway][/p?id=Henry%20Hathaway] refused to cast her.
  • [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] reportedly chased costar [Dennis][/f?id=12020] Hopper around Paramount with a loaded gun. Hopper had to hide in [Glen Campbell's][/p?id=Glen%20Campbell] dressing room until [Wayne][/p?id=1504] cooled down.
  • [Mia Farrow][/p?id=Mia%20Farrow], among other well-known actresses, was approached to play Mattie, but she turned it down. [Robert Mitchum][/p?id=1815] had told her that [Henry Hathaway][/p?id=Henry%20Hathaway] was impossible to work with. She later said it was one of the biggest professional mistakes of her career.
  • [Elvis Presley][/p?id=Elvis%20Presley] was considered for the role of La Boeuf, the Texas Ranger. However, his manager "Colonel" [Tom Parker][/p?id=Tom%20Parker] insisted that [Presley][/p?id=Elvis%20Presley] should receive top billing, so the part was given to [Glen Campbell][/p?id=Glen%20Campbell] instead.
  • This is the (uncredited) film debut of [Wilford Brimley][/p?id=Wilford%20Brimley].
  • After judging The Carpenters on a talent show, [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] had Karen Carpenter read for the part of Mattie. The producers went with [Kim Darby][/p?id=Kim%20Darby], who had acting experience; however, [Wayne][/p?id=1504] did not like working with her, because he felt she was unprofessional on the set.
  • The scene near the end where Rooster Cogburn ([John Wayne][/p?id=1504]) and Ned Pepper's gang meet in a field and Pepper ([Robert Duvall][/p?id=1810]) is shot was filmed in a clearing near the top of Owl Creek Pass outside Ridgway, CO. The field is off the road to the left and is very easy to find.
  • [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] was disappointed by the casting of [Kim Darby][/p?id=Kim%20Darby] as Mattie Ross, and the two hardly spoke at all off camera. He later said, "Christ, talk about having no chemistry with your leading lady! She was the goddamn lousiest actress I ever worked with."
  • [Henry Hathaway][/p?id=Henry%20Hathaway] later said he hated [Glen Campbell's][/p?id=Glen%20Campbell] performance, which he described as wooden, and claimed the singer was only cast so he could have a hit with the theme song which would help promote the film.
  • [John Wayne's][/p?id=1504] Best Actor Oscar win was widely seen as a sentimental choice, more in recognition of his forty year career. His performance in this movie was dismissed by many critics as over-the-top and hammy.
  • [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] did not get along with [Robert Duvall][/p?id=1810] during filming, and at one point threatened to punch the young Method actor if he argued with the director again.
  • The character of Rooster Cogburn was supposed to be around forty. [John Wayne][/p?id=1504] was 61 when the film was made.
