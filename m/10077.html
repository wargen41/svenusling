  • In her early monologue scene with [Katharine Hepburn][/p?id=1531], [Judy Holliday][/p?id=Judy%20Holliday] can be seen trembling. This was not acting, but nervousness. The inexperienced [Judy Holliday][/p?id=Judy%20Holliday] was terrified of performing with [Katharine Hepburn][/p?id=1531].
  • The movie's line "Licorice, mmmm. If there's anything I'm a sucker for, it's licorice." was voted as the #60 of "The 100 Greatest Movie Lines" by Premiere in 2007.
  • Ranked #7 on the American Film Institute's list of the 10 greatest films in the genre "Romantic Comedy" in June 2008.
  • [Katharine Hepburn][/p?id=1531] reportedly urged director [George Cukor][/p?id=George%20Cukor] to focus the camera on [Judy Holliday][/p?id=Judy%20Holliday] during a number of their shared scenes, not only because she was a fan of the new-to-movies [Holliday][/p?id=Judy%20Holliday] but because it was hoped the studios would see how terrific [Holliday][/p?id=Judy%20Holliday] was and cast her as the lead in [Born Yesterday][/f?id=F%C3%B6dd%20Ig%C3%A5r] (1950), the role she'd created on Broadway. It worked.
