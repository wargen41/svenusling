Visste du?

  • [Spencer Tracy][/p?id=1888] wanted [Katharine Hepburn][/p?id=1531] for his screen wife, but it was felt that they were too romantic a team to play a happily domesticated couple with children, so [Joan Bennett][/p?id=Joan%20Bennett] got the part.
  • In this movie, one of the gifts Kay (Dame [Elizabeth Taylor][/p?id=1275]) gets as a present is a Venus de Milo statue with a clock in the stomach, which Stanley T. Banks ([Spencer Tracy][/p?id=1888]) refers to as a "stinker". This same gift makes its way into the re-make ([Father of the Bride][/f?id=11335] (1991)) amongst the presents, and is still not received well.
  • The June 10 date of Kay and Buckley's wedding was a nod by director [Vincente][/f?id=21951] Minnelli to his wife, [Judy Garland][/p?id=Judy%20Garland] - June 10th was her birthday. It also happens to be the date [Spencer Tracy][/p?id=1888] died in 1967.
  • [Jack Benny][/p?id=Jack%20Benny] badly wanted the lead role, but was deemed unsuitable.
  • Dame [Elizabeth Taylor][/p?id=1275] and [Joan Bennett][/p?id=Joan%20Bennett] played the role of Amy [March][/p?id=1307] in big-screen adaptations of "Little Women". [Bennett][/p?id=Joan%20Bennett] in [Little Women][/f?id=Unga%20Kvinnor%20(1933)] (1933), and [Taylor][/p?id=1275] in [Little Women][/f?id=Unga%20Kvinnor%20(1949)] (1949).
  • [Fredric March][/p?id=1307], [Charles Laughton][/p?id=1147], and [Walter Pidgeon][/p?id=1988] were in the running for the title role.
