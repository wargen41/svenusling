Visste du?

  • [Douglas Trumbull][/p?id=Douglas%20Trumbull] says that he learned how to be a director while working on this film, as he had no training or experience in the job.
  • To keep costs down, [Trumbull][/p?id=Douglas%20Trumbull] hired college students for modelmaking and other such special effects work. One of them, John Dykstra, went on to a distinguished special effects career of his own.
  • The three drone robots Huey, Dewey, and Louie (named after Donald Duck's nephews) were operated by four multiple-amputee actors: [Mark Persons][/p?id=Mark%20Persons], Steve Brown, [Cheryl Sparks][/p?id=Cheryl%20Sparks], and [Larry Whisenhunt][/p?id=Larry%20Whisenhunt].
  • Several shots of the Valley Forge and its sister ships were later re-used in the television series "Battlestar Galactica," as agricultural ships in the refugee fleet.
  • The "Saturn sequence" was originally intended to be featured in [Stanley Kubrick's][/p?id=1891] [2001 - A Space Odyssey][/f?id=%C3%85r%202001%20-%20Ett%20Rymd%C3%A4ventyr] (1968), but the technology needed for the visual effects team to do such a sequence was not ready for use.
  • After the success of [Easy Rider][/f?id=13302] (1969), Universal Studios hit upon the idea to let young filmmakers make "semi-independent" films for low budgets in hopes of generating similar profits. The idea was to make five movies for low budgets (one million dollars or less), not interfere in the filmmaking process, and give the directors final cut. The movies were: this movie, [The Hired Hand][/f?id=12049] (1971), [The Last Movie][/f?id=The%20Last%20Movie] (1971), [Taking Off][/f?id=Taking%20Off] (1971) and [American Graffiti][/f?id=18464] (1973).
  • In the skinny-dipping scene, the water [Bruce Dern][/p?id=1114] is swimming in was actually ice cold. The production saved money by not providing a water heater.
  • This film is considered by many scholars to be the first environmentalist film.
