  • Since [Chungking Express][/f?id=11542] was filmed in sequence or "like a road movie" as [Wong Kar-Wai][/p?id=Kar-Wai%20Wong] has said, [Wong][/p?id=Kar-Wai%20Wong] wrote each scene either the night before or in the morning of the day of filming.
  • [Quentin Tarantino][/p?id=1763] signed a deal with Miramax specially to start his own releasing company so that he could get Chung Hing sam lam (1994) out to a wider public.
  • Veteran actress [Brigitte Lin][/p?id=Brigitte%20Lin] came out of retirement to have a starring role in the film as the drug smuggler in a blonde wig. She was very eager to work with [Kar-Wai Wong][/p?id=Kar-Wai%20Wong].
  • [Kaneshiro][/p?id=Takeshi%20Kaneshiro] Takeshi spoke four languages himself in this film. His narrations were all in Mandarin, most of his live lines are in heavily-accented Cantonese, he spoke Japanese when he called one of his ex-girlfriends, and had one line of English when he apprehended a suspect (he said "hands up!" to him). He used all 4 when he approached Bridgett [Lin's][/p?id=Brigitte%20Lin] character.
  • [Faye Wong][/p?id=Faye%20Wong] is better known in Hong Kong as a pop singer. Indeed, that is her singing on the cover version of The Cranberries song "Dreams" that we hear during the film.
  • A third segment was written but [Kar-Wai Wong][/p?id=Kar-Wai%20Wong] felt that the film was complete without it. The third story formed the basis of [Do lok tin si][/f?id=Helgon%20I%20Neon] (1995).
  • This film was shot, edited and released while [Kar-Wai Wong][/p?id=Kar-Wai%20Wong] was on hiatus from the shooting of his epic [Dung che sai duk][/f?id=Ashes%20of%20Time] (1994).
  • [Wong Kar-Wai][/p?id=Kar-Wai%20Wong] modeled [Brigitte Lin's][/p?id=Brigitte%20Lin] character after [Gena Rowlands][/p?id=Gena%20Rowlands] in [John Cassavetes's][/p?id=John%20Cassavetes] Gloria (1980), and actress [Greta Garbo][/p?id=Greta%20Garbo].
  • Director [Barry Jenkins][/p?id=Barry%20Jenkins] has described this film to be formative in his artistic development and inspirational for his own films.
  • [Takeshi Kaneshiro][/p?id=Takeshi%20Kaneshiro], whose character turns 25 in the film, was barely twenty when the film was shot.
  • Shu Kei, film critic and professor at the Academy of Performing Arts in Hong Kong, said actress [Brigitte Lin][/p?id=Brigitte%20Lin] was filming this and a period piece movie at the same time, often on the same day, and she didn't have time to go through makeup and costume twice. So the director had her character wear a wig, sunglasses and a big raincoat to cover the makeup and costume from the other film.
  • The filmmakers were sued by the people living below the apartment that was flooded for one scene. The apartment was on the third floor and both levels below it received water damage.
  • Originally, [Brigitte Lin's][/p?id=Brigitte%20Lin] character was meant to be a retired actress and Cop 223 was assigned the task of convincing her to meet with some kidnappers who had asked for her. Several scenes were shot before this storyline was abandoned, some of which was given a new context and included in the final film.
  • The two stories were shot by different cinematographers. Andrew Lau, who shot [Wong Kar-Wai's][/p?id=Kar-Wai%20Wong] first film [As Tears Go By][/f?id=As%20Tears%20Go%20By], shot the first story; [Christopher Doyle][/p?id=Christopher%20Doyle], who shot [Wong Kar-Wai's][/p?id=Kar-Wai%20Wong] second film Days of Being Wild and several others afterwards, shot the second story.
