Visste du?

  • During filming of the scene in which the Cable Guy ([Jim Carrey][/p?id=1460]) plays basketball, it was discovered that [Carrey][/p?id=1460] could barely dribble a basketball, much less make a basket. Director [Ben Stiller][/p?id=1075] had [Carrey][/p?id=1460] mime the action without a ball and visual effects technicians added the basketball in postproduction.
  • The part of the Cable Guy was originally written for [Chris Farley][/p?id=1159], but scheduling conflicts forced him to decline the role.
  • The scene at Medieval Times where Chip Douglas ([Jim Carrey][/p?id=1460]) asks for Steven's ([Matthew Broderick][/p?id=1626]) chicken skin and then performs his The Silence of the Lambs (1991) impression was all improvised. During one take, [Carrey][/p?id=1460] asked for the chicken skin out of nowhere. After doing the "Silence of the Lambs" bit, director [Ben Stiller][/p?id=1075] loved it and wanted to keep it in the film even though it wasn't in the script. If you look at [Matthew Broderick's][/p?id=1626] face during this scene, you can tell he is cracking up. His reaction is a genuine laugh.
  • [Carrey][/p?id=1460] was paid 20 million dollars for his work in this film - a new record.
